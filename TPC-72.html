Ch. 72: Racketeering and Unlawful Debt Collection

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 72.01)

Pattern of racketeering

Engaging in at least 2 incidents of racketeering conduct with similar intents, results, etc. The last incident must occur not later than the 5th anniversary of a previous incident.

Pecuniary value

(A) Anything of value (money, negotiable instrument, etc.) with economic advantage; OR (B) Any other property or service with a value in excess of $100.

Racketeering

To commit, attempt to commit, conspire to commit, or solicit, coerce, or intimidate another to commit listed offenses (e.g., felony securities act, kidnapping, agg kidnapping, operation of stash house, felony perjury, felony resisting, felony hindering, felony public indecency, or engaging in organized crime).

Unlawful debt

Money/value from a debt that is unenforceable because it was contracted in violation of laws (e.g., Racing Act, Gambling), OR a usurious rate that is at least twice the enforceable rate.

Enterprise

Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 140B.001, Civil Practice and Remedies Code.

Money

Means funds as defined by Sec. 34.01.

Real property

Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 140B.001, Civil Practice and Remedies Code.

Sec. 72.02. Use of Proceeds Derived from Racketeering...

  • Offense (a): A person intentionally uses or invests any proceeds knowingly derived from a pattern of racketeering or unlawful debt collection... in acquiring title to real property or in the establishment or operation of any enterprise.
  • Penalty (b): Felony of the Second Degree.
  • Prosecution (c): May be prosecuted under this section, other laws, or both.

Sec. 72.03. Acquisition of Interest... Through Racketeering...

  • Offense (a): A person, knowingly through a pattern of racketeering or unlawful debt, acquires or maintains... any interest in or control of any enterprise or real property.
  • Penalty (b): Felony of the Second Degree.
  • Prosecution (c): May be prosecuted under this section, other laws, or both.

Sec. 72.04. Participation in Enterprise Through Racketeering...

  • Offense (a): A person is employed by or associated with an enterprise and knowingly conducts or participates... in that enterprise through a pattern of racketeering or collection of unlawful debt.
  • Penalty (b): Felony of the Second Degree.
  • Prosecution (c): May be prosecuted under this section, other laws, or both.

Sec. 72.05. Alternative Fine

A court may impose an alternative fine on a person convicted under 72.02, 72.03, or 72.04. This fine can be up to:

  • The greater of:
    • Three times the gross value gained; OR
    • Three times the gross loss caused.
  • PLUS the amount of court costs and reasonable costs of investigation and prosecution.
