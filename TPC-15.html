Ch. 15: Preparatory Offenses

Back to Main Page

Sec. 15.01. Criminal Attempt

Elements (15.01(a)):

A person commits attempt if, with specific intent to commit an offense, he does an act:

  • Amounting to more than mere preparation;
  • That tends but fails to effect the commission of the offense intended.

Key Provisions:

  • (b) Aggravated Offense: If the offense attempted may be aggravated, the conduct is an attempt to commit the aggravated offense if the aggravating element accompanies the attempt.
  • (c) No Defense: It is no defense to prosecution for attempt that the offense was actually committed.
  • (d) Punishment: The offense is one category lower than the offense attempted.
    • Exception: If the offense attempted is a State Jail Felony, the attempt is a Class A Misdemeanor.
  • (e) Capital Murder of PO Exception: An attempt to commit Capital Murder of a peace officer (Sec. 19.03(a)(1)) is a First Degree Felony with a minimum term of 25 years (punishable by 25-99 or life).

Sec. 15.02. Criminal Conspiracy

Elements (15.02(a)):

A person commits conspiracy if, with intent that a felony be committed:

  1. He agrees with one or more persons that they or one or more of them engage in the conduct; AND
  2. He or one or more of them performs an overt act in pursuance of the agreement.

Key Provisions:

  • (b) Agreement: An agreement may be inferred from the acts of the parties.
  • (c) No Defense: It is no defense that:
    • One or more co-conspirators is not criminally responsible.
    • One or more co-conspirators has been acquitted (so long as 2 or more have not been).
    • One or more co-conspirators has not been prosecuted or is immune.
    • The actor is legally incapable of committing the object offense.
    • The object offense was actually committed.
  • (d) Punishment: The offense is one category lower than the most serious felony that is the object of the conspiracy. (Does not apply to Sec. 76.02).
    • Exception: If the most serious felony object is a State Jail Felony, the conspiracy is a Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 15.03. Criminal Solicitation

Elements (15.03(a)):

A person commits solicitation if, with intent that a Capital Felony or Felony of the 1st Degree be committed, he requests, commands, or attempts to induce another to engage in the specific conduct.

Key Provisions:

  • (b) Corroboration Required: A person cannot be convicted on the uncorroborated testimony of the person solicited. The testimony must be strongly corroborative of both the solicitation and the actor's intent.
  • (c) No Defense: It is no defense that the person solicited is not criminally responsible, has been acquitted, is immune, or that the offense was actually committed.
  • (d) Punishment:
    • Solicitation of a Capital Offense is a First Degree Felony.
    • Solicitation of a First Degree Felony is a Second Degree Felony.

Sec. 15.031. Criminal Solicitation of a Minor

A person commits this offense if they solicit a minor (younger than 17), or someone they believe is a minor, to commit specific offenses (e.g., offenses listed in Art. 42A.054 CCP, or various sexual offenses).

  • (c) Corroboration Required: Same as 15.03(b), requires strong corroboration of the minor's testimony.
  • (d) No Defense: Same defenses as 15.03(c) do not apply.
  • (e) Punishment: The offense is one category lower than the solicited offense.
    • Gang Enhancement: The offense is the SAME category as the solicited offense if the actor (17+) is a gang member and commits the offense to further gang activities.
  • (f) Definition: "Minor" means an individual younger than 17 years of age.

Sec. 15.032. Child Grooming

A person commits this offense if, with intent that a sexual offense be committed, they knowingly persuade, induce, entice, or coerce a child younger than 18 years of age to engage in the conduct.

  • (b) Punishment: The offense is a Third Degree Felony.
    • Enhancement: Becomes a Second Degree Felony if the actor has a previous conviction for certain serious sexual offenses.
  • (c) Affirmative Defense: It is an affirmative defense if the actor is < 18 AND the victim is < 18 AND they are (1) close in age (<= 3 years) and dating, OR (2) spouses, AND the conduct was only between them.
  • (d) Prosecution Bar: An actor may be prosecuted for this OR another offense (e.g., attempt), but not both.

Sec. 15.04. Renunciation Defense

This section provides an AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE to prosecution for preparatory offenses.

Requirements for the Defense:

  • The actor must show a voluntary and complete renunciation of his criminal objective.
  • For Attempt (a): The actor must have avoided commission by abandoning his conduct OR (if abandonment wasn't enough) by taking further affirmative action that prevented it.
  • For Conspiracy/Solicitation (b): The actor must have countermanded (solicitation) or withdrawn (conspiracy) *before* the object offense was committed AND took further affirmative action that prevented its commission.

When Renunciation is NOT Voluntary (c):

  • It is NOT voluntary if motivated by:
    1. Circumstances that increase the probability of detection or apprehension; OR
    2. A decision to postpone the act or transfer it to a similar victim.

Renunciation as Mitigation (d):

  • If a defendant is found guilty but shows they renounced and made a substantial effort to prevent the offense, the evidence is admissible as mitigation at punishment.
  • If renunciation is found at punishment, the punishment shall be one grade lower.

Sec. 15.05. No Offense

Attempt, conspiracy, or solicitation of a preparatory offense defined in this chapter is not an offense.

This means you cannot be charged with "Attempt to Commit Criminal Attempt" or "Conspiracy to Commit Criminal Solicitation."
