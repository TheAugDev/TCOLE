Ch. 48: Conduct Affecting Public Health

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Ch. 48)

E-cigarette (48.01)

Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 161.081, Health and Safety Code.

Human organ (48.02)

Human kidney, liver, heart, lung, pancreas, eye, bone, skin, or other organ/tissue. *Does not* include hair, blood, blood components, or human fetal tissue.

Human fetal tissue (48.03)

Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 173.001, Health and Safety Code.

Adult stem cell (48.04)

An undifferentiated cell found in differentiated tissue, able to renew itself and differentiate into specialized cell types of that tissue.

Investigational stem cell treatment (48.04)

An adult stem cell treatment under investigation in a clinical trial and not yet approved for general use by the FDA.

Camp (48.05)

To reside temporarily in a place, with shelter.

Shelter (48.05)

A tent, tarpaulin, lean-to, sleeping bag, bedroll, blankets, or any temporary/permanent shelter. *Does not* include clothing or handheld devices.

Sec. 48.01. Smoking Tobacco

  • Offense (a-1): A person commits an offense if they possess a burning tobacco product, smoke tobacco, or operate an e-cigarette in:
    • Facility of a public primary or secondary school
    • Elevator, enclosed theater/movie house, library, museum, hospital
    • Transit system bus, intrastate bus, plane, or train (which is a public place)
  • Defense (b): No "Smoking Prohibited" notice (stating state law, fine not to exceed $500) is prominently displayed.
  • Defense (c): The conveyance or public place is not equipped with facilities for extinguishment of smoking materials.
  • Exception (d): Actor is exclusively within a designated smoking/vaping area OR is a participant in an authorized theatrical performance.
  • Note (e): On transit (bus/plane/train), a designated area *must* also include the operator's area.
  • Penalty (f): Class C Misdemeanor.

Sec. 48.015. Prohibitions Relating to Certain Cigarettes

  • Offense (a): A person may not acquire, hold, possess, transport, or import *for sale or distribution* in this state:
    1. Cigarettes that do not comply with all applicable federal laws; OR
    2. Cigarettes to which stamps may not be affixed (per Sec. 154.0415, Tax Code).
  • Exceptions (a)(2): Does not apply to cigarettes lawfully imported for *personal use* or as *duty-free* merchandise.
  • Mental State (b): Actor commits the offense *knowing or having reason to know* they are doing so.
  • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 48.02. Prohibition of the Purchase and Sale of Human Organs

  • Offense (b): A person commits an offense if they *knowingly or intentionally* offer to buy, sell, acquire, receive, or transfer any human organ for *valuable consideration*.
  • Exceptions (c): "Valuable consideration" does *not* include:
    1. Fees paid to medical personnel/hospitals for services.
    2. Reimbursement of legal or medical expenses for the *receiver*.
    3. Reimbursement of travel, housing, and lost wages for the *donor*.
  • Penalty (d): State Jail Felony.

Sec. 48.03. Prohibition on Purchase and Sale of Human Fetal Tissue

  • Offense (b): A person commits an offense if they *knowingly* offer to buy, sell, acquire, receive, or transfer any human fetal tissue for *economic benefit*.
  • Penalty (c): State Jail Felony.
  • Defense (d): Actor is an employee/contractor of an *accredited institution of higher education* AND acquires/receives/transfers the tissue *solely* for an authorized donation (per H&S Code 173.005).
  • Exceptions (e): Does *not* apply to tissue:
    1. Solely for diagnostic or pathological testing.
    2. Solely for a criminal investigation.
    3. Solely for disposal.
    4. Acquired during pregnancy/delivery for approved research.
    5. Cell lines existing before Sept 1, 2017, used for approved research.
  • Jurisdiction (f): The Attorney General has concurrent jurisdiction with a local prosecutor's consent.

Sec. 48.04. Prohibition on Purchase and Sale of Adult Stem Cells...

  • Offense (b): A person commits an offense if they *knowingly* offer to buy, sell, acquire, receive, or transfer any adult stem cells for *valuable consideration* for use in an *investigational stem cell treatment*.
  • Exceptions (c): Same as 48.02 (medical fees, legal/medical expenses, donor travel/housing/lost wages).
  • Exception (d): Conduct is authorized under Chapter 162, Health and Safety Code.
  • Penalty (e): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 48.05. Prohibited Camping

  • Offense (b): A person commits an offense if they *intentionally or knowingly* camp in a *public place* without the effective consent of the managing officer or agency.
  • Evidence (c): Intent/knowledge can be established by activities like cooking, making a fire, storing personal belongings for an extended period, digging, or sleeping.
  • Ineffective Consent (d): Consent from a political subdivision is *NOT* effective *unless* it's for:
    1. Recreational purposes.
    2. Sheltering homeless individuals (under an approved Govt Code Ch. 2306 plan).
    3. Purposes permitted by a Beach Access Plan (Nat. Res. Code 61.015).
    4. Emergency shelter during a declared disaster.
  • Penalty (e): Class C Misdemeanor.
  • Preemption (f): This section does *not* preempt a state/local ordinance that is *equal to or more stringent*.
  • Officer Duty (g): *Before* issuing a citation, an officer must make a reasonable effort to:
    1. Advise the person of an alternative, lawful place to camp; AND
    2. Contact a local official or nonprofit to request services for the person (e.g., human trafficking info, etc.).
  • Exception (h): The officer's duty in (g) does not apply if there is an *imminent threat to health or safety* making it impracticable.
  • Personal Property (i): If a person is arrested *solely* for this offense, the officer must preserve their personal property (by letting them take it or by taking custody of it for later retrieval).
  • No Fee (j): A fee may not be charged for storing or releasing property under (i)(2).
