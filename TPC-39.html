Ch. 39: Abuse of Office

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 39.01)

Law relating to a public servant's office or employment

A law specifically applying to a public servant that directly/indirectly: (A) imposes a duty; OR (B) governs conduct.

Misuse

Dealing with property contrary to: (A) an agreement; (B) employment contract/oath; (C) law (including GAA); OR (D) limited purpose for which received.

Sec. 39.02. Abuse of Official Capacity

  • Offense (a): Public servant, with intent to obtain benefit OR intent to harm/defraud another, intentionally or knowingly: (1) violates a law relating to office/employment; OR (2) misuses government property, services, personnel, etc. in custody/possession by virtue of office.
  • Penalty (b): Offense under (a)(1) (Violating Law) is Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Penalties (c): Offense under (a)(2) (Misuse) graded by value of use:
    • Less than $100: Class C Misdemeanor
    • $100 < $750: Class B Misdemeanor
    • $750 < $2,500: Class A Misdemeanor
    • $2,500 < $30,000: State Jail Felony
    • $30,000 < $150,000: Felony of the Third Degree
    • $150,000 < $300,000: Felony of the Second Degree
    • $300,000 or more: Felony of the First Degree
  • Exception (d): Travel perks (frequent flyer miles, hotel discounts, etc.) are NOT things of value belonging to govt due to administrative difficulty.
  • Aggregation (e): Separate transactions under (a)(2) pursuant to one scheme/course of conduct can be aggregated for value.
  • Value Limit (f): Value of use may not exceed fair market value OR replacement cost if FMV unascertainable.

Sec. 39.03. Official Oppression

  • Offense (a): Public servant acting under color of office/employment commits offense if: (1) Intentionally subjects another to mistreatment or unlawful arrest, detention, search, seizure, etc.; (2) Intentionally denies/impedes another's right/privilege, knowing conduct is unlawful; OR (3) Intentionally subjects another to sexual harassment.
  • "Under Color Of" (b): Acts or purports to act in official capacity OR takes advantage of actual/purported capacity.
  • "Sexual Harassment" (c): Unwelcome sexual advances/requests/conduct where submission is made an explicit/implicit term/condition of exercising a right/privilege.
  • Penalty (d): Base offense is Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (d): Becomes Felony of the Third Degree if actor intended to impair accuracy of data reported to TEA through PEIMS.

Sec. 39.04. Violations of the Civil Rights of Person in Custody; Improper Sexual Activity...

  • Who Commits (a): Official/employee/worker/volunteer of correctional/juvenile facility, or peace officer.
  • Offense (a): Intentionally: (1) Denies/impedes person in custody's right/privilege, knowing conduct is unlawful; OR (2) Engages in sexual contact/intercourse/deviate SI with individual in custody (or employs/induces sexual conduct if TJJD/juv facility).
  • Penalties (b - first version listed, H.B. 3157):
    • (a)(1) (Civil Rights Violation): Felony of the Third Degree.
    • (a)(2) (Improper Sexual Activity): Felony of the Second Degree.
    • (a)(2) Enhancement: Becomes Felony of the First Degree if against individual in TJJD custody/juv facility OR juvenile offender in correctional facility.
  • Penalties (b - second version listed, S.B. 312):
    • (a)(1) (Civil Rights Violation): Class A Misdemeanor.
    • (a)(2) (Improper Sexual Activity): Felony of the Second Degree.
  • Offense (f): Employee/worker/volunteer (TDCJ, TJJD, juv facility, prob dept, CSCD) engages in sexual contact/intercourse/deviate SI with individual known to be under supervision but not in custody.
  • Penalty (g): Offense under (f) is State Jail Felony.
  • Affirmative Defense (h): Defense to (f) if actor was spouse at time of offense.
  • Other Provisions: Attorney General has concurrent jurisdiction for SBI/death cases (d). Definitions provided for "Custody," "Correctional facility" (includes immigration, secure juv), "Juvenile facility," etc. (e).

Note: There are two conflicting versions of subsection (b) in the official statute due to legislative amendments, resulting in different penalties for (a)(1). Both are listed above.

Sec. 39.041. Improper Sexual Activity With Committed Person

  • Who Commits (b): Officer/employee of TX Civil Commitment Office, contractor/employee for CCO facility, or volunteer at CCO facility.
  • Offense (b): Intentionally engages in deviate SI, sexual contact, or sexual intercourse with person committed to a civil commitment facility.
  • Penalty (c): Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Affirmative Defense (d): Actor was spouse at time of offense.
  • Prosecution Overlap (e): May be prosecuted under this section and/or other applicable laws.

Sec. 39.015. Concurrent Jurisdiction

  • With consent of local county/district attorney, the Attorney General has concurrent jurisdiction to prosecute offenses under Chapter 39.

Sec. 39.05. Failure to Report Death of Prisoner

  • Offense (a): Person required by CCP Art. 49.18 (or future 49A.153) fails to investigate death, file report, or include known facts.
  • Offense (b): Person required by Govt Code 501.055 fails to: (1) Give notice of inmate death; OR (2) Conduct investigation/file report or include known facts.
  • Penalty (c): Class B Misdemeanor.

Sec. 39.06. Misuse of Official Information

  • Offense (a): Public servant, relying on non-public info from office: (1) Acquires/aids pecuniary interest; (2) Speculates/aids speculation; OR (3) Coerces another into suppressing/failing to report info to LE.
  • Offense (b): Public servant, with intent to benefit or harm/defraud, discloses/uses non-public info from office for nongovernmental purpose.
  • Offense (c): Person, with intent to benefit or harm/defraud, solicits/receives non-public info from public servant.
  • "Information not made public" (d): Info public doesn't generally have access to AND prohibited from disclosure under Ch. 552, Govt Code.
  • Penalty (e): Base offense is Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Penalties Based on Gain (g): If offense results in net pecuniary gain:
    • Gain < $150,000: Felony of the Third Degree
    • $150,000 <= Gain < $300,000: Felony of the Second Degree
    • Gain >= $300,000: Felony of the First Degree

Sec. 39.07. Failure to Comply With Immigration Detainer Request

  • Who Commits (a): Sheriff, chief of police, constable, or person with primary authority for administering jail.
  • Offense (a): Has custody of person subject to ICE detainer request AND knowingly fails to comply with request.
  • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Exception (c): Exception if person subject to detainer provided proof of US citizenship or lawful immigration status (e.g., TX DL).
