Ch. 71: Organized Crime

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 71.01)

Combination

3 or more persons who collaborate in carrying on criminal activities. Participants may not know each other, membership may change, and may have arm's-length relationships (e.g., wholesaler-retailer).

Conspires to commit

A person agrees with 1 or more persons to engage in the offense, AND that person and 1 or more of them perform an overt act. Agreement can be inferred from acts.

Profits

Property constituting or derived from any proceeds obtained, directly or indirectly, from an offense listed in Sec. 71.02.

Criminal street gang

3 or more persons having a common identifying sign or symbol OR an identifiable leadership who continuously or regularly associate in committing criminal activities.

Foreign terrorist organization

3 or more persons operating at least partially outside the U.S. who engage in criminal activity and threaten state security. Includes (but not limited to) a drug cartel.

Sec. 71.02. Engaging in Organized Criminal Activity

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if, with the intent to establish, maintain, or participate in:
    • (1) a combination; OR
    • (2) in the profits of a combination; OR
    • (3) as a member of a criminal street gang; OR
    • (4) as a member of a foreign terrorist organization...
    ...the person commits or conspires to commit one or more of the offenses listed in (a)(1) through (a)(23/24).
  • Listed Offenses: Includes a long list of offenses, such as Murder, Arson, Agg. Robbery, Burglary, Theft, Drug Offenses, Trafficking, etc. (Note: The text contains two slightly different versions of this list from different legislative acts).
  • Base Penalty (b): Offense is one category higher than the most serious underlying offense committed.
    • If underlying offense is a Class A Misdemeanor, the 71.02 offense is a State Jail Felony.
  • Special Penalties (b)(1-3):
    • (b)(1) Life Without Parole: If underlying offense is Aggravated Sexual Assault AND victim is <6, OR <14 + strangulation (22.021(a)(2)(A)), OR <17 + SBI.
    • (b)(2) Life or 30-99 yrs: If underlying offense is Continuous Trafficking of Persons (20.06(g)).
    • (b)(3) Life or 15-99 yrs: If underlying offense is any other F1 (not in 1 or 2).
  • Conspiracy Penalty (c): A conspiracy to commit 71.02 is punished the same as the 71.02 offense (i.e., it is not one category lower).
  • Renunciation at Punishment (d): Defendant can raise issue at punishment. If they prove (by preponderance) they voluntarily withdrew AND made substantial effort to prevent the offense, the offense is the same category as the underlying offense (the +1 enhancement is removed).
  • Deadly Weapon Enhancement (e): If a deadly weapon was used/exhibited, the offense is one *additional* category higher than the penalty under (b) (for F2 or lower). This can make a total of a +2 enhancement.

Sec. 71.022. Coercing, Inducing, or Soliciting Membership...

  • Offense (a): Knowingly causes, enables, encourages, recruits, or solicits another to join a CSG or FTO which requires committing a Class A Misdemeanor or Felony as a condition of membership.
  • Offense (a-1): With intent to coerce/solicit a child (<17) to actively participate, the person:
    • (1) Threatens imminent bodily injury to the child or their family; OR
    • (2) Causes bodily injury to the child or their family.
  • Penalty (b, c):
    • Felony of the Third Degree (Base offense).
    • Felony of the Second Degree (If a second or subsequent offense).

Sec. 71.023. Directing Activities of Criminal Street Gangs...

  • Offense (a): A person, as part of the identifiable leadership of a CSG or FTO, knowingly finances, directs, or supervises the commission of (or conspiracy to commit) one or more specific serious offenses (e.g., 3g offenses (42A.054(a)), felonies with a DW, high-level drug felonies).
  • Penalty (b):
    • Felony of the First Degree, with a minimum sentence of 25 years (punishable for life or 25-99 years).

Sec. 71.021. Violation of Court Order...

  • Offense (a): Knowingly violates a temporary or permanent order issued under Sec. 125.065(a) or (b), Civil Practice and Remedies Code (enjoining organized criminal activity).
  • Penalty (c): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 71.028. Gang-Free Zones

  • Applies to (b): 71.02 offenses based on (a)(1) (e.g., murder, agg assault), (a)(4) (firearms), or (a)(7) (child offenses). Does *not* apply to burglary, theft, BMV, or UUMV.
  • Enhancement (c): Punishment is increased to the next highest category.
  • Locations (1,000 ft): School, Institution of higher education, Youth center, Playground, General residential operation.
  • Locations (300 ft): Shopping mall, Movie theater, Public swimming pool, Video arcade facility.
  • Location (On): A school bus.
  • Exception (d): No enhancement if the offense is already an F1.

Sec. 71.029. Maps as Evidence

  • Admissibility (a): A map showing gang-free zones is admissible and is prima facie evidence if the city/county adopts a resolution or ordinance approving the map.
  • Filing (c): Map must be filed with the county clerk.

Sec. 71.03. Defenses Excluded

It is NO DEFENSE to prosecution under Sec. 71.02 that:

  • One or more members are not criminally responsible for the object offense;
  • One or more members have been acquitted, have not been prosecuted or convicted, or are immune;
  • A person has been charged with, acquitted, or convicted of any of the underlying offenses; OR
  • The number or identity of persons changed (as long as 2 or more remain).

Sec. 71.04. Testimonial Immunity

  • Required Testimony (a): A party to an offense under this chapter may be required to furnish evidence or testify about the offense.
  • Immunity (b): The testimony (or info derived from it) may not be used against the witness in any criminal case (except for aggravated perjury or contempt).

Sec. 71.05. Renunciation Defense

  • Affirmative Defense (a): It is an affirmative defense (to 71.02) that the actor voluntarily and completely renounced, withdrew from the combination *before* the offense, AND took affirmative action that PREVENTED the commission of the offense.
  • Not Voluntary (b): Renunciation is *not* voluntary if motivated by increased risk of detection or a decision to postpone the crime.
  • Mitigation (c) / (71.02(d)): If the actor withdrew and made substantial effort to prevent (but failed), it is *not* an affirmative defense, but can be used at punishment to remove the +1 category enhancement.
