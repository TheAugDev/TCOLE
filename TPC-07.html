Ch. 7: Criminal Responsibility for Conduct of Another

Back to Main Page

Sec. 7.01. Parties to Offenses

  • A person is criminally responsible as a "party" if the offense is committed by their own conduct, by the conduct of another (for which they are responsible), or by both.
  • Each party to an offense may be charged with the commission of the offense.
  • This section abolishes all traditional distinctions between accomplices and principals. Every party can be charged directly for the offense.

Sec. 7.02. Criminal Responsibility for Conduct of Another

A person is criminally responsible for another's conduct if:

  • (a)(1): Acting with the required culpability, they cause or aid an innocent or nonresponsible person to commit the offense.
  • (a)(2): Acting with intent to promote or assist the offense, they solicit, encourage, direct, aid, or attempt to aid the other person to commit it. (This is the primary "party liability" statute).
  • (a)(3): They have a legal duty to prevent the offense AND act with intent to promote or assist it, and then fail to make a reasonable effort to prevent it.
  • (b) Conspiracy Liability: If in the attempt to carry out a conspiracy to commit *one felony*, *another felony* is committed by a co-conspirator, all conspirators are guilty of the felony actually committed (even with no intent to commit it) IF:
    1. The offense was committed in furtherance of the unlawful purpose; AND
    2. The offense was one that should have been anticipated.
  • (b) "Conspiracy" is defined here as an agreement between two or more persons to commit a felony.

Sec. 7.03. Defenses Excluded

When being prosecuted as a party to an offense, it is NOT a defense that:

  • (1): The actor belongs to a class of persons legally incapable of committing the offense in an individual capacity (e.g., a man being a party to an offense only a woman can commit).
  • (2): The other person (the primary actor) has been acquitted, has not been prosecuted or convicted, has been convicted of a different offense, or is immune from prosecution.

Sec. 7.21. Key Definitions (Subchapter B)

Agent

A director, officer, employee, or other person authorized to act in behalf of the business entity.

Business entity

An entity or organization governed by the Business Organizations Code, other than a corporation, association, or limited liability company.

High managerial agent

A partner; an officer; or an agent with duties of such responsibility that their conduct may be assumed to represent the entity's policy.

Sec. 7.22. Criminal Responsibility of Corporation, Association, etc.

  • (a) General Responsibility: An entity is responsible for an offense if an agent performs it on their behalf and within the scope of employment, AND the offense is defined by a law that intends to impose liability on such entities (or is a strict liability offense).
  • (b) Felony Responsibility: An entity is responsible for a FELONY *only if* its commission was authorized, requested, commanded, performed, or recklessly tolerated by:
    1. A majority of the governing body; or
    2. A high managerial agent acting in behalf of the entity and within their scope of office/employment.

Sec. 7.23. Criminal Responsibility of Person for Conduct in Behalf of Entity

  • (a) Individual Liability: An individual is criminally responsible for conduct they perform in the name of or on behalf of an entity to the *same extent* as if they did it in their own name.
  • (b) Omission Liability: An agent with primary responsibility for a duty imposed by law on an entity is criminally responsible for *omitting* that duty, just as if the duty were imposed directly on them.
  • (c) Sentencing: An individual convicted of an offense performed on behalf of an entity is subject to the sentence authorized by law for an *individual* (not the entity).

Sec. 7.24. Defense to Criminal Responsibility of Entity

  • It is an AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE to prosecution under 7.22(a)(1) or (a)(2) that the high managerial agent with supervisory responsibility employed due diligence to prevent its commission.
