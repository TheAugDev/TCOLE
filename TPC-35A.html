Ch. 35A: Health Care Fraud

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 35A.01)

(1) Claim

A written or electronically submitted request for reimbursement for a service/product under a health care program.

(2) Fiscal Agent

A person (or their agent) who contracts with the state/fed to receive, process, and pay claims under a health care program.

(3) Health Care Practitioner

A licensed provider such as a dentist, podiatrist, psychologist, physical therapist, chiropractor, or registered nurse.

(4) Health Care Program

A state or federally funded program (or both) to provide health care services, including those with a managed care model.

(5) Health Care Recipient

An individual to whom a service/product is provided (or purported to be) and for whom a claim is made.

(6) Managed Care Organization (MCO)

A person authorized by law to arrange for or provide a managed care plan.

(7) Physician

A physician licensed to practice medicine in this state.

(8) Provider

A person who participates in a health care program. Includes: management companies, employees, MCOs, and manufacturers/distributors of products.

(9) Service

Includes care or treatment of a health care recipient.

(10) High Managerial Agent

A director, officer, or employee whose conduct may be assumed to represent the policy or intent of the provider.

Sec. 35A.02(a). Health Care Fraud Offenses

A person commits an offense if the person...

  • (1) Knowingly makes a false statement of material fact for an unauthorized benefit.
  • (2) Knowingly conceals or fails to disclose information for an unauthorized benefit.
  • (3) Knowingly receives a benefit for another and converts it to their own use.
  • (4) Knowingly makes a false statement about facility conditions for certification.
  • (5) Knowingly pays, solicits, or receives a kickback in addition to the authorized payment.
  • (6) Knowingly presents a claim for services rendered by an unlicensed person.
  • (7) Knowingly makes a claim for a service that was not approved by a doc, is substantially inadequate, or for a product that is adulterated/mislabeled.
  • (8) Knowingly makes a claim but fails to indicate the license/ID number of the practitioner who provided the service.
  • (9) Knowingly enters a conspiracy to defraud the government.
  • (10) Is an MCO that knowingly fails to provide a required service, falsifies info, or engages in fraudulent enrollment/marketing.
  • (11) Knowingly obstructs an investigation by the attorney general.
  • (12) Knowingly makes/uses a false record to conceal, avoid, or decrease an obligation to pay money to the govt (a "reverse false claim").

Sec. 35A.02(b). Penalties

Standard Penalties (by Value):

  • < $100: Class C Misdemeanor
  • $100 < $750: Class B Misdemeanor
  • $750 < $2,500: Class A Misdemeanor
  • $2,500 < $30,000: State Jail Felony
  • $30,000 < $150,000: Felony of the Third Degree
  • $150,000 < $300,000: Felony of the Second Degree
  • $300,000 or more: Felony of the First Degree

Special Penalties (Not Based on Value):

  • State Jail Felony:
    • (b)(4)(B): Offense is (a)(11) Obstructing an AG Investigation.
    • (b)(4)(C): The value cannot be reasonably ascertained.
  • Felony of the Third Degree:
    • (b)(5)(B): Actor submitted more than 25 but fewer than 50 fraudulent claims.
  • Felony of the Second Degree:
    • (b)(6)(B): Actor submitted 50 or more fraudulent claims.

Other Provisions (Sec. 35A.02(c) - (f))

  • Prosecution (c): Actor may be prosecuted under this section, another law, or both.
  • Aggregation (d): If part of one scheme or continuing course of conduct, amounts can be aggregated into one offense.
  • Enhancement (e): If the actor is a High Managerial Agent, the punishment is increased to the next highest category (unless already an F1).
  • Jurisdiction (f): The Attorney General has concurrent jurisdiction to prosecute with the consent of the local prosecutor.
