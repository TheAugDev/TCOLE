Key Definitions (Sec. 28.01)

Habitation

A structure or vehicle adapted for the overnight accommodation of persons. Includes each separately secured portion and connected structures.

Building

Any structure or enclosure intended for use as a habitation or for trade, manufacture, ornament, or use.

Property

(A) Real property; (B) Tangible or intangible personal property; or (C) A document (like money) that represents value.

Vehicle

Any device in, on, or by which any person or property is or may be propelled, moved, or drawn in commerce or transportation.

Open-space land

Real property that is undeveloped for the purpose of human habitation.

Controlled burning

Burning unwanted vegetation with the owner's consent, controlled and limited to a designated area.

Sec. 28.02. Arson

  • Offense (a): A person commits arson if they start a fire or cause an explosion with intent to destroy or damage:
    1. Any vegetation, fence, or structure on open-space land; OR
    2. Any building, habitation, or vehicle knowing that it is:
      • (A) within an incorporated city or town;
      • (B) insured;
      • (C) subject to a mortgage;
      • (D) on property belonging to another;
      • (E) has property of another located within it; OR
      • (F) when the person is reckless about whether it will endanger the life of an individual or safety of another's property.
  • Offense (a-1): A person commits an offense if they recklessly start a fire/explosion while manufacturing a controlled substance AND the fire/explosion damages any building, habitation, or vehicle.
  • Offense (a-2): A person commits an offense if they intentionally start a fire/explosion and in doing so:
    • (1) recklessly damages or destroys a building of another; OR
    • (2) recklessly causes bodily injury or death to another.
  • Exception (b): It is an exception to (a)(1) (open-space land) if the fire was part of a controlled burning.
  • Defense (c): It is a defense to (a)(2)(A) (within city limits) if the actor got a permit or written authorization.
  • Penalties:
    • (d) Offense under (a): Felony of the Second Degree.
      • ENHANCED TO F1 if: (1) bodily injury or death was suffered, OR (2) the property was a habitation, place of assembly, or place of worship.
    • (e) Offense under (a-1): State Jail Felony.
      • ENHANCED TO F3 if bodily injury or death was suffered.
    • (f) Offense under (a-2): State Jail Felony.
  • Overlap (g): If conduct fits (a-1) or (a-2) AND another offense (e.g., Arson (a) or another PC section), the actor can be prosecuted under any applicable section.

Sec. 28.03. Criminal Mischief

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if, without the effective consent of the owner, they:
    1. Intentionally or Knowingly damages or destroys tangible property.
    2. Intentionally or Knowingly tampers with tangible property and causes pecuniary loss or substantial inconvenience.
    3. Intentionally or Knowingly makes markings (graffiti) on tangible property.
  • Penalties (b), (f), (h), (i), (j), (l): This offense is punished by a value ladder, with many specific enhancements:
    • Class C: < $100 or causes substantial inconvenience.
    • Class B: $100 - $749.
    • Class A: $750 - $2,499 OR impairs/diverts public water supply.
    • State Jail Felony:
      • $2,500 - $29,999
      • < $2,500 if habitation damaged by firearm/explosive.
      • < $2,500 if fence for livestock/game.
      • < $30,000 if impairs/diverts public comms, transport, gas, flood control, etc.
      • < $30,000 if damage to vehicle during catalytic converter removal.
      • (f) $750 - $29,999 if damage to place of worship, burial, monument, or community center.
      • (h) $750 - $29,999 if damage to a school or institution of higher education.
    • Felony of the Third Degree:
      • $30,000 - $149,999
      • Causes death of cattle, bison, or horse by firearm/weapon.
      • Impairs access to an ATM.
      • < $150,000 if impairs/diverts public power supply.
      • (j) < $150,000 if damage to transportation communications equipment.
      • (l) Damage to copper/brass components of a critical infrastructure facility causing impairment.
    • Felony of the Second Degree:
      • $150,000 - $299,999
      • $1,000 - $299,999 if habitation damaged during a Criminal Trespass (30.05).
    • Felony of the First Degree:
      • $300,000 or more.
      • (i) Damage to livestock by introducing "mad cow disease" or other listed animal disease.
  • Presumption (c): A person receiving the benefit is presumed to have *knowingly* tampered if the supply (gas, water, etc.) is diverted from or prevented from being registered by a meter.
  • Aggregation (e): Amounts can be aggregated into one offense if part of one scheme or continuing course of conduct.
  • Exception (k): Does not apply to killing cattle/bison/horse if done during official military duties or regular agricultural labor.

Sec. 28.04. Reckless Damage or Destruction

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if, without effective consent, they recklessly damage or destroy property.
  • Penalty (b): Class C Misdemeanor.

Sec. 28.06. Amount of Pecuniary Loss

  • If Destroyed (a): Fair market value OR replacement cost.
  • If Damaged (b): Cost of repairing or restoring.
  • Documents (c): Amount due (if a debt) OR greatest amount of economic loss owner might suffer.
  • If Unascertained (d): Deemed to be $750 or more but less than $2,500 (Class A).
  • Actor's Interest (e): If actor proves they have an interest, the value of that interest is deducted from the pecuniary loss.

Sec. 28.07. Interference with Railroad Property

  • Offense (b)(1): Throws an object or discharges a firearm/weapon at a train.
    • Class B Misdemeanor. Enhanced to F3 if it causes bodily injury.
  • Offense (b)(2)(A): Enters or remains on railroad property (trespass).
    • Class C Misdemeanor.
  • Offense (b)(2)(B-D): Tampers with property, places obstruction, or causes derailment.
    • Follows the standard pecuniary loss ladder (from Class C up to F1) identical to Criminal Mischief.
  • Exception (f): Trespass offense does not apply to railroad employees or union reps acting under the Railway Labor Act.

Sec. 28.08. Graffiti

  • Offense (a): Intentionally or knowingly makes markings on property without consent using paint, an indelible marker, or an etching/engraving device.
  • Penalties (b): Follows the standard pecuniary loss ladder (from Class C up to F1) identical to Criminal Mischief.
  • Aggregation (c): Amounts can be aggregated if part of one scheme or continuing course of conduct.
  • Enhancement (d): Offense is a State Jail Felony if:
    • Loss is $750 - $29,999 AND
    • Marking is on a school, institution of higher ed, place of worship/burial, public monument, or community center.

Sec. 28.09. Damaging Critical Infrastructure Facility

  • Definitions (a):
    • "Critical infrastructure facility" means a power generating facility, substation, switching station, control center, or transmission/distribution facility.
    • "Extended power outage" means an outage lasting 2+ hours OR affecting 1,000+ meters.
  • Offense (b): Person I/K damages, destroys, vandalizes, or impairs a CIF AND as a result, causes an extended power outage.
  • Penalty (c): Felony of the Second Degree.
    • ENHANCED TO F1 if: (1) pecuniary damage is $100,000 or more, OR (2) actor uses a firearm, drone, cyber attack, or explosive weapon.

Sec. 28.10. Enhanced Penalty

  • Enhancement: Any misdemeanor or state jail felony offense under Chapter 28 is increased to a Felony of the Third Degree if committed in the course of Smuggling of Persons (Sec. 20.05(a)(2)).

Sec. 28.05. Actor's Interest in Property

  • No Defense: It is NOT a defense that the actor has an interest in the property (e.g., co-owns it) if another person also has an interest that the actor is not entitled to infringe.
