Ch. 33A: Telecommunications Crimes

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 33A.01)

Counterfeit telecommunications access device

An access device that is false, fraudulent, not issued to a legitimate account, or otherwise unlawful.

Counterfeit telecommunications device

A device (e.g., clone telephone, tumbler microchip) altered to get service without consent of the provider.

Deliver

To sell, give, loan, or otherwise transfer a device or materials to another person (actually or constructively).

Publish

To communicate or make information available (orally, writing, telecommunications) including on a computer bulletin board.

Telecommunications

The transmission of data, images, signals, sounds, etc., over a communications system by any method.

Telecommunications access device

An instrument, card, code, account number, PIN, serial number, etc., that can facilitate the use of a telecommunications service.

Telecommunications device

Any instrument that facilitates telecommunications, including a computer, telephone, pager, radio, or modem.

Telecommunications service

The provision or generation of telecommunications through the use of a device or access device.

Value of the telecommunications service...

Includes: (A) lawful charge avoided; (B) money/property lost or stolen; and (C) expenditure by victim to verify service integrity.

Sec. 33A.02. Unauthorized Use of Telecommunications Service

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if they are an officer, shareholder, partner, employee, agent, or independent contractor of a telecommunications service provider AND they knowingly and without authority use or divert telecommunications service for their own benefit or to the benefit of another.
  • Penalties (b): (Based on value of service used/diverted)
    • Less than $500: Class B Misdemeanor.
    • $500 < $1,500: Class A Misdemeanor.
      • (Or < $500 + 1 prior conviction under this chapter).
    • $1,500 < $20,000: State Jail Felony.
      • (Or < $1,500 + 2 or more prior convictions under this chapter).
    • $20,000 < $100,000: Felony of the Third Degree.
    • $100,000 < $200,000: Felony of the Second Degree.
    • $200,000 or more: Felony of the First Degree.
  • Aggregation (c): If part of one scheme or continuing course of conduct, values may be aggregated to determine the grade of offense.

Sec. 33A.04. Theft of Telecommunications Service

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if they knowingly obtain or attempt to obtain telecommunications service to avoid or cause another to avoid a lawful charge by using:
    1. A telecommunications access device without authority/consent; OR
    2. A counterfeit telecommunications access device; OR
    3. A telecommunications device or counterfeit telecommunications device; OR
    4. A fraudulent or deceptive scheme, pretense, method, or conspiracy.
  • Penalties (b): (Based on value of service obtained/attempted)
    • Less than $500: Class B Misdemeanor.
    • $500 < $1,500: Class A Misdemeanor.
      • (Or < $500 + 1 prior conviction under this chapter).
    • $1,500 < $20,000: State Jail Felony.
      • (Or < $1,500 + 2 or more prior convictions under this chapter).
    • $20,000 < $100,000: Felony of the Third Degree.
    • $100,000 < $200,000: Felony of the Second Degree.
    • $200,000 or more: Felony of the First Degree.
  • Aggregation (c): If part of one scheme or continuing course of conduct, values may be aggregated to determine the grade of offense.

Sec. 33A.03. Manufacture, Possession, or Delivery of Unlawful Telecommunications Device

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if they manufacture, possess, deliver, offer to deliver, or advertise:
    1. A counterfeit telecommunications device; OR
    2. A telecommunications device that is intended to be used to:
      • Commit an offense under 33A.04 (Theft of Service); OR
      • Conceal the existence or place of origin or destination of a service.
  • Offense (b): A person commits an offense if they deliver, offer to deliver, or advertise plans, instructions, or materials for manufacture of a device in (a).
  • Penalty (c): Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Defense (d): It is a defense if the person was an officer, agent, or employee of a provider engaging in conduct for a law enforcement investigation.

Sec. 33A.05. Publication of Telecommunications Access Device

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if they with criminal negligence publish a telecommunications access device or counterfeit telecommunications access device that is designed to be used to commit an offense under 33A.04 (Theft of Service).
  • Penalties (b):
    • Class A Misdemeanor: Base offense.
    • Felony of the Third Degree: If the person has been previously convicted of an offense under this chapter.

Sec. 33A.051. False Caller Identification Information Display

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if they, with intent to defraud or cause harm, make a call or engage in conduct that results in the display on another's device of data that misrepresents the actor's identity or telephone number.
  • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • No Enhancement (c): A conviction under this section may not be used for enhancement purposes under any other section of this chapter.
  • Defenses (d): It is a defense if the actor:
    1. Blocked caller ID information;
    2. Was a peace officer or federal law enforcement officer lawfully discharging duty;
    3. Was an agent of a federal intelligence or security agency lawfully discharging duty;
    4. Was an officer, agent, or employee of a telecommunications service provider acting in their official capacity; OR
    5. Was a licensed private investigator lawfully conducting an investigation.

Sec. 33A.06. Assistance by Attorney General

  • The Attorney General, if requested by a prosecuting attorney, may assist in the investigation or prosecution of an offense under this chapter.
