Ch. 76: Terroristic Offenses

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 76.01)

Material support or resources

Includes currency, financial services, lodging, training, safehouses, false ID, communications equipment, computers, software, facilities, deadly weapons, lethal substances, explosives, personnel, and transportation.
Exceptions: Medical care by a licensed provider or religious materials.

Training

Teaching or demonstrating (in person, writing, or computer) the use, application, or making of:
(A) A weapon prohibited by 46.05
(B) A deadly weapon
(C) A component of an explosive weapon
(D) An incendiary device capable of causing injury or death.

Sec. 76.02. Terrorism

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if they:
    1. Commit or conspire to commit a predicate offense (e.g., Chapter 19, Sec. 20.02, 20.03, 20A.02, 22.02, 22.021, felony 22.05, felony 22.07, 22.09, 28.02, felony 28.07, 29.03, felony 38.152, or 46.08); AND
    2. Commits or conspires with the intent to:
      • (A) Intimidate or coerce the public; or
      • (B) Influence the policy or conduct of the state or US by intimidation or coercion.
  • Penalty (b): One category higher than the most serious predicate offense.
    • Exception (b)(1): If predicate is Class A Misdemeanor, it remains a Class A Misdemeanor but with a minimum 180 days confinement.
    • Exception (b)(2): If predicate is Felony of the First Degree, it remains an F1 but with a minimum 15 years confinement.

Sec. 76.03. Aiding in Commission of Terrorism

  • Offense (a): A person intentionally raises, solicits, collects, or provides material support or resources with intent or knowledge that they will be used (wholly or partly) to aid in committing a 76.02 (Terrorism) offense.
  • Penalty (b): Same degree as the offense under 76.02 that the person aided.

Sec. 76.04. Hindering Prosecution of Terrorism

  • Offense (a): With intent to hinder the arrest, prosecution, conviction, or punishment of another for a 76.02 or 76.03 offense, the person:
    1. Harbors or conceals the other;
    2. Provides or aids in providing means of avoiding arrest or effecting escape;
    3. Warns the other of impending discovery or apprehension; OR
    4. Tampers with any physical evidence.
  • Penalty (b): Same degree as the offense under 76.02 or 76.03 for which the person hindered.

Sec. 76.045. Transnational Repression

  • Key Definitions (a):
    • Agent of a foreign government...: A person acting on behalf of or employed/controlled by a foreign government or foreign terrorist organization.
    • Protected conduct: Speech or expressive conduct protected by the US or Texas Constitution.
  • Offense (b): A person commits an offense if they:
    1. Commit or conspire to commit a predicate offense (e.g., 20A.02, 22.01, 22.02, 42.07, 42.072, or 43.05); AND
    2. Does so with intent to:
      • (A) Cause another to act for a foreign government;
      • (B) Cause another to leave/be confined in the US;
      • (C) Discourage another from protected conduct; OR
      • (D) Retaliate against another for protected conduct; AND
    3. Commits or conspires to commit the offense as an agent of a foreign government or foreign terrorist organization.
  • Penalty (c): One category higher than the most serious predicate offense.
    • Exception: If predicate is F1, it remains an F1 but with a minimum 15 years confinement.

Sec. 76.046. Unauthorized Enforcement of Foreign Law

  • Offense (b): A person, as an agent of a foreign government or foreign terrorist organization, without approval of this state or the US:
    1. Prevents another person from violating the laws of a foreign government; OR
    2. Detects, investigates, monitors, or surveilles another person to prevent them from violating the laws of a foreign government.
  • Penalty (c): Felony of the Second Degree.

Sec. 76.05. Assistance by Attorney General

  • The Attorney General, if requested by the attorney representing the state, may assist in the investigation or prosecution of an offense under this chapter.
  • The AG must designate one individual in their office to coordinate responses to these requests.
