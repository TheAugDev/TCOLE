Ch. 30: Burglary & Criminal Trespass

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 30.01)

Habitation

A structure/vehicle adapted for overnight accommodation. Includes each separately secured portion AND each connected/appurtenant structure.

Building

Any enclosed structure intended for use as a habitation or for trade, manufacture, ornament, or use.

Vehicle

Any device for transporting people/property, *except* devices classified as "habitation."

Controlled substance

Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 481.002, Health and Safety Code.

Wholesale distributor...

A wholesale distributor as defined by Sec. 431.401, Health and Safety Code.

Sec. 30.02. Burglary

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if, without effective consent, they:
    1. Enters a habitation or building (not open to public) with intent to commit a felony, theft, or assault; OR
    2. Remains concealed in a building or habitation with intent to commit a felony, theft, or assault; OR
    3. Enters a building or habitation AND commits or attempts to commit a felony, theft, or assault.
  • "Enter" (b): Means to intrude (1) any part of the body; or (2) any physical object connected with the body.
  • Penalties (c), (c-1), (c-2), (d):
    • State Jail Felony: Base offense for Burglary of a Building.
    • Felony of the Third Degree:
      • (c-1) Building is a commercial building (pharmacy, clinic, etc.) storing controlled substances, AND actor entered with intent to commit theft of a controlled substance.
      • (c-2) Building (non-habitation) + offense committed during Smuggling of Persons (20.05(a)(2)).
    • Felony of the Second Degree: Base offense for Burglary of a Habitation.
    • Felony of the First Degree:
      • (d) Premises is a Habitation AND any party to the offense:
      • (1) Entered with intent to commit a felony other than felony theft; OR
      • (2) Committed or attempted to commit a felony other than felony theft.

Sec. 30.03. Burglary of Coin-Operated... Machines

  • Offense (a): Without effective consent, breaks or enters into any coin-operated machine, coin collection machine, or similar equipment... with intent to obtain property or services.
  • "Entry" (b): Includes every kind of entry except one with effective consent.
  • Penalty (c): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 30.04. Burglary of Vehicles

  • Offense (a): Without effective consent, breaks into or enters a vehicle (or any part) with intent to commit any felony or theft.
  • "Enter" (b): Same as 30.02: (1) any part of the body; or (2) any physical object connected with the body.
  • "Rail Car" (c): A container or trailer on a rail car is part of the rail car.
  • Penalties (d):
    • Class A Misdemeanor: Base offense.
    • Class A Misdemeanor (w/ 6mo min confinement): If 1 prior conviction under this section.
    • State Jail Felony: If:
      • (A) 2 or more prior convictions under this section; OR
      • (B) The vehicle is a rail car.
    • Felony of the Third Degree: If:
      • (A) Vehicle is owned by wholesale distributor of prescription drugs AND actor intended to steal a controlled substance; OR
      • (B) Offense committed during Smuggling of Persons (20.05(a)(2)).
  • "Previously Convicted" (d-1): Includes deferred adjudication.
  • Defense (e): Actor was an employee/rep exercising a right under the Railway Labor Act.

Sec. 30.05. Criminal Trespass

  • Offense (a): Person enters or remains on or in property of another without effective consent, AND the person:
    1. Had notice that the entry was forbidden; OR
    2. Received notice to depart but failed to do so.
  • "Entry" (b)(1): Intrusion of the entire body. (DIFFERENT from Burglary)
  • "Notice" (b)(2): (A) Oral/written communication; (B) Fencing/enclosure; (C) Signs; (D) Purple paint marks (8"x1" lines, 3-5ft off ground, 100ft apart forest / 1000ft apart non-forest); (E) Visible crops.
  • Key Definitions (b): Includes "Critical infrastructure facility" (must be fenced/barriered), "Residential land", "Institution of higher education", etc.
  • Firearm Sign (c): Specific requirements for a 30.05 sign prohibiting firearms (Eng/Span, 1" letters, conspicuous).
  • Penalties (d):
    • Class B Misdemeanor: Base offense.
    • Class C Misdemeanor: On (A) ag land (w/in 100ft of boundary) OR (B) residential land (w/in 100ft of protected freshwater area).
    • Class A Misdemeanor: If:
      • (A) In a habitation, shelter, Superfund site, or critical infrastructure facility; OR
      • (B) On campus w/ prior trespass conviction; OR
      • (C) Person carries a deadly weapon; OR
      • (D) At a general residential operation.
    • Felony of the Third Degree: If committed during Smuggling of Persons (20.05(a)(2)).
  • Firearm Trespass Penalty (d-3): If *sole basis* for trespass is a firearm:
    • Class C Misdemeanor (max $200 fine).
    • Becomes Class A Misdemeanor if actor receives *personal* oral notice and *fails to depart*.
  • Defenses (e): (1) Firefighter/EMS; (2) Utility worker/agent; (3) Other authorized agent.
  • Defenses (f, f-1, f-2, f-3, f-4): (f) LTC holder w/ concealed/holstered handgun; (f-1) Condo owner/tenant/guest w/ firearm; (f-2) Tenant w/ firearm; (f-3) Mfg. home tenant w/ firearm; (f-4) Hotel guest w/ firearm.
  • Exception (i): Section does not apply to Peace Officers (or recognized state POs) carrying a weapon.

Sec. 30.06. Trespass by LTC Holder (Concealed)

  • Offense (a): A license holder carries a concealed handgun on property of another without effective consent AND received notice it was forbidden.
  • Notice (b, c): Oral communication OR "Written communication" (a specific card OR a sign with exact 30.06 language, Eng/Span, 1" letters, conspicuous).
  • Penalty (d): Class C Misdemeanor (max $200 fine).
    • Becomes Class A Misdemeanor if actor receives *personal* oral notice and *fails to depart*.
  • Exception (e): Does not apply to most government property (where carrying isn't already banned by 46.03).
  • Defense (g): Actor was given oral notice and promptly departed.
  • Defenses (e-1 - f-2): Includes defenses for condo/tenant/hotel guest, volunteer emergency services, first responders, etc.

Sec. 30.07. Trespass by LTC Holder (Openly Carried)

  • Offense (a): A license holder openly carries a handgun on property of another without effective consent AND received notice it was forbidden.
  • Notice (b, c): Oral communication OR "Written communication" (a specific card OR a sign with exact 30.07 language, Eng/Span, 1" letters, conspicuous, at each entrance).
  • Penalty (d): Class C Misdemeanor (max $200 fine).
    • Becomes Class A Misdemeanor if actor receives *personal* oral notice and *fails to depart*.
  • No Defense (f): It is NOT a defense that the handgun was in a holster.
  • Defense (h): Actor was given oral notice and promptly departed.
  • Other Defenses: Same exceptions/defenses as 30.06 (govt property, condo/tenant/hotel, emergency services, etc.).
