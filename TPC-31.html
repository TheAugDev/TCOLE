Ch. 31: Theft

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 31.01)

Deception (5 forms)

Creating/confirming a false impression of law/fact; failing to correct a false impression; preventing information acquisition; selling encumbered property without disclosure; or **promising performance the actor does not intend to perform** (failure to perform is *not* sufficient proof alone).

Deprive (3 methods)

Withhold property **permanently** or for so long a time that a **major portion of value** is lost; restore only upon **payment of reward**; or **dispose** of property making recovery unlikely.

Appropriate (2 methods)

To bring about a transfer of title or **other nonpossessory interest** in property (other than real property) OR to **acquire or otherwise exercise control** over property (other than real property).

Effective Consent (Exclusions)

Consent is **NOT effective** if induced by deception or coercion; given by a person the actor knows is unauthorized; given by a person known to be incapacitated (youth, defect, intoxication, or **advanced age/diminished capacity**); or given solely to **detect the commission of an offense** (e.g., undercover sting).

Property

Real property; tangible or intangible personal property (including anything severed from land); or a document (e.g., money) embodying anything of value.

Service

Labor, professional service, utility/transportation service, lodging, restaurant/entertainment, and the supply of property for use (e.g., a rental car).

Retail Merchandise

One or more items of tangible personal property displayed, held, stored, or offered for sale by a merchant. Includes a **gift card**.

Shielding Instrument

Item or tool designed, made, or adapted to prevent the detection of stolen merchandise by a retail theft detector (e.g., metal-lined bag, security tag removal tool).

Sec. 31.03. Theft - Core Offense

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if he **unlawfully appropriates property** with **intent to deprive** the owner of property.
  • Unlawful Appropriation (b): Appropriation is unlawful if:
    1. It is **without the owner's effective consent**.
    2. The property is **stolen** and the actor appropriates it **knowing it was stolen** by another.
    3. Property represented by law enforcement as **stolen** and the actor appropriates it **believing it was stolen** by another (Sting operation).
  • Value (31.08): Determined by fair market value at the time/place of offense, or cost of replacement. Stolen **retail merchandise** uses the sales price/rental price + replacement cost.
  • Consolidation (31.02): The single offense of Theft (31.03) supersedes all previous separate offenses like swindling, theft by false pretext, embezzlement, and receiving stolen property.
  • Aggregation (31.09): Amounts stolen under one **scheme or continuing course of conduct** may be aggregated to determine the grade of offense.

Punishment Tiers (Sec. 31.03(e))

Offense Grade Value Threshold / Special Property
Class C Misdemeanor Value is **less than $100**.
Class B Misdemeanor Value is **$100 to less than $750**, OR value is less than $100 AND **1 prior theft conviction**, OR property is a **driver's license/ID card**.
Class A Misdemeanor Value is **$750 to less than $2,500**.
State Jail Felony (SJF) Value is **$2,500 to less than $30,000**; OR property is **less than 10 head of sheep/swine/goats**; OR stolen **from the person** of another/corpse/grave; OR the property is a **firearm**; OR **2 or more prior theft convictions** (value less than $2,500); OR catalytic converter (replacement cost < $30k); OR official ballot/envelope.
Felony 3rd Degree (F3) Value is **$30,000 to less than $150,000**; OR **cattle/horses/exotic livestock** (< $150,000); OR **10 or more head of sheep/swine/goats** (< $150,000); OR controlled substance stolen from a commercial building/wholesale distributor vehicle (< $150,000).
Felony 2nd Degree (F2) Value is **$150,000 to less than $300,000**; OR property stolen is an **automated teller machine** or its contents/components (< $300,000).
Felony 1st Degree (F1) Value is **$300,000 or more**.

Penalty Enhancements (Sec. 31.03(f))

An offense is increased to the **next higher category** if shown that:

  • The actor was a **public servant** or in a **contractual relationship with government/Medicare** and property came into custody by virtue of that status.
  • The owner was an **elderly individual** or a **nonprofit organization**.
  • During the offense, the actor used means to **defeat security:** caused a **fire exit alarm** to sound, **deactivated an alarm/detector**, or used a **shielding/deactivation instrument**.

Sec. 31.04. Theft of Service

Offense (a): Committed if, with **intent to avoid payment** for service known to be for compensation, the actor:

  • Secures performance by **deception, threat, or false token**.
  • Intentionally/knowingly **diverts** another's services (to which the actor is not entitled) to their own or another's benefit.
  • Holds personal property beyond the **rental period** without effective consent, depriving the owner of its use in further rentals.
  • Secures service by agreeing to compensate, and fails to make **full payment after receiving notice demanding payment**.

Presumptions of Intent to Avoid Payment (31.04(b))

  • Absconded without paying or **expressly refused** to pay (e.g., hotel/restaurant).
  • Failed to make payment under a service agreement **within 10 days** after receiving notice demanding payment.
  • Failed to pay rental charges within **10 days** after receiving notice *after* returning the property late.
  • Failed to return rental property within **2, 3, or 5 days** of receiving notice demanding return (depending on value).

Sec. 31.07. Unauthorized Use of a Vehicle (UUV)

  • Offense (a): Intentionally or knowingly **operates** another's **boat, airplane, or motor-propelled vehicle** without the effective consent of the owner.
  • Penalty (b): State Jail Felony.

Specialized Property & Aggravations

Organized Retail Theft (31.16)

  • Committed by acting **in concert with one or more others** (Subdivision 1), or on **two or more occasions within 180 days** (Subdivision 2), or knowingly obtaining a benefit from such conduct (Subdivision 3), or acting in concert to **overwhelm security response** (Subdivision 4).
  • Punishment is based on the **total aggregated value** of the property involved (ranging from Class B Misdemeanor to Felony 1st Degree).

Mail Theft (31.20)

  • Unlawful appropriation of **mail** without effective consent, with intent to **deprive the addressee** or **steal a negotiable instrument**.
  • Graded based on the **number of addressees** (Class A Misdemeanor to Felony 3rd Degree).
  • **Enhancement:** Increased penalty if the mail contained **identifying information** (to facilitate 32.51) or a **negotiable instrument** (to facilitate Ch. 32), or if the victim was an **elderly/disabled individual**.
  • **Presumption:** Possession of mail from **five or more addressees** creates a rebuttable presumption of unlawful appropriation/intent to deprive.

Unauthorized Possession of Catalytic Converter (31.21)

  • **Offense:** Intentionally or knowingly possesses a catalytic converter removed from a motor vehicle, and is **not authorized** (owner or business acting in ordinary course).
  • **Penalty:** Base is State Jail Felony. Enhanced to Felony 3rd Degree if previous conviction, conspiracy to commit arson/theft, or **possessed a firearm**.

Theft of Trade Secrets (31.05)

  • **Offense:** Knowingly, without consent, steals a trade secret, makes a copy, or communicates/transmits a trade secret.
  • **Penalty:** Base is Felony 3rd Degree. Enhanced to Felony 2nd Degree if the actor **intended to benefit a foreign agent, foreign government, or foreign instrumentality**.
