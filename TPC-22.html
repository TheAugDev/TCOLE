Key Definitions (Ch. 22 & Related)

Selected definitions relevant to Chapter 22 offenses.

Bodily Injury (1.07)

Physical pain, illness, or any impairment of physical condition.

Serious Bodily Injury (1.07)

Bodily injury that creates a substantial risk of death or that causes death, serious permanent disfigurement, or protracted loss or impairment of the function of any bodily member or organ.

Deadly Weapon (1.07)

(A) A firearm or anything manifestly designed, made, or adapted for the purpose of inflicting death or serious bodily injury; or (B) anything that in the manner of its use or intended use is capable of causing death or serious bodily injury.

Public Servant (1.07)

Officer, employee, or agent of government; juror or grand juror; arbitrator or referee; attorney general candidate; person performing governmental function under claim of right.

Emergency Services Personnel (22.01)

Includes firefighters, EMS personnel, ER personnel, and others providing services during emergencies.

Hospital Personnel (22.01)

Includes nurses, physicians, PAs, maintenance/janitorial staff, receptionists, etc., employed by or working in a licensed hospital.

Security Officer (22.01, 22.02)

Commissioned security officer (Sec 1702.002 Occ Code) or noncommissioned security officer registered under Sec 1702.221 Occ Code.

Process Server (22.01, 22.02)

Meaning assigned by Sec 156.001, Government Code.

Sports Participant (22.01)

Person in official capacity in organized athletic competition (athlete, ref, coach, staff, etc.).

Child (22.011)

Person younger than 17 years of age.

Disabled Individual (22.021)

Person older than 13 who by reason of age or physical/mental issue is substantially unable to protect self or provide basic care.

Elderly Individual (22.04)

Person 65 years of age or older.

Child (22.04)

Person 14 years of age or younger.

Disabled Individual (22.04)

Person with specific listed conditions (autism, developmental disability, intellectual disability, severe emotional disturbance, TBI, mental illness) OR who by age/disease/defect/injury is substantially unable to protect self or provide basic care.

Abandon (22.041)

Leave a child/elderly/disabled individual in any place without providing reasonable/necessary care under circumstances where no reasonable person would leave them.

Utility (22.01)

Electric, telecommunications, cable/video, gas utility; pipeline; electric coop/municipally owned utility (as defined by Utilities Code).

Health Care Services Provider (22.011)

Physician, chiropractor, physical therapist, PA, RN/LVN/APN, massage therapist (licensed under Occ Code).

Mental Health Services Provider (22.011)

Licensed/unlicensed person performing/purporting MH services (social worker, chem dependency counselor, LPC, LMFT, clergy, psychologist, special officer for MH).

Employee of a facility (22.011)

Employee of facility under H&SC 250.001 or person providing services for compensation (incl. contract laborer).

Assisted Reproduction / Donor (22.011)

Meanings assigned by Family Code 160.102.

Human Reproductive Material (22.011)

Spermatozoon, ovum, or organism from fertilized ovum to embryo.

Consumer Product (22.09)

Any product offered for sale to or for consumption by the public (includes food/drugs per HSC 431.002).

Tamper (22.09)

Alter or add a foreign substance to a consumer product to make it probable that it will cause serious bodily injury.

Family/Household/Family Violence

Definitions assigned by Family Code Sections 71.003, 71.005, and 71.004 respectively.

Consent (Lack of - for 22.011/22.021)

Assault is without consent if actor uses force/violence/coercion; threatens force/violence/harm (victim believes actor can execute); victim unconscious/unable to resist (actor knows); victim incapable due to mental disease/defect (actor knows); victim unaware (actor knows); victim intoxicated/impaired & incapable (actor knows); threat against 3rd person; actor is public servant using coercion; actor is MH/HC provider exploiting dependency; actor is clergyman exploiting dependency; actor is facility employee & victim is resident (unless married); actor is HC provider using unconsented donor material in assisted reproduction; actor is coach/tutor exploiting dependency; actor is caregiver exploiting dependency. (See 22.011(b)).

Sec. 22.01. Assault

Three Ways to Commit (a):

  • (a)(1): Intentionally, knowingly, or recklessly causes bodily injury to another (incl. spouse).
  • (a)(2): Intentionally or knowingly threatens another with imminent bodily injury (incl. spouse).
  • (a)(3): Intentionally or knowingly causes physical contact when knows/should know the other will regard it as offensive or provocative.

Punishments:

  • (a)(1) - Bodily Injury:
    • Base: Class A Misdemeanor.
    • Enhanced to Felony of the Third Degree (F3) if against:
      • Public servant (on duty/retaliation) (b)(1)
      • Family member + prior FV conviction (from specific list: Ch19, 20.03, 20.04, 21.11, Ch22, 25.07(a)(1), 25.072 based on 25.07(a)(1), 25.11) (b)(2)(A)
      • Family member + impeding breathing/circulation (b)(2)(B)
      • Govt contractor/employee in specified facility (b)(3)
      • Security officer (on duty) (b)(4)
      • Emergency services personnel (providing services) (b)(5)
      • Process server (on duty) (b)(6)
      • Pregnant individual (to force abortion) (b)(7)
      • Pregnant individual (actor knows pregnant) (b)(8)
      • Hospital personnel (on hospital property) (b)(9)
      • Utility worker/agent (on duty) (b)(10)
      • Committed by civilly committed person against TCO employee/contractor (on duty/retaliation) (b-1)
      • Committed during Smuggling of Persons (fleeing LEO - 20.05(a)(2)) (b-4)
    • Enhanced to Felony of the Second Degree (F2) if against:
      • Peace officer or judge (on duty/retaliation) (b-2)
      • Family member + prior FV conviction (from specific list) + impeding breathing/circulation (b-3)
  • (a)(2) - Threat / (a)(3) - Offensive Contact:
    • Base: Class C Misdemeanor.
    • Enhanced to Class B Misdemeanor if (a)(2) or (a)(3) committed by non-participant against sports participant (on duty/retaliation) (c)(2).
    • Enhanced to Class A Misdemeanor if:
      • (a)(3) committed against elderly or disabled individual (c)(1).
      • (a)(2) or (a)(3) committed against pregnant individual to force abortion (c)(3).

Presumption (d, d-1):

Actor presumed to know victim was public servant, security officer, utility worker, emergency personnel, or TCO personnel/contractor if victim was wearing distinctive uniform/badge indicating status.

Prior Conviction Rule (f):

For FV enhancements (b)(2)(A) & (b-3)(2), "previously convicted" includes deferred adjudication, regardless of discharge. Out-of-state convictions count if elements are substantially similar.

Prosecution Rule (g):

Actor can be prosecuted under this section and/or other sections for the same conduct.

Sec. 22.02. Aggravated Assault

Offense (a): Commits Assault (22.01) AND:

  • Causes Serious Bodily Injury (SBI); OR
  • Uses or exhibits a deadly weapon during the assault.

Punishments:

  • Base: Felony of the Second Degree (F2).
  • Enhanced to Felony of the First Degree (F1) if:
    • Uses deadly weapon AND causes SBI to family member (b)(1)(A).
    • Uses deadly weapon AND causes traumatic brain/spine injury resulting in persistent vegetative state or irreversible paralysis (b)(1)(B).
    • Committed by public servant acting under color of the servant's office or employment (b)(2)(A).
    • Against a person the actor knows is a public servant while the public servant is lawfully discharging an official duty, or in retaliation or on account of an exercise of official power or performance of an official duty as a public servant (b)(2)(B).
    • In retaliation against or on account of the service of another as a witness, prospective witness, informant, or person who has reported the occurrence of a crime (b)(2)(C).
    • Against a person the actor knows is a process server while the person is performing a duty as a process server (b)(2)(D).
    • Against a person the actor knows is a security officer while the officer is performing a duty as a security officer (b)(2)(E).
    • Committed by an actor who is committed to a civil commitment facility, against specified TCO personnel or contractors (on duty/retaliation) (b)(2)(F).
    • Drive-by shooting: actor inside/en route to/from motor vehicle AND (A) knowingly discharges firearm at/toward habitation/bldg/vehicle, (B) reckless if occupied, AND (C) causes BI/property damage OR places person in fear of imminent SBI (b)(3).
    • The actor commits the assault as part of a mass shooting (b)(4).

Presumption (c, c-1):

Actor presumed to know victim was public servant, security officer, or TCO personnel/contractor if victim was wearing distinctive uniform/badge.

Sec. 22.011. Sexual Assault

Two Ways to Commit:

  • (a)(1): Intentionally or knowingly causes penetration of anus/sexual organ of another by any means, or penetration of mouth by actor's sexual organ, or causes contact/penetration involving sexual organs - all without consent.
  • (a)(2): Intentionally or knowingly causes penetration/contact involving anus/mouth/sexual organ of/by a child (< 17) (regardless of actor's knowledge of age). Consent is not an element here.

Lack of Consent (b):

Extensive list defining when assault is without consent (force, threat, unconscious, mental defect, unaware, intoxicated, public servant coercion, provider/clergyman/employee exploiting dependency, wrong donor material, coach/tutor exploiting dependency, caregiver exploiting dependency, etc.). See definitions tab or statute for full list.

Defenses:

  • (d) Defense to (a)(2): Conduct was medical care, no prohibited contact occurred.
  • (e) Affirmative Defenses to (a)(2): (1) Actor was spouse; OR (2) "Romeo & Juliet": Actor <= 3 yrs older, not registered, victim >= 14, and not prohibited relationship (incest under 25.01/25.02).

Punishments (f):

  • Base: Felony of the Second Degree (F2).
  • Enhanced to Felony of the First Degree (F1) if victim was person actor prohibited from marrying/living with/having sex with under Ch 25 (Incest). (f)(1).
  • Reduced to State Jail Felony (SJF) if under (a)(1) + (b)(12) (wrong donor material). (f)(2).

Sec. 22.021. Aggravated Sexual Assault

Offense (a): Commits Sexual Assault (conduct from 22.011(a)(1) or (a)(2)) AND one of the following aggravating factors exists:

  • (a)(2)(A): During same criminal episode, actor:
    • (i) Causes SBI or attempts to cause death.
    • (ii) By acts/words, places victim in fear of imminent Trafficking (sex under 20A.02(a)(3,4,7,8)), death, SBI, or kidnapping.
    • (iii) By acts/words in victim's presence, threatens Trafficking (sex under 20A.02(a)(3,4,7,8)), death, SBI, or kidnapping of *any* person.
    • (iv) Uses or exhibits a deadly weapon.
    • (v) Acts in concert with another who commits SA against same victim in same episode.
    • (vi) Administers impairing substance to facilitate.
  • (a)(2)(B): Victim is younger than 14 years old (regardless of actor's knowledge).
  • (a)(2)(C): Victim is an elderly individual or a disabled individual.

Other Rules:

  • Consent (c): Uses same lack-of-consent definitions as 22.011(b).
  • Defense (d): Medical care defense from 22.011(d) applies.
  • Punishment (e): Felony of the First Degree (F1).
  • Minimum Term Increased (f): Minimum term becomes 25 years if: (1) victim < 10 yrs old; OR (2) victim < 14 yrs old AND actor committed offense using an (a)(2)(A) manner (SBI, threat, weapon, etc.).

Sec. 22.012. Indecent Assault

Offense (a): Without consent and with intent to arouse/gratify, person:

  • Touches anus, breast, or genitals of another.
  • Touches another with anus, breast, or genitals of any person.
  • Exposes or attempts to expose another's genitals, pubic area, anus, buttocks, or female areola.
  • Causes another to contact blood, seminal fluid, vaginal fluid, saliva, urine, or feces of any person.

Punishments (b):

  • Base: Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhanced to State Jail Felony (SJF) if:
    • Prior conviction under this section (not HCS provider type); OR
    • Actor is HCS/MHS provider, act committed during treatment, AND beyond scope of accepted practice.
  • Enhanced to Felony of the Third Degree (F3) if:
    • Prior conviction under the HCS/MHS provider enhancement (b)(1)(B).
    • (Per SB 1610) Committed by actor in civil commitment facility against TCO employee/contractor.
  • Enhanced to Felony of the Second Degree (F2) if:
    • (Per HB 2593) Victim is disabled individual or elderly individual.

Prosecution Rule (c):

Actor can be prosecuted under this section and/or other sections for the same conduct.

Sec. 22.04. Injury to a Child, Elderly Individual, or Disabled Individual

Offense (a): Intentionally, knowingly, recklessly, or with criminal negligence (by act) OR intentionally, knowingly, or recklessly (by omission) causes SBI, serious mental injury, or BI to a child (< 14), elderly individual (65+), or disabled individual.

Offense (a-1): Owner/operator/employee of specified facility (group home, nursing home, ALF, boarding home, ICF, etc.) Intentionally, knowingly, recklessly, or with criminal negligence by omission causes SBI, serious mental injury, or BI to a resident C/E/D.

Omission Liability (b): Omission is offense if actor has (1) legal/statutory duty OR (2) assumed care, custody, or control.

Assumption of Care (d): Includes acting so reasonable person would conclude actor accepted responsibility. Facility owners/operators/employees are considered to have accepted responsibility for residents.

Punishments:

  • SBI / Serious Mental Injury (a)(1)/(2) or (a-1)(1)/(2):
    • Intentionally/Knowingly: F1.
    • Recklessly: F2.
  • Bodily Injury (a)(3) or (a-1)(3):
    • Intentionally/Knowingly: F3 (Enhanced to F2 if I/K, victim is disabled resident of certain facilities (HSC 555.001 center or Ch 252 licensed facility), and actor is employee providing direct care).
    • Recklessly: SJF.
  • Criminal Negligence (a) or (a-1 by omission): SJF.

Defenses / Affirmative Defenses:

  • (i) Affirmative Defense (Omission): Actor notified victim in person AND parents/other in writing (or notified DFPS in writing) they would no longer provide care before offense.
  • (k) Defense: Reasonable medical care by/under physician OR emergency care in good faith.
  • (l) Affirmative Defenses: (1) Religious healing; (2) Omission victim of FV by co-defendant + no prior knowledge of injury + didn't cause injury + couldn't prevent; (3) Actor <= 3 yrs older than child victim.
  • (m) Affirmative Defense: Actor didn't know/couldn't reasonably know victim was disabled.

Prosecution Rule (h):

Actor can be prosecuted under this section and/or others. If sentences assessed under both for same episode, they run concurrently. Sec 3.04 (consolidation/joinder) does not apply.

Sec. 22.041. Abandoning or Endangering...

Offense (b) - Abandoning: Having custody/care/control, intentionally abandons child (< 14), elderly (65+), or disabled individual in place exposing them to unreasonable risk of harm.

Offense (c) - Endangering: Intentionally, knowingly, recklessly, or with criminal negligence, by act or omission, engages in conduct placing C/E/D in imminent danger of death, BI, or impairment.

Presumption of Endangerment (c-1): Presumed if actor (1) makes/possesses/uses Meth or PG1-B drug in presence of C/E/D; (2) Meth/PG1-B is accessible AND found in C/E/D's system; or (3) unlawfully injects/ingests/etc. PG1 or PG1-B drug.

Punishments:

  • Abandoning (b):
    • With intent to return: SJF. (d)(1)
    • Without intent to return: F3. (d)(2)
    • Under circumstances reasonable person believes place C/E/D in imminent danger of death/BI/impairment: F2. (e)
  • Endangering (c): SJF. (f)

Defense / Exception:

  • (g) Defense to (c) Endangering: Act/omission enabled participation in organized athletic event with appropriate safety measures.
  • (h) Exception to Abandoning/Endangering CHILD: Actor voluntarily delivered child to designated emergency infant care provider (Baby Moses Law).

Sec. 22.05. Deadly Conduct

  • Offense (a): Recklessly engages in conduct that places another in imminent danger of SBI.
    • Punishment: Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Offense (b): Knowingly discharges a firearm at or in the direction of: (1) one or more individuals; OR (2) a habitation, building, or vehicle AND is reckless as to whether it's occupied.
    • Punishment: Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Presumption (c): Recklessness and danger presumed if actor knowingly pointed firearm at/toward another (whether loaded or not). Does not apply to LEO on duty.
  • LEO Exception (f): Subsection (b)(1) (shooting AT individual) does not apply if LEO was on duty AND reasonably believed discharge was justified under Ch 9.

Sec. 22.07. Terroristic Threat

Offense (a): Threatens to commit any offense involving violence to person or property with specific intent to:

  • Cause reaction by emergency agency.
  • Place person in fear of imminent SBI.
  • Prevent/interrupt use of building, public place, conveyance, etc.
  • Impair/interrupt public communications, utilities, transport.
  • Place public/substantial group in fear of SBI.
  • Influence government conduct.

Punishments:

  • (a)(1) Emergency Reaction: Class B Misdemeanor. (b)
  • (a)(2) Fear of Imminent SBI: Class B Misdemeanor. (c)
    • Enhanced to Class A Misdemeanor if against family/household member (FV) or public servant. (c)
    • Enhanced to State Jail Felony if against peace officer or judge. (c-1)
  • (a)(3) Prevent/Interrupt Use: Class A Misdemeanor. (d)
    • Enhanced to State Jail Felony if causes pecuniary loss >= $1,500. (d)
  • (a)(4) Impair Public Service, (a)(5) Place Public in Fear, or (a)(6) Influence Govt: Felony of the Third Degree. (e)

Pecuniary Loss (g):

For (a)(3) enhancement, loss is economic loss suffered by owner due to interruption.

Sec. 22.08. Aiding Suicide

  • Offense (a): With intent to promote/assist suicide by another, aids or attempts to aid the other to commit or attempt suicide.
  • Punishment (b): Class C Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (b): State Jail Felony if actor's conduct causes suicide or attempted suicide resulting in SBI.

Sec. 22.09. Tampering With Consumer Product

  • Offense (b) - Tampering: Knowingly/intentionally tampers (alters/adds substance likely to cause SBI) with consumer product knowing it will be offered for sale/gift.
    • Punishment: F2. Enhanced to F1 if SBI results.
  • Offense (c) - Threat: Knowingly/intentionally threatens to tamper with intent to cause fear, affect sale, or cause BI.
    • Punishment: F3.

Sec. 22.10. Leaving a Child in a Vehicle

  • Offense (a): Intentionally or knowingly leaves a child in motor vehicle for longer than 5 minutes, knowing child is (1) younger than 7 AND (2) not attended by someone 14 or older.
  • Punishment (b): Class C Misdemeanor.

Sec. 22.11. Harassment by Persons in Certain Facilities...

Offense (a): With intent to assault, harass, annoy, alarm, abuse, torment, or embarrass:

  • While imprisoned/confined in correctional/detention facility, causes another person to contact blood, seminal fluid, vaginal fluid, saliva, urine, or feces of actor/other/animal, or any other fluid/liquid.
  • While committed to civil commitment facility, causes TCO employee/contractor to contact specified fluids (on duty/retaliation).
  • Causes person actor knows is public servant to contact specified fluids (on duty/retaliation).

Punishment (b): Felony of the Third Degree.

Definition (d): "Correctional or detention facility" includes secure juvenile facilities.

No Defense (g): Not a defense that actor warned the person.

Presumptions (e, f): Actor presumed to know victim status if distinctive uniform/badge worn.

Prosecution Rule (c): Actor may be prosecuted under this section and/or another section.

Sec. 22.06. Consent as Defense to Assaultive Conduct

  • Defense (a): Victim's effective consent (or actor's reasonable belief victim consented) is a defense to 22.01 (Assault), 22.02 (Agg Assault), or 22.05 (Deadly Conduct) IF:
    • (1) Conduct did NOT threaten or inflict SBI; OR
    • (2) Victim knew conduct was a risk of (A) occupation, (B) medical treatment, or (C) scientific experiment.
  • Limitation (b): Defense is NOT available if conduct committed as condition of initiation/membership in a criminal street gang.

Sec. 22.12. Applicability to Certain Conduct (Unborn Child)

Chapter 22 does not apply to conduct against an unborn child if the conduct is:

  • Committed by the mother.
  • Lawful medical procedure by provider w/ consent.
  • Lawful medical procedure for assisted reproduction w/ consent.
  • Dispensation/administration of drug in accordance with law.
