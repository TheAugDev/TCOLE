Ch. 46: Weapons

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 46.01)

Note: Some terms (Illegal Knife, Knuckles, Short-Barrel Firearm, Switchblade Knife) have been repealed from this section over time.

Club

Instrument specially designed, made, or adapted for inflicting SBI/death by striking. Includes blackjack, nightstick, mace, tomahawk.

Explosive Weapon

Explosive/incendiary bomb, grenade, rocket, mine designed for SBI/death/property damage OR undue public alarm/terror; includes delivery devices.

Firearm

Device designed/made/adapted to expel projectile via explosion/burning, or readily convertible. Excludes pre-1899 antique/curio or replica not using rim/center fire ammo.

Handgun

A firearm designed, made, or adapted to be fired with one hand.

Location-Restricted Knife

A knife with a blade over 5.5 inches.

Knife

Bladed hand instrument capable of inflicting SBI or death by cutting or stabbing.

Machine Gun

Firearm capable of shooting >2 shots automatically by single trigger function without manual reloading.

Armor-Piercing Ammunition

Handgun ammo designed primarily to penetrate metal/body armor, used principally in pistols/revolvers.

Hoax Bomb

Device that reasonably appears to be explosive/incendiary OR causes alarm/reaction by public safety officials.

Chemical Dispensing Device

Device (other than small commercial personal protection one) designed/made/adapted to dispense substance causing adverse psychological/physiological effect.

Racetrack

Meaning per Occupations Code 2021.003(41).

Zip Gun

Device not originally a firearm, adapted to expel projectile via explosion/burning substance through a barrel.

Tire Deflation Device

Device (caltrop, spike strip) that impedes/stops vehicle by puncturing tires. Excludes certain one-way traffic control devices with clearly visible warning signs.

Volunteer Emergency Services Personnel

Includes volunteer firefighter, EMS volunteer, etc., providing services during emergencies. Excludes LEOs performing LE duties.

Improvised Explosive Device (IED)

Completed, operational bomb designed for SBI/death/property damage, fabricated in improvised manner w/ nonmilitary components. Excludes legally purchasable unassembled components & exploding target kits.

First Responder

Public safety employee (fire, EMS) whose duties include rapidly responding to emergencies. Excludes volunteers & LEOs performing LE duties.

Sec. 46.02. Unlawful Carrying Weapons

  • Offense (a): I/K/R carries handgun AND is < 21 OR convicted of Assault-BI (22.01a1), Terroristic Threat (22.07), Deadly Conduct (22.05), or Disorderly Conduct-Firearm/Display (42.01a7/8) in past 5 yrs; AND is NOT on own premises/control OR inside/en route to own vehicle/watercraft.
    • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Offense (a-1): I/K/R carries handgun in own/controlled vehicle/watercraft AND (1) handgun is in plain view (unless person is 21+ OR LTC holder *and* handgun is carried in a holster); OR (2) person is engaged in criminal activity (> Class C traffic/boating) OR prohibited by law from possessing firearm.
    • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Offense (a-4): I/K/R carries location-restricted knife (blade > 5.5") AND is < 18; AND is NOT on own premises/control, inside/en route to own vehicle/watercraft, OR under direct supervision of parent/guardian.
    • Penalty (d): Class C Misdemeanor.
  • Offense (a-5): Carries a handgun and intentionally displays it in plain view of another person in a public place.
    • Exception: Handgun was partially/wholly visible but was carried in a holster.
    • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Offense (a-6): Carries a handgun while the person is intoxicated; AND is NOT on own property/property under control OR private property w/ owner's consent OR inside/en route to own/controlled vehicle/watercraft OR vehicle/watercraft w/ owner/operator consent.
    • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Offense (a-7): I/K/R carries handgun AND at time of offense, was prohibited from possessing firearm under 46.04(a) [Felon], (b) [FV Misd], or (c) [Protective Order]; AND is NOT on own premises/control OR inside/en route to own vehicle/watercraft.
    • Penalty (e)(1): Felony of the Second Degree (min 5 yrs confinement) if prohibited under 46.04(a).
    • Penalty (e)(2): Felony of the Third Degree if prohibited under 46.04(b) or (c).
  • Definitions:
    • "Premises" (a-2): Includes real property and a recreational vehicle used as living quarters (temp or perm).
    • "Watercraft" (a-3): Any boat, motorboat, vessel, PWC (other than seaplane) used for transport on water.
  • Note (a-8): Conduct under (a-7) can be prosecuted under (a-7) AND/OR other provisions of law.

Sec. 46.03. Places Weapons Prohibited

Applies to: Firearm, Location-Restricted Knife, Club, or Prohibited Weapon (46.05a)

  • Offense (a): I/K/R possesses or goes with listed weapon on/in:
    1. School/Postsecondary Institution premises/grounds/vehicle. Exceptions: written authorization; LTC holder concealed on postsecondary premises (unless 30.06/campus rules apply).
    2. Polling place premises (election day / early voting).
    3. Government court/offices premises (unless authorized).
    4. Racetrack premises.
    5. Secured area of an airport ("Secured area" definition in (c)(5)).
    6. Within 1,000 ft of TDCJ execution premises on execution day (if notice given). Exceptions: vehicle on road, residence/employment (i).
    7. Premises of business deriving 51%+ income from alcohol for on-premises consumption (TABC 51% sign). Exception: LTC holder if no effective 30.06/30.07 (p).
    8. Premises of high school/collegiate/pro sporting or interscholastic event (unless participant using weapon in event). Exception: LTC holder at collegiate event if no effective 30.06/30.07 (q).
    9. Correctional facility premises.
    10. Civil commitment facility premises.
    11. Hospital/Nursing facility premises (unless written admin authorization). Exception: LTC holder if no effective 30.06/30.07 (p).
    12. Mental hospital premises (unless written admin authorization).
    13. Amusement park ("Amusement park" definition in (c)(1)). Exception: LTC holder if no effective 30.06/30.07 (p).
    14. Room(s) where open meeting of governmental entity is held (if notice under Ch 551 Gov Code given).
  • Offense (a-2): LTC holder carries partially/wholly visible handgun (holstered or not) and I/K displays it in plain view on premises or public/private drive/street/walk/parking area of an institution of higher education.
    • Penalty (g-2): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Offense (a-3): LTC holder carries handgun (concealed or not) on campus of private/independent higher ed institution that prohibits carry (per 411.2031e Gov Code) and gives effective 30.06 notice.
    • Penalty (g-2): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Offense (a-4): LTC holder intentionally carries concealed handgun in prohibited portion of public higher ed campus (per 411.2031d-1 Gov Code) where effective 30.06 notice is given.
    • Penalty (g-2): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Base Penalty (g): Felony of the Third Degree (unless otherwise specified).
  • Penalty (g-1): If weapon is location-restricted knife: Class C Misdemeanor, *unless* on school premises (a)(1), then it's Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Penalty (g-2): Offenses under (a)(8) [Sporting Event], (a)(10) [Civil Commit], (a)(11) [Hospital/Nursing], (a)(13) [Amusement Park], (a-2) [Display @ Higher Ed], (a-3) [Carry @ Prohibiting Private Higher Ed], (a-4) [Concealed @ Prohibited Higher Ed Zone] are Class A Misdemeanors.
  • Defenses:
    • (b) Official duties defense for armed forces/guards/officer of court (for a1-a4).
    • (d) Official duties/travel defense for armed forces/guards/security officers in airport secured area (a5).
    • (e) Checked firearm as baggage or federal/airport authorization in airport secured area (a5).
    • (e-1) Defense for LTC holder at airport checkpoint who exits immediately after notification (a5). No arrest unless opportunity to exit given (e-2).
    • (h) Official duties/travel defense for security officer at racetrack (a4).
    • (m) Defense if actor received personal notice to leave and promptly departed (does not apply if sign under (o) was posted or actor already knew).
  • "Premises" (c)(4): Building/portion; excludes driveways, streets, sidewalks, parking areas.
  • Notice Sign (o): Specifies requirements for sign giving notice under 46.03 (language, English/Spanish, 1" letters, conspicuous).

Sec. 46.04. Unlawful Possession of Firearm

  • Offense (a) - Felon: Person convicted of felony possesses firearm: (1) After conviction & before 5th anniversary of release from confinement OR supervision (whichever later); OR (2) After that 5-year period, at any location *other than the premises at which the person lives*.
    • Penalty (e): Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Offense (a-1) - Gang Member: Member of criminal street gang (Ch 71 PC) I/K/R carries handgun in motor vehicle or watercraft.
    • Penalty (e): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Offense (b) - FV Misdemeanor: Person convicted of Assault (Class A) involving family/household member possesses firearm before 5th anniversary of release from confinement OR supervision (whichever later).
    • Penalty (e): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Offense (c) - Protective Order: Person subject to qualifying protective order (Family Code, CCP) possesses firearm after receiving notice of order & before order expires. (Excludes non-LEO gov employees on duty).
    • Penalty (e): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • "Felony" Definition (f/g): Broadly includes TX felonies, offenses with same elements, or offenses punishable by 1+ year in penitentiary, UNLESS at time of possession, the offense is not designated a felony in TX AND does not contain all elements of a TX felony.

Sec. 46.041. Unlawful Possession of Metal or Body Armor by Felon

  • "Metal or Body Armor" (a): Body covering manifestly designed, made, or adapted to protect against gunfire.
  • Offense (b): Person convicted of felony possesses metal or body armor after the conviction.
  • Penalty (c): Felony of the Third Degree.

Sec. 46.05. Prohibited Weapons

Note: Short-barrel firearms (repealed effective 9/1/2025), firearm silencers, switchblades, and knuckles are no longer listed as prohibited weapons under this specific statute.

  • Offense (a): I/K possesses, manufactures, transports, repairs, or sells:
    1. Explosive weapon OR Machine gun (UNLESS registered in NFA record / not subject to registration / curio or relic).
    2. Armor-piercing ammunition.
    3. Chemical dispensing device.
    4. Zip gun.
    5. Tire deflation device.
    6. Improvised explosive device (IED).
  • Penalties (e):
    • (a)(5) Tire Deflation Device: State Jail Felony.
    • All others listed in (a): Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Defenses:
    • (b) Defense: Incidental to official duty by armed forces/Nat Guard, gov LE agency, or correctional facility.
    • (d) Affirmative Defense: Incidental to dealing w/ tire deflation device as antique/curio; OR making armor-piercing ammo / tire deflation device available solely to orgs listed in (b).
    • (f) Defense: Possession of chemical dispensing device by security officer w/ TCOLE or PSB-approved training.

Sec. 46.06. Unlawful Transfer of Certain Weapons

  • Offenses (a): Person commits offense if they:
    1. Sell/rent/lease/loan/give handgun knowing recipient intends unlawful use/act.
    2. I/K sell/rent/lease/give/offer firearm, club, or location-restricted knife to child < 18.
    3. I/K/R sell firearm or ammo to intoxicated person ("Intoxicated" def in (b)(1)).
    4. Knowingly sell firearm or ammo to felon before 5th anniversary of release (from confinement or supervision, whichever later).
    5. Sell/rent/lease/loan/give handgun knowing recipient is subject to active protective order ("Active PO" def in (b)(2), excludes temp orders before hearing).
    6. Knowingly purchase/rent/lease/receive as loan/gift handgun while subject to active protective order.
    7. While prohibited from possessing firearm (state/fed law), knowingly make material false statement on required form submitted to licensed firearms dealer.
  • Penalties (d):
    • Base Offense: Class A Misdemeanor.
    • (a)(2) Transfer to Minor: State Jail Felony IF weapon is a handgun. (Otherwise MA)
    • (a)(7) False Statement on Form: State Jail Felony.
  • Affirmative Defense (c): For (a)(2 - transfer to minor), parent/legal custodian gave written permission (sale) or effective consent (other transfer).

Sec. 46.07. Interstate Purchase

  • Texas residents may purchase firearms, ammo, reloading components, or accessories in another state if not otherwise precluded by law (conforms to federal law).

Sec. 46.08. Hoax Bombs

  • Offense (a): Knowingly manufactures, sells, purchases, transports, or possesses a hoax bomb with intent to: (1) Make another believe it's an explosive/incendiary device; OR (2) Cause alarm or reaction by public safety/emergency agency official.
  • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 46.09. Components of Explosives

  • Offense (a): Knowingly possesses components of an explosive weapon with the intent to combine the components into an explosive weapon for use in a criminal endeavor.
  • Penalty (b): Felony of the Third Degree.

Sec. 46.10. Deadly Weapon in Penal Institution

  • Offense (a): While confined in a penal institution, intentionally, knowingly, or recklessly: (1) Carries on or about his person a deadly weapon; OR (2) Possesses or conceals a deadly weapon in the penal institution.
  • Affirmative Defense (b): At the time of the offense, the actor was engaged in conduct authorized by an employee of the penal institution.
  • Prosecution Note (c): Can be prosecuted under this section AND/OR another section in Ch 46.
  • Penalty (d): Felony of the Third Degree.

Sec. 46.13. Making a Firearm Accessible to a Child

  • Definitions (a):
    • "Child": Person younger than 17 years of age.
    • "Readily dischargeable firearm": Firearm loaded with ammunition (round in chamber not required).
    • "Secure": To take steps a reasonable person would take to prevent access by child (e.g., locked container, trigger lock).
  • Offense (b): A child (< 17) gains access to a readily dischargeable firearm AND the person, with criminal negligence: (1) Failed to secure the firearm; OR (2) Left the firearm in a place to which the person knew or should have known the child would gain access.
  • Penalties (d, e):
    • Base Offense: Class C Misdemeanor.
    • Enhanced Offense: Class A Misdemeanor IF the child discharges the firearm and causes death or serious bodily injury to himself or another person.
  • Affirmative Defense (c): Child's access was: (1) Supervised by person >18 for hunting, sporting, other lawful purpose; (2) Lawful defense by child of people/property; (3) Gained by entering property illegally; OR (4) Occurred during actor's agricultural enterprise.
  • Arrest Delay (f): Peace officer may not arrest actor before 7th day after offense if actor is family member AND child caused own death/SBI.
  • Dealer Sign Requirement (g): Firearms dealers must post conspicuous warning sign about unlawful storage where children can access (specific wording provided).

Sec. 46.14. Firearm Smuggling

  • Offense (a): Person knowingly engages in the business of transporting or transferring a firearm that the person knows was acquired illegally (state or federal law). "Engages in business" means conduct on >1 occasion OR for profit/remuneration.
  • Penalties (b):
    • Base Offense: Felony of the Third Degree.
    • Enhanced Offense: Felony of the Second Degree IF committed with respect to 3 or more firearms in a single criminal episode.
  • Exception (c): Does not apply to peace officer engaged in actual discharge of official duty.
  • Prosecution Note (d): Can be prosecuted under this section AND/OR other applicable laws.

Sec. 46.11. Penalty if Offense Committed Within Weapon-Free School Zone

  • Enhancement (a): Punishment for an offense under Ch. 46 is increased to the punishment prescribed for the next highest category of offense IF it's shown actor committed the offense in a place the actor *knew* was: (1) Within 300 feet of the premises of a school; OR (2) On premises where an official school function OR a UIL sanctioned/sponsored event is taking place.
  • Exception (b): This enhancement does *not* apply to an offense under Sec. 46.03(a)(1) (Possession *on* school premises - which is already an F3).
  • Definitions (c):
    • "Premises": Meaning per HSC 481.134.
    • "School": Private or public elementary or secondary school.

Sec. 46.12. Maps as Evidence of Location or Area

  • (a) In prosecution enhanced under 46.11, a map produced by municipal/county engineer showing weapon-free zone boundaries is admissible & prima facie evidence of boundaries IF governing body adopted resolution/ordinance approving map.
  • (b) Engineer may revise map on request.
  • (c) Approved/revised maps must be filed with county clerk(s).
  • (d) Prosecution not prevented from using other evidence/testimony or other admissible maps/diagrams.

Sec. 46.15. Nonapplicability

Note: This section lists numerous exemptions. Key examples highlighted below. Check statute for full list and details.

  • (a) Sections 46.02 (UCW) & 46.03 (Places Prohibited) do NOT apply to:
    • Peace officers / Special investigators (carrying weapon in state, regardless of on/off duty).
    • Parole officers (on duty & compliant w/ TDCJ policy).
    • CSCD officers (on duty & authorized by 76.0051 Gov Code).
    • Active/Retired Judicial officers (defined in 411.201 Gov Code) w/ LTC.
    • Honorably retired LEOs (fed definition 18 USC 926C, w/ proficiency cert & agency ID).
    • Certain prosecutors w/ LTC (AG, US Atty, DA, CA, Muni Atty).
    • Certain assistant prosecutors w/ LTC.
    • Designated bailiffs w/ LTC escorting judicial officer.
    • Juvenile probation officers authorized by 142.006 HRC.
    • Volunteer emergency services personnel w/ LTC while providing services.
    • Certain retired judges/justices w/ LTC.
    • District/County Clerks w/ LTC.
  • (b) Sections 46.02 (UCW), 46.03(a)(14) (Gov Meeting), & 46.04(a-1) (Gang Member UCW) do NOT apply to:
    • Members of armed forces/state military/penal guards on duty.
    • Persons traveling.
    • Persons engaged in lawful hunting, fishing, other sporting activity (on premises or en route between premises & residence/vehicle/watercraft, if weapon type commonly used).
    • Commissioned security officers (on duty or traveling to/from assignment, wearing uniform, weapon in plain view).
    • Personal Protection Officers (PPOs) (on duty or traveling; either uniform w/ weapon visible OR non-uniform w/ weapon concealed).
    • Person carrying LTC and handgun (either concealed OR in a holster).
    • Alcohol permit/license holder/employee supervising premises.
    • LE students in class activity (on immediate premises or en route between premises/residence, carrying weapon unloaded).
  • (d) 46.02 does not apply to: Public security officers employed by Adjutant General (Sec 437.053 Gov Code) on duty or traveling.
  • (e) 46.02(a-4) (Minor w/ Loc-Restr Knife) does not apply to: Individual carrying knife in historical demonstration or significant ceremony.
  • (f) 46.03(a)(6) (Execution Site) does not apply to: Armed forces/military/penal employee on official duty.
  • (g)/(h) 46.03 (Club prohibition) does not apply to: Certified Animal Control Officers / Registered Code Enforcement Officers carrying animal bite deterrent instrument on duty/traveling.
  • (j) 46.02 & parts of 46.03 do not apply to: Individual carrying handgun in historical reenactment per TABC rules.
  • (k) 46.02 does not apply to: Person carrying handgun while evacuating/reentering disaster area (w/in 168 hrs of declaration, unless extended), if not prohibited from possessing.
  • (l) 46.02 & parts of 46.03 do not apply to: Person carrying handgun on premises of emergency shelter during disaster, IF authorized by owner/operator, complies w/ rules, AND not prohibited from possessing.
  • (m) Defense to 46.03: Actor promptly departed after receiving personal notice that carry was prohibited (does not apply if sign posted or actor knew).
  • (p) LTC Holders & Notice: 46.03(a)(7)[51%], (a)(11)[Hosp/Nursing], (a)(13)[Amuse Park] do *not* apply if LTC holder was not given effective 30.06/30.07/411.204 notice.
  • (q) LTC Holders & Notice: 46.03(a)(8)[Collegiate Sport] does *not* apply if LTC holder was not given effective 30.06/30.07 notice.
  • (r)/(s) First Responders / Tactical Medical Professionals: 46.02 & 46.03 do not apply if carrying handgun (concealed/holstered), certified, on duty, and employed/supervised by entity under Ch 179 LGC (for first responders).
