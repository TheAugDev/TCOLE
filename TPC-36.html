Ch. 36: Bribery and Corrupt Influence

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 36.01)

(1) Custody

(A) Detained or under arrest by a peace officer; or (B) Under restraint by a public servant pursuant to a court order.

(2) Party Official

A person who holds any position or office in a political party (by election, appointment, or employment).

(3) Benefit

Anything reasonably regarded as pecuniary gain or pecuniary advantage, including benefit to another person in whose welfare the beneficiary has a direct and substantial interest.

(4) Vote

To cast a ballot in an election regulated by law.

Sec. 36.02. Bribery

Offense

(a) A person commits an offense if he intentionally or knowingly offers, confers, or agrees to confer on another, OR solicits, accepts, or agrees to accept from another:

  • (1) Any benefit as consideration for the recipient's decision, opinion, recommendation, vote, or other exercise of discretion as a public servant, party official, or voter.
  • (2) Any benefit as consideration for the recipient's decision, vote, recommendation, or other exercise of official discretion in a judicial or administrative proceeding.
  • (3) Any benefit as consideration for a violation of a duty imposed by law on a public servant or party official.
  • (4) Any benefit that is a political contribution IF it was pursuant to an express agreement to take/withhold a specific official action. (Requires direct evidence).

Defenses & Exceptions

  • (b) It is NO DEFENSE that the person sought to be influenced was not qualified to act (not yet in office, lacked jurisdiction, etc.).
  • (c) It is NO DEFENSE that the benefit was not offered/accepted until AFTER the official act or after the person ceased to be a public servant.
  • (d) It is an EXCEPTION to (a)(1), (2), and (3) that the benefit was a political contribution (as defined by law). Note: This does not apply to (a)(4) which deals with contributions given under an "express agreement."

Penalty

(e) An offense under this section is a Felony of the Second Degree.

Sec. 36.03. Coercion of Public Servant or Voter

Offense

(a) A person commits an offense if by means of coercion he:

  • (1) Influences or attempts to influence a public servant in a specific exercise of official power or duty, or to violate a known legal duty; OR
  • (2) Influences or attempts to influence a voter not to vote or to vote in a particular manner.

Penalty

(b) An offense is a Class A Misdemeanor, UNLESS the coercion is a threat to commit a felony, in which event it is a Felony of the Third Degree.

Exception

(c) It is an exception to (a)(1) if the person is a member of the governing body of a governmental entity and the action is an official action (like deliberations).

Sec. 36.04. Improper Influence

Offense

(a) A person commits an offense if he privately addresses a representation, entreaty, argument, or other communication to any public servant who exercises discretion in an adjudicatory proceeding with intent to influence the outcome on the basis of considerations other than those authorized by law.

Definition

(b) "Adjudicatory proceeding" means any proceeding before a court or agency in which the legal rights, powers, duties, or privileges of specified parties are determined.

Penalty

(c) An offense under this section is a Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 36.05. Tampering With Witness

Offense

(a) A person commits an offense if, with intent to influence the witness, he offers/confers a benefit on OR coerces a witness/prospective witness to:

  • Testify falsely;
  • Withhold testimony, information, or documents;
  • Elude legal process;
  • Absent himself from an official proceeding; or
  • Abstain from, discontinue, or delay the prosecution of another.

(b) A witness/prospective witness commits an offense if he knowingly solicits, accepts, or agrees to accept any benefit for doing any of the things in (a).

Defense

(c) It is a defense to (a)(5) (delaying prosecution) that the benefit was reasonable restitution and was negotiated with the assistance or acquiescence of an attorney for the state.

Penalty

(d) An offense is a Felony of the Third Degree, UNLESS the official proceeding is a criminal case, then it is the same category as the most serious offense charged.

  • (e) Notwithstanding (d), if the most serious offense is a Capital Felony, tampering is a Felony of the First Degree.
  • (e-1) If the proceeding involves Family Violence, the offense is the greater of F3 or the most serious offense charged.
  • (e-2) If it involves Family Violence AND the actor has a prior FV conviction, it is the greater of F2 or the most serious offense charged.

Sec. 36.06. Obstruction or Retaliation

Offense

(a) A person commits an offense if he intentionally or knowingly harms or threatens to harm another by an unlawful act:

  • (1) In retaliation for or on account of the service/status of another as a public servant, witness, or informant (or person reporting a crime).
  • (2) To prevent or delay the service of another as a public servant, witness, or informant (or person reporting a crime).

(a-1) Commits an offense if posts residence address/phone number of a public servant on a public website with intent to cause harm or threat of harm in retaliation.

Definitions

(b) "Public servant" also includes an honorably retired peace officer and a person contracting to work in a civil commitment facility.

Penalty

(c) An offense is a Felony of the Third Degree, UNLESS:

  • (1) The victim was a juror, then it is a Felony of the Second Degree.
  • (2) The offense was under (a-1) (posting info) AND results in bodily injury, then it is a Felony of the Second Degree.

Sec. 36.07. Acceptance of Honorarium

(a) A public servant commits an offense if he solicits, accepts, or agrees to accept an honorarium in consideration for services that the public servant would not have been requested to provide but for the public servant's official position or duties.

(b) Does NOT prohibit accepting transportation, lodging, and meals in connection with a conference or similar event where the services are more than merely perfunctory.

(c) Penalty: Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 36.08. Gift to Public Servant...

A public servant commits an offense if he solicits, accepts, or agrees to accept any benefit from a person the public servant knows is subject to his jurisdiction, regulation, custody, or involved in transactions/litigation/matters before him. This section is broken down by the public servant's specific role:

  • (a) Regulatory/Inspection: from person subject to regulation/inspection.
  • (b) Custody of Prisoners: from person in custody.
  • (c) Litigation: from person whom litigation is pending/contemplated.
  • (d) Contracts/Purchases: from person interested in a contract/purchase.
  • (e) Judicial/Administrative Authority: from person interested in a matter before the tribunal.
  • (f) Legislator/Governor/Lt. Governor: from any person.

(h) Penalty: Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 36.09. Offering Gift to Public Servant

(a) A person commits an offense if he offers, confers, or agrees to confer any benefit on a public servant that he knows the public servant is prohibited by law from accepting.

(b) Penalty: Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 36.10. Non-Applicable

Sections 36.08 (Gift) and 36.09 (Offering Gift) DO NOT APPLY to:

  • (1) A lawfully entitled fee or benefit.
  • (2) A gift based on kinship or a personal/business relationship independent of official status.
  • (3) A benefit for a function in honor/appreciation, if properly reported.
  • (4) A political contribution.
  • (6) An item with a value of less than $50 (excluding cash).
  • (b) 36.08 (Gift) does not apply to food, lodging, transportation, or entertainment accepted as a guest and properly reported.
