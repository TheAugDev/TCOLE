Ch. 49: Intoxication & Alcoholic Beverage Offenses

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 49.01)

Alcohol concentration

Grams of alcohol per: (A) 210L of breath; (B) 100mL of blood; or (C) 67mL of urine.

Intoxicated

(A) Not having normal use of mental/physical faculties due to alcohol, drugs, etc.; OR (B) Having an alcohol concentration of 0.08 or more.

Motor vehicle

Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 32.34(a) (device for transport).

Watercraft

Vessel, water skis, aquaplane, or other device for transport on water (except if propelled *only* by water current).

Amusement ride

Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 2151.002, Occupations Code.

Mobile amusement ride

Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 2151.002, Occupations Code.

Sec. 49.02. Public Intoxication

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if they appear in a *public place* while *intoxicated* to the degree that the person may *endanger* the person or another.
  • Public Place (a-1): A premises licensed/permitted under the Alcoholic Beverage Code *is* a public place.
  • Defense (b): Substance was administered for *therapeutic purposes* as part of *professional medical treatment* by a licensed physician.
  • Penalty (c): Class C Misdemeanor.
  • Penalty - Minor (e): If offender is < 21 yoa, punishable under Sec. 106.071, Alcoholic Beverage Code.
  • Note (d): This offense is *not* a lesser included offense of DWI (49.04).

Sec. 49.031. Possession of Alcoholic Beverage in Motor Vehicle

  • Key Definitions (a):
    • Open Container: Bottle, can, etc. that contains *any amount* of alcoholic beverage and is open, has a broken seal, or has contents partially removed.
    • Passenger Area: Area for seating of operator and passengers. *Does NOT* include: (A) a locked glove box, (B) the trunk, or (C) area behind last upright seat (if no trunk).
    • Public Highway: Entire width between boundary lines of a public road, street, highway, etc. (includes the right-of-way).
  • Offense (b): A person commits an offense if they *knowingly possess* an *open container* in a *passenger area* of a motor vehicle that is on a *public highway* (regardless of whether vehicle is moving, stopped, or parked).
  • Single Offense (b): Possession of one or more open containers in a single episode is a single offense.
  • Exceptions (c): Does *not* apply to a passenger in:
    1. A vehicle used for compensation (bus, taxicab, limousine); or
    2. The living quarters of a motorized house coach (RV, motor home, self-contained camper).
  • Penalty (d): Class C Misdemeanor.
  • Citation (e): Officer *shall* issue a written citation and notice to appear (cite & release) instead of taking the person before a magistrate.

Sec. 49.04. Driving While Intoxicated (DWI)

  • Offense (a): Person is *intoxicated* while *operating a motor vehicle* in a *public place*.
  • Base Penalty (b): Class B Misdemeanor, with *minimum 72 hours* confinement. (Subject to 49.09 enhancements).
  • Enhancement (c): If person had *open container* in immediate possession: Class B Misdemeanor, with *minimum 6 days* confinement.
  • Enhancement (d): If analysis showed alcohol concentration of *0.15 or more*: Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (e): If operating in a *school crossing zone* (during reduced speed limit times): State Jail Felony.

Sec. 49.045. Driving While Intoxicated with Child Passenger

  • Offense (a): Commits DWI (49.04) AND the vehicle is occupied by a passenger *younger than 15 years of age*.
  • Penalty (b): State Jail Felony.

Other "Operation While Intoxicated" Offenses

  • Sec. 49.05. Flying While Intoxicated (FWI)
    • Offense (a): Person is *intoxicated* while *operating an aircraft*.
    • Penalty (b): Class B Misdemeanor, w/ *min 72 hours* confinement. (Subject to 49.09).
  • Sec. 49.06. Boating While Intoxicated (BWI)
    • Offense (a): Person is *intoxicated* while *operating a watercraft*.
    • Penalty (b): Class B Misdemeanor, w/ *min 72 hours* confinement. (Subject to 49.09).
  • Sec. 49.061. Boating While Intoxicated with Child Passenger
    • Offense (a): Commits BWI (49.06) AND watercraft is occupied by passenger *younger than 15 years of age*.
    • Penalty (b): State Jail Felony.
  • Sec. 49.065. Assembling or Operating an Amusement Ride While Intoxicated
    • Offense (a): Person is *intoxicated* while *operating* an amusement ride OR *assembling* a mobile amusement ride.
    • Base Penalty (b): Class B Misdemeanor, w/ *min 72 hours* confinement. (Subject to 49.09).
    • Enhancement (c): If person had *open container* in immediate possession: Class B Misdemeanor, w/ *min 6 days* confinement.

Sec. 49.07. Intoxication Assault

  • Offense (a): A person, by accident or mistake, *by reason of that intoxication* causes *serious bodily injury* to another while:
    1. Operating an aircraft, watercraft, amusement ride, or motor vehicle (in public place) while intoxicated; OR
    2. Assembling a mobile amusement ride while intoxicated.
  • Definition (b): "Serious Bodily Injury" means injury that creates a substantial risk of death or causes serious permanent disfigurement or protracted loss or impairment of any bodily member or organ.
  • Base Penalty (c): Felony of the Third Degree. (Subject to 49.09 enhancements).

Sec. 49.08. Intoxication Manslaughter

  • Offense (a): A person operates a motor vehicle (in public), aircraft, watercraft, or amusement ride, OR assembles a mobile amusement ride; AND is *intoxicated* and *by reason of that intoxication* causes the *death* of another by accident or mistake.
  • Base Penalty (b): Felony of the Second Degree. (Subject to 49.09 enhancements).

Sec. 49.09. Enhanced Offenses and Penalties

  • General Enhancements (a), (b):
    • DWI, FWI, BWI, or Ride (49.04-065) + 1 prior conviction (of any in that list) = Class A Misdemeanor (w/ 30-day min confinement).
    • DWI, FWI, BWI, Ride, or w/ Child (49.04-065) + 2 prior convictions (of any in that list) = Felony of the Third Degree.
    • DWI, FWI, BWI, Ride, or w/ Child (49.04-065) + 1 prior conviction for Intox. Manslaughter (49.08) = Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Intoxication Assault (49.07) Enhancements (b-1), (b-4):
    • Base Penalty: F3
    • SBI to *Firefighter* or *EMS* (on duty) = Felony of the Second Degree.
    • SBI to *Peace Officer* or *Judge* (on duty) = Felony of the First Degree.
    • SBI is a *Traumatic Brain Injury* (TBI) resulting in a *persistent vegetative state* = Felony of the Second Degree.
  • Intoxication Manslaughter (49.08) Enhancements (b-2):
    • Base Penalty: F2
    • Death of *Peace Officer, Judge, Firefighter, or EMS* (on duty) = Felony of the First Degree.
    • Death of *more than one person* = Felony of the First Degree.
    • (Note: A separate amendment also makes it F1 if actor had a prior intox offense AND was in violation of Ch. 51 at time of offense).
  • Definitions for Enhancement (c): This section (49.09(c)) provides comprehensive lists of what qualifies as a prior "Offense relating to..." DWI, FWI, BWI, or Ride, including old statute numbers.
  • "Final Conviction" (d): A conviction is *final* even if the sentence was *probated* (suspended).
  • Deferred Adjudication (g): For enhancement, *deferred adjudication* for 49.04 (DWI) or 49.06 (BWI) *counts as a prior conviction*.
  • Ignition Interlock (h): For a 2nd+ DWI offense *within 5 years* of the prior offense, the court *shall* order an ignition interlock device installed on all vehicles owned/operated by the defendant for 1 year.

Other Key Provisions (Sec. 49.10-49.12)

  • Sec. 49.10. No Defense
    • In a prosecution for any offense *except* Open Container (49.031), the fact that the defendant is or has been *entitled to use* the substance (e.g., prescription medication) is *NOT* a defense.
  • Sec. 49.11. Proof of Mental State Unnecessary
    • Proof of a *culpable mental state* (intentional, knowing, reckless, negligent) is *NOT required* for conviction of any offense in Ch. 49.
    • Exception (b): This does *NOT* apply to Sec. 49.031 (Open Container), which *does* require a "knowing" mental state.
  • Sec. 49.12. Applicability to Certain Conduct
    • Intoxication Assault (49.07) and Intoxication Manslaughter (49.08) do *NOT* apply to injury or death of an *unborn child* if the conduct was committed *by the mother* of the unborn child.
