Ch. 9: Justification Excluding Criminal Responsibility

Back to Main Page

Sec. 9.01. Definitions

Custody: Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 38.01.
Escape: Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 38.01.
Deadly Force: Force intended or known to cause, or in its manner of use/intended use is *capable* of causing, death or serious bodily injury.
Habitation: Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 30.01.
Vehicle: Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 30.01.

Subchapter A: General Provisions

Sec. 9.02. Justification as a Defense: It is a defense to prosecution that the conduct is justified under this chapter. (Note: This is a "defense," not an "affirmative defense.")

Sec. 9.03. Confinement as Justifiable Force: Confinement is justified when force is justified, *if* the actor takes reasonable measures to terminate the confinement as soon as he safely can (unless the person is under arrest).

Sec. 9.04. Threats as Justifiable Force: A threat of force is justified when the use of force is justified. A threat to cause death/SBI (e.g., producing a weapon) does NOT constitute the use of deadly force, as long as the purpose is limited to creating an apprehension.

Sec. 9.05. Reckless Injury of Innocent Third Person: If an actor is justified in using force but recklessly injures or kills an innocent third person, the justification is unavailable for the reckless injury/killing.

Sec. 9.06. Civil Remedies Unaffected: Justification under this chapter does not abolish or impair any remedy available in a civil suit.

Subchapter B: Justification Generally

Sec. 9.21. Public Duty

  • (a) General: Conduct is justified if the actor reasonably believes it is required or authorized by law, court order, or legal process.
  • (b) Control: Other subchapters (C, D, E, F) control for specific uses of force (e.g., protection of persons, property, law enforcement).
  • (c) Deadly Force: Justified *only* if the actor reasonably believes it is specifically required by statute or occurs in the lawful conduct of war. If justified, there is no duty to retreat.
  • (d) Mistake: Justification is still available even if the court/process was unlawful, as long as the actor reasonably believed it was lawful.

Sec. 9.22. Necessity

Conduct is justified if all three elements are met:

  1. The actor reasonably believes the conduct is immediately necessary to avoid imminent harm; AND
  2. The desirability and urgency of avoiding the harm clearly outweigh (by ordinary standards) the harm sought to be prevented by the law; AND
  3. A legislative purpose to exclude the justification does not plainly appear.

Subchapter C: Protection of Persons

Sec. 9.31. Self-Defense (Force)

  • (a) Test: Justified in using force when and to the degree the actor reasonably believes the force is immediately necessary to protect against another's use/attempted use of unlawful force.
  • (a) Presumption of Reasonableness: Belief is presumed reasonable if the other person was unlawfully and forcibly entering (or removing actor from) actor's occupied habitation, vehicle, or place of business; OR was committing/attempting Agg. Kidnapping, Murder, Sex. Assault, Agg. Sex. Assault, Robbery, or Agg. Robbery. (Actor must not have provoked & not be engaged in criminal activity).
  • (b) Not Justified: Force is NOT justified for: (1) Verbal provocation alone; (2) Resisting arrest by PO (unless 9.31(c) applies); (3) Actor consented; (4) Actor provoked (unless they abandon); (5) Actor sought discussion while illegally carrying/possessing a weapon.
  • (c) Resisting Arrest: Justified *only if* PO uses greater force than necessary *before* actor resists, and actor reasonably believes force is immediately necessary to protect against that *excessive* force.
  • (e) No Duty to Retreat (Force): A person with a right to be present, who did not provoke, and is not engaged in criminal activity is not required to retreat before using force.

Sec. 9.32. Deadly Force in Defense of Person

  • (a) Test: Justified in using deadly force if:
    1. Actor would be justified in using force under 9.31; AND
    2. Actor reasonably believes deadly force is immediately necessary to:
      • Protect against other's use/attempted use of unlawful deadly force; OR
      • Prevent imminent commission of Agg. Kidnapping, Murder, Sex. Assault, Agg. Sex. Assault, Robbery, or Agg. Robbery.
  • (b) Presumption of Reasonableness: Same as 9.31(a) presumption (home/vehicle/business invasion or specified offenses).
  • (c) No Duty to Retreat (Deadly Force): A person with a right to be present, who did not provoke, and is not engaged in criminal activity is not required to retreat before using deadly force.

Sec. 9.33. Defense of Third Person

  • Test: Justified in using force or deadly force if:
    1. Actor reasonably believes they would be justified under 9.31 or 9.32 in protecting *themselves* (i.e., "stands in the shoes" of the third person); AND
    2. Actor reasonably believes intervention is immediately necessary.

Sec. 9.34. Protection of Life or Health

  • (a) Suicide/SBI: Justified in using force (but NOT deadly force) to prevent another from committing suicide or inflicting SBI.
  • (b) Emergency: Justified in using both force AND deadly force if reasonably believed immediately necessary to preserve the other's life in an emergency.

Subchapter D: Protection of Property

Sec. 9.41. Protection of One's Own Property

  • (a) Prevent Interference: Lawful possessor is justified in using force if reasonably believed immediately necessary to prevent/terminate other's trespass or unlawful interference.
  • (b) Recover Property: Unlawfully dispossessed person is justified in using force to reenter/recover if:
    1. Actor uses force immediately or in fresh pursuit; AND
    2. Actor reasonably believed other had no claim of right OR other used force, threat, or fraud.

Sec. 9.42. Deadly Force to Protect Property

Justified in using deadly force if all three elements are met:

  1. Actor is justified in using force under 9.41; AND
  2. Actor reasonably believes deadly force is immediately necessary to:
    • Prevent imminent Arson, Burglary, Robbery, Agg. Robbery, Theft during nighttime, or Criminal Mischief during nighttime; OR
    • Prevent other fleeing immediately after Burglary, Robbery, Agg. Robbery, or Theft during nighttime; AND
  3. Actor reasonably believes that:
    • Property cannot be protected/recovered by other means; OR
    • Using lesser force would expose actor/another to substantial risk of death or SBI.

Sec. 9.43. Protection of Third Person's Property

  • Test: Justified if actor would be justified under 9.41 or 9.42 protecting their own property AND reasonably believes:
    1. It's attempted/consummated theft or criminal mischief; OR
    2. Third person requested protection; OR actor has legal duty; OR third person is family/resides with actor.

Sec. 9.44. Use of Device to Protect Property

  • Justification (9.41/9.43) applies to a device if:
    1. Device is NOT designed to cause (or known to create substantial risk of) death or SBI; AND
    2. Use of device is reasonable under all circumstances.

Subchapter E: Law Enforcement

Sec. 9.51. Arrest and Search

  • (a) Peace Officer (Force): Justified if actor reasonably believes force is immediately necessary to arrest/search/prevent escape, AND (1) reasonably believes arrest/search is lawful, AND (2) manifests purpose and identity (unless known/cannot be made known).
  • (b) Citizen (Force): Justified if actor reasonably believes force is immediately necessary to make a lawful arrest (or prevent escape), AND manifests purpose and reason for arrest.
  • (c) Peace Officer (Deadly Force): Justified if (a) is met AND actor reasonably believes conduct included use/attempted use of deadly force, OR substantial risk of death/SBI if arrest delayed.
  • (d) Citizen (Deadly Force): Justified if (b) is met AND actor reasonably believes the felony or offense against public peace included use/attempted use of deadly force, OR substantial risk of death/SBI if arrest delayed.
  • (e) No Duty to Retreat: No duty to retreat before using deadly force justified by (c) or (d).

Sec. 9.52. Prevention of Escape from Custody

  • Force is justified to prevent escape if it could have been employed to effect the arrest.
  • Exception: A guard or peace officer is justified in using any force, including deadly force, reasonably believed immediately necessary to prevent escape from a correctional facility.

Sec. 9.53. Maintaining Security in Correctional Facility

An officer/employee of a correctional facility is justified in using force if reasonably believed necessary to maintain security of the facility, safety of others, or their own safety.

Sec. 9.54. Limitation on Use of Force by Drone

Force by drone is justified *only if* (1) actor is LEO, (2) force is otherwise justified AND does not involve deadly force by an autonomous drone, AND (3) LEO agency has an adopted policy on drone force.

Sec. 9.55. Use of Less-Lethal Force Weapon

Applies only to guards or peace officers on duty. Justified in using force with a less-lethal weapon if reasonably believed necessary to accomplish official duties and use is in substantial compliance with training.

Subchapter F: Special Relationships

Sec. 9.61. Parent-Child

  • Justified in using force (but NOT deadly force) against a child < 18 if:
    1. Actor is parent, stepparent, or "in loco parentis"; AND
    2. Actor reasonably believes force is necessary to discipline or to safeguard/promote welfare.
  • "In loco parentis" includes grandparent, guardian, person under court order, or person with express/implied consent of parent.

Sec. 9.62. Educator-Student

Justified in using force (but NOT deadly force) if (1) actor is entrusted with care, supervision, or administration for a special purpose, AND (2) reasonably believes force is necessary to further the special purpose or to maintain discipline.

Sec. 9.63. Guardian-Incompetent

Justified in using force (but NOT deadly force) if (1) actor is guardian or similarly responsible, AND (2) reasonably believes force is necessary to safeguard welfare OR maintain discipline in an institution.
