Ch. 16: Criminal Instruments & Interception

Back to Main Page

Sec. 16.01. Unlawful Use of Criminal Instrument or Mechanical Security Device

Offenses:

  • (a)(1) Possession: A person commits an offense if they possess a criminal instrument or mechanical security device with the intent to use it in committing an offense.
  • (a)(2) Manufacture/Sell: A person commits an offense if, with knowledge and intent to aid in an offense, they manufacture, adapt, sell, install, or set up the instrument/device.

Punishment (c):

  • Possession (a)(1): One category lower than the offense intended.
  • Manufacture/Sell (a)(2): State Jail Felony.

Sec. 16.02. Unlawful Interception, Use, or Disclosure of Wire, Oral, or Electronic Communications

Primary Offenses (b):

A person commits an offense if they intentionally:

  • (b)(1) Intercept: Intercept, try to intercept, or get someone else to intercept a wire, oral, or electronic communication.
  • (b)(2) Disclose: Disclose the contents of a communication if they know it was obtained in violation of this section.
  • (b)(3) Use: Use the contents of a communication if they know (or are reckless) it was obtained in violation of this section.
  • (b)(4) Covert Entry: Knowingly effect a covert entry to intercept a communication without a court order.
  • (b)(5) Device on Wire: Use a device to intercept an *oral* communication when the device is *affixed to* a wire/cable or transmits by radio.

Device / Notice Offenses:

  • (d)(1) Manufacture/Possess/Sell Device: Intentionally manufacture, possess, or sell an interception device knowing it's designed for nonconsensual interception and will be used unlawfully.
  • (d)(2) Advertise Device: Place an ad for an interception device promoting its nonconsensual use.
  • (g) Give Notice of Interception: Knowing a LEO is authorized to intercept, gives notice to another person of the interception.

Key Provisions & Punishments (f):

  • (c) & (e) Affirmative Defenses: This section provides many specific affirmative defenses (e.g., a party to the call consents, LEO with a warrant, carrier protecting property, LEO in life-threatening situation, intercepting computer trespasser, etc.).
  • (f) Punishment:
    • Main Offenses (b): Second Degree Felony.
    • Device (d) or Notice (g) Offenses: State Jail Felony.

Sec. 16.03. Unlawful Use of Pen Register or Trap and Trace Device

  • Offense (a): Knowingly installs or uses a pen register or trap and trace device to record/decode numbers.
  • Punishment (d): State Jail Felony.
  • Affirmative Defenses (c): It is a defense if the actor is a communication carrier (for service protection/assisting LEO), a lawful enterprise (for internal service protection, not for LEO), or a person authorized by the CCP (e.g., LEO with an order).

Sec. 16.04. Unlawful Access to Stored Communications

  • Offense (b): Intentionally obtains, alters, or prevents authorized access to a wire or electronic communication while it is in electronic storage by (1) accessing a facility without authorization, or (2) exceeding authorization.
  • Punishment (c): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (d): Becomes a State Jail Felony if committed "to obtain a benefit or to harm another."
  • Affirmative Defenses (e): It is a defense if the conduct was authorized by the provider, the user, the addressee/recipient, or the CCP.

Sec. 16.05. Illegal Divulgence of Public Communications

  • Offense (b): A provider of electronic communications service to the public knowingly divulges the contents of a communication to someone other than the intended recipient.
  • Punishments (d), (e):
    • If scrambled/encrypted: State Jail Felony.
    • If *not* scrambled + for tortious purpose/benefit: Class A Misdemeanor.
    • If *not* scrambled + for tortious purpose/benefit + is public mobile/paging: Class C Misdemeanor.
  • Affirmative Defenses (c): It is a defense if divulged (1) as authorized by law, (2) to a person forwarding the communication, or (3) to a LEO if contents "reasonably appear to pertain to the commission of a crime."

Sec. 16.06. Unlawful Installation of Tracking Device

  • Offense (b): Knowingly installs a tracking device on a motor vehicle owned or leased by another person.
  • Punishment (c): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Affirmative Defenses (d): It is a defense if the actor (1) had consent of the owner/lessee, (2) was assisting a LEO, or (3) was a licensed Private Investigator with written consent or a court order.
  • Exemption (e): This section does not apply to a peace officer acting in the course of a criminal investigation.

Key Definitions (Ch. 16)

Note: Many technical terms (e.g., "intercept," "oral communication") are defined in the Code of Criminal Procedure (CCP) Chapters 18A and 18B.

Criminal Instrument (16.01)

Anything, (that is not otherwise illegal to possess/make/sell), that is specially designed, made, or adapted for use in the commission of an offense.

Mechanical Security Device (16.01)

A device designed or manufactured for use by a locksmith to perform services for a customer (e.g., lockpicks, slim jims).

Electronic or Mechanical Tracking Device (16.06)

A device capable of emitting an electronic frequency or other signal that may be used by a person to identify, monitor, or record the location of another person or object.

Readily Accessible to the General Public (16.02)

A radio communication that is NOT: scrambled or encrypted; using withheld modulation techniques; carried on a subcarrier; a common carrier system (with exceptions); or an electronic communication.
