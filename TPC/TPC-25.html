Ch. 25: Offenses Against the Family

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Ch. 25)

Under the appearance of being married (25.01)

Holding out that the parties are married with cohabitation and an intent to be married by either party.

Deviate sexual intercourse (25.02)

Any contact between the genitals of one person and the mouth or anus of another person with intent to arouse or gratify.

Sexual intercourse (25.02)

Any penetration of the female sex organ by the male sex organ.

Child (25.05)

Includes a child born out of wedlock whose paternity has been acknowledged by the actor or established in a civil suit.

Family violence, family, household (25.07)

Have the meanings assigned by Chapter 71, Family Code.

Global positioning monitoring system (25.07)

Has the meaning assigned by Article 17.49, Code of Criminal Procedure.

Assistance animal (25.07)

Has the meaning assigned by Section 121.002, Human Resources Code.

Sexual abuse / assault / stalking / trafficking (25.07)

Means conduct constituting an offense under Sec. 21.02/21.11, 22.011/22.021, 42.072, or 20A.02, respectively.

Adopted child (25.081)

A person younger than 18 who was legally adopted.

Unregulated custody transfer (25.081)

The transfer of permanent physical custody of an adopted child without court approval as required by Sec. 162.026, Family Code.

Public media (25.09)

Has the meaning from Sec. 38.01, and also includes the Internet or another public computer network.

Possessory right (25.10)

The right of a guardian to have physical possession of a ward and establish their legal domicile.

Ward (25.10)

Has the meaning assigned by Chapter 1002, Estates Code.

Sec. 25.01. Bigamy

  • Offense (a)(1): A legally married person commits an offense if they:
    1. Purport to marry or do marry someone else; OR
    2. Live with someone else in this state under the "appearance of being married."
  • Offense (a)(2): A person commits an offense if they *know* another person is married and they:
    1. Purport to marry or do marry that married person; OR
    2. Live with that married person in this state under the "appearance of being married."
  • Definition (b): "Under the appearance of being married" = holding out + cohabitation + intent to be married by *either* party.
  • Defense (c): It is a defense (for the married party) that the actor reasonably believed they were eligible to marry because the prior marriage was void or dissolved. A belief is reasonable if substantiated by a certified death certificate or court document.
  • Testimony (d): A lawful spouse *may* testify for or against the actor.
  • Penalty (e): Felony of the third degree, enhanced to:
    • Felony of the second degree if the other person is 17.
    • Felony of the first degree if the other person is 16 or younger.

Sec. 25.02. Prohibited Sexual Conduct

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if they engage in sexual intercourse or deviate sexual intercourse with another person the actor *knows* to be their:
    1. Ancestor or descendant (blood or adoption).
    2. Current or former stepchild or stepparent.
    3. Aunt or uncle.
    4. Brother or sister (whole, half, or adoption).
    5. Niece or nephew.
    6. First cousin.
  • Penalty (c): Felony of the third degree, *unless* it's with an ancestor/descendant (a)(1), which is a Felony of the second degree.

Sec. 25.03. Interference with Child Custody

  • Offense (a): A person takes or retains a child < 18 when:
    1. They *know* it violates a court order; OR
    2. They have no custody order, *know* a suit has been filed, and take the child out of the court's geographic area *with intent to deprive the court of authority*.
    3. They take the child *outside the U.S.* with intent to deprive another of possession/access.
  • Offense (b): A *noncustodial parent* commits an offense if, with intent to interfere, they knowingly entice or persuade the child to leave the custodian.
  • Defense (c): For (a)(2), it's a defense if the actor returned the child to the geographic area within 3 days.
  • Affirmative Defense (c-1): For (a)(3) (taking outside U.S.), it's an affirmative defense if it was pursuant to a valid order OR retention was due to circumstances beyond control and actor provided prompt notice.
  • Exception (c-2): (a)(3) does not apply if the person was entitled to possession/access AND was fleeing family violence.
  • Penalty (d): State jail felony.
  • Overlap (e): If conduct under (a)(3) is also Kidnapping (20.03), it can *only* be prosecuted as Kidnapping.

Sec. 25.031. Agreement to Abduct from Custody

  • Offense (a): A person agrees, *for remuneration*, to abduct a child (< 18) by force, threat, misrepresentation, etc., *knowing* the child is under court-ordered care.
  • Penalty (b): State jail felony.

Sec. 25.04. Enticing a Child

  • Offense (a): With *intent to interfere with lawful custody* of a child (< 18), knowingly entices, persuades, or takes the child from the custodian.
  • Penalty (b): Class B misdemeanor, *unless* the actor intended to commit a felony against the child, then it's a Felony of the third degree.

Sec. 25.05. Criminal Nonsupport

  • Offense (a): An individual *intentionally or knowingly* fails to provide support for their child (< 18) or a child they are under a court order to support.
  • Affirmative Defense (d): It is an affirmative defense that the actor *could not* provide support.
  • Penalty (f): State jail felony.

Sec. 25.06. Harboring Runaway Child

  • Offense (a): A person *knowingly* harbors a child and is *criminally negligent* about whether the child is < 18 AND has escaped custody or is voluntarily absent from home.
  • Defense (b): Actor was related within the second degree (e.g., grandparent, sibling).
  • Defense (c): Actor notified law enforcement or the child's home/custodian within 24 hours of discovery.
  • Penalty (d): Class A misdemeanor.
  • Mandatory LE Action (e): LE *shall immediately* enter a report of a missing/escaped child into NCIC.

Sec. 25.07. Violation of Certain Court Orders... (Protective Orders)

  • Offense (a): A person *knowingly or intentionally* violates a condition of bond or protective order (related to FV, sexual assault, stalking, etc.) by:
    1. Committing family violence or an act in furtherance of stalking, trafficking, etc.
    2. Communicating in a threatening/harassing manner or any communication if prohibited.
    3. Going to or near a protected place (residence, work, school).
    4. Possessing a firearm.
    5. Harming, threatening, or interfering with a protected animal.
    6. Tampering with a GPS monitor.
    7. Tracking a protected person's property/vehicle without consent.
  • No Effect (d): Reconciliatory actions (e.g., the victim invites the person over) *do not* affect the order's validity or the officer's duty to enforce.
  • Arrest (e): A peace officer *may not* arrest a *protected person* for a violation of that order.
  • Penalty (g): Class A misdemeanor, enhanced to:
    • State Jail Felony if it violates a post-conviction order OR the person possesses a deadly weapon during violations (a)(1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 7).
    • Felony of the third degree if the person has two or more prior convictions (under 25.07 or 25.072) OR violates the order by committing assault or stalking.

Sec. 25.071. Violation of Protective Order Preventing Offense Caused by Bias or Prejudice

  • Offense (a): Knowingly/intentionally violates a "hate crime" PO by:
    1. Committing a Title 5 (against person) or Title 7 (property) offense *because of* bias or prejudice.
    2. Communicating in a threatening/harassing manner.
    3. Going to or near a protected place.
  • Penalty (d): Class A misdemeanor, enhanced to a Felony of the third degree if two or more prior convictions or violated by committing assault.

Sec. 25.072. Repeated Violation of Certain Court Orders...

  • Offense (a): A person, *during a period of 12 months or less*, engages in conduct *two or more times* that constitutes an offense under Sec. 25.07.
  • Jury (b): Jury does not need to be unanimous on the specific acts, only that the actor committed two or more acts in the 12-month period.
  • Penalty (e): Felony of the third degree, enhanced to a Felony of the second degree if at least one of the acts was punishable as a SJF under 25.07(g)(1)(B) (violation while possessing a deadly weapon).

Sec. 25.08. Sale or Purchase of Child

  • Offense (a)(1): A person with custody of a child (< 18) offers to accept, or accepts, a thing of value for the child's delivery to another for adoption.
  • Offense (a)(2): A person offers to give, or gives, a thing of value to another to acquire a child for adoption.
  • Exceptions (b): Does *not* apply to legitimate fees paid to a child-placing agency, attorney, social worker, doctor, or for pregnancy-related expenses.
  • Penalty (c): Felony of the third degree, enhanced to a Felony of the second degree if done with intent to commit certain trafficking or sexual offenses.

Sec. 25.081. Unregulated Custody Transfer of Adopted Child

  • Offense (b): A person knowingly (1) conducts an unregulated custody transfer, or (2) facilitates or participates in one.
  • Exceptions (d): Does *not* apply to placement with a licensed agency, DFPS, a relative/stepparent, temporary placements (e.g., vacation, military service), or emergency "Baby Moses" delivery.
  • Penalty (c): Felony of the third degree, enhanced to a Felony of the second degree if done with intent to commit certain trafficking or sexual offenses.

Sec. 25.09. Advertising for Placement of Child

  • Offense (a): A person advertises in the *public media* (including the Internet) that they will place, provide, or obtain a child for adoption.
  • Exception (b): Does *not* apply to a *licensed child-placing agency* that identifies itself as such in the ad.
  • Penalty (c): Class A misdemeanor, enhanced to a Felony of the third degree if the person has a previous conviction under this section.

Sec. 25.10. Interference with Rights of Guardian of the Person

  • Offense (b): A person takes, retains, or conceals a *ward* when the person *knows* their action interferes with a *possessory right* of the guardian.
  • Penalty (c): State jail felony.

Sec. 25.11. Continuous Violence Against the Family

  • Offense (a): A person, *during a period of 12 months or less*, engages in conduct *two or more times* that constitutes Assault (22.01(a)(1)) against a family member.
  • Jury (b): Jury does *not* need to be unanimous on the specific acts, dates, or counties. They must only agree unanimously that the actor committed two or more such acts in the 12-month period.
  • Penalty (e): Felony of the third degree.
