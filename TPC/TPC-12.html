Ch. 12: Punishments

Back to Main Page

Subchapter A: General Provisions

Sec. 12.01. Punishment in Accordance with Code: A person adjudged guilty shall be punished in accordance with this chapter and the Code of Criminal Procedure. This chapter does not remove a court's authority to impose civil penalties (e.g., forfeit property, suspend license, cite for contempt).

Sec. 12.02. Classification of Offenses: Offenses are designated as felonies or misdemeanors.

Sec. 12.03. Classification of Misdemeanors: Misdemeanors are classified as Class A, Class B, or Class C. An offense designated a misdemeanor without specification is a Class C misdemeanor. A Class C conviction does not impose any legal disability or disadvantage.

Sec. 12.04. Classification of Felonies: Felonies are classified as: (1) capital felonies, (2) first degree, (3) second degree, (4) third degree, and (5) state jail felonies. An offense designated a felony without specification is a state jail felony.

Subchapter B: Ordinary Misdemeanor Punishments

Class A Misdemeanor

Up to 1 year in jail

Up to a $4,000 fine

Class B Misdemeanor

Up to 180 days in jail

Up to a $2,000 fine

Class C Misdemeanor

(No jail time)

Up to a $500 fine

Subchapter C: Ordinary Felony Punishments

First Degree Felony

5 to 99 years or Life

Up to a $10,000 fine

Second Degree Felony

2 to 20 years

Up to a $10,000 fine

Third Degree Felony

2 to 10 years

Up to a $10,000 fine

State Jail Felony

180 days to 2 years

Up to a $10,000 fine

Sec. 12.31. Capital Felony

  • If state seeks death penalty: Death or Life Without Parole (LWOP).
  • If state does NOT seek death (offender 18+): Life Without Parole (LWOP).
  • If state does NOT seek death (offender < 18): Life (with possibility of parole).

Sec. 12.35. State Jail Felony Punishment

  • Base range is 180 days to 2 years in a *state jail* and an optional fine up to $10,000.
  • (c) Enhancement: Punished as a Third Degree Felony (2-10 years) if it's shown:
    1. A deadly weapon was used/exhibited; OR
    2. The individual has a previous final felony conviction for certain offenses (e.g., Sec. 21.02, or offenses listed in Art. 42A.054(a) CCP).

Subchapter D: Exceptional Sentences (Enhancements)

Sec. 12.41. Classification of Offenses Outside This Code

For enhancement purposes, convictions from other laws are classified as:

  • "Felony of the third degree" if penitentiary time was a possible punishment.
  • "Class B misdemeanor" if it wasn't a felony but jail time was a possible punishment.
  • "Class C misdemeanor" if punishable by fine only.

Sec. 12.42. Penalties for Repeat and Habitual Felony Offenders

  • 3rd Degree Felony + 1 prior felony = Punished as 2nd Degree.
  • 2nd Degree Felony + 1 prior felony = Punished as 1st Degree.
  • 1st Degree Felony + 1 prior felony = 15-99 years or Life (min. 15 yrs).
  • Any Felony (non-SJF) + 2 prior sequential felonies = 25-99 years or Life (min. 25 yrs).
  • Note: Standard SJF priors cannot be used for these enhancements.
  • Note: Subsections (c)(2), (c)(3), and (c)(4) create specific "Life" or "LWOP" sentences for repeat sexual offenders.

Sec. 12.425. Penalties for Repeat State Jail Felony Offenders

  • SJF + 2 prior SJF convictions = Punished as 3rd Degree.
  • SJF + 2 prior *sequential* non-SJF felony convictions = Punished as 2nd Degree.

Sec. 12.43. Penalties for Repeat Misdemeanor Offenders

  • Class A + 1 prior (Class A or Felony) = Min. 90 days jail (max 1yr / $4k).
  • Class B + 1 prior (Class A, B, or Felony) = Min. 30 days jail (max 180 days / $2k).

Sec. 12.44. Reduction of State Jail Felony to Misdemeanor

  • (a) A court may punish a SJF as a Class A misdemeanor if it finds it would best serve the ends of justice.
  • (b) At the prosecutor's request, the court may allow a SJF to be prosecuted as a Class A misdemeanor.

Other Enhancements & Provisions

  • Sec. 12.45. Admission of Unadjudicated Offense: A defendant can (with state's consent) admit guilt to unadjudicated offenses during sentencing, and the court can take them into account. This bars future prosecution for those admitted offenses.
  • Sec. 12.46. Use of Prior Convictions: Using a conviction for enhancement once does not preclude it from being used again for a subsequent enhancement.
  • Sec. 12.47. Bias or Prejudice: (Hate Crime) If an affirmative finding is made, punishment is increased to the next highest category. (Class A bumps to 180-day min).
  • Sec. 12.49. Controlled Substance Used to Commit Offense: Punishment is increased to the next highest category.
  • Sec. 12.50. Offense in Disaster Area: For certain offenses (Burglary, Theft, Assault, etc.), punishment is increased to the next highest category.
  • Sec. 12.501. Retaliation vs. Public Servant: For certain offenses, punishment is increased to the next highest category.
  • Sec. 12.503. Metal/Body Armor Used: Punishment is increased to the next highest category.

Subchapter E: Corporations and Associations

Sec. 12.51 authorizes fines for corporations, associations, and other business entities adjudged guilty of an offense.

Maximum Fines:

  • $50,000 if an individual suffers SBI or death as a result of a Felony or Class A Misdemeanor.
  • $20,000 if the offense is a felony of any category.
  • $10,000 if the offense is a Class A or B misdemeanor.
  • $2,000 if the offense is a Class C misdemeanor.

Alternative Fine (Sec. 12.51(c))

In lieu of the fines above (except Class C), if the court finds the entity gained money/property or caused loss/damage/injury, it may impose a fine up to double the amount gained or lost, whichever is greater.

Other Penalties

  • The court may order the entity to give notice of the conviction to any person the court deems appropriate.
  • The court must notify the attorney general of the conviction.
