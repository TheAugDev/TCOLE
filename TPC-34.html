Ch. 34: Money Laundering

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 34.01)

Criminal activity

(A) Any offense classified as a felony under TX or US law; OR (B) an offense punishable by confinement for more than one year under another state's laws.

Funds

A very broad term including (A) coin/paper money, (B) US notes, (C) foreign bank notes, and (D) currency equivalents like electronic funds, checks, money orders, bearer instruments, stored value cards, or digital currency.

Financial institution

Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 32.01 (Fraud).

Proceeds

Funds acquired or derived (directly or indirectly) from, produced through, realized through, or used in the commission of: (A) an act; or (B) conduct under Tax Code Sec. 151.7032.

Sec. 34.02. Money Laundering

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if the person knowingly:
    1. Acquires or maintains an interest in, conceals, possesses, transfers, or transports the proceeds of criminal activity;
    2. Conducts, supervises, or facilitates a transaction involving the proceeds of criminal activity;
    3. Invests, expends, or receives (or offers to) the proceeds of criminal activity OR funds the person believes are the proceeds; OR
    4. Finances or invests (or intends to) funds the person believes are intended to further the commission of criminal activity.
  • Mental State (a-1): Knowledge of the *specific nature* of the criminal activity is NOT required to establish culpability.
  • Presumption (b): (Sting Operations) A person is presumed to believe funds are proceeds if a peace officer (or person at their direction) represents to the person that the funds are proceeds.
  • Penalties (e):
    • $2,500 < $30,000: State Jail Felony.
    • $30,000 < $150,000: Felony of the Third Degree.
    • $150,000 < $300,000: Felony of the Second Degree.
    • $300,000 or more: Felony of the First Degree.
  • Aggregation (f): If part of one scheme or continuing course of conduct, values may be aggregated to determine the grade of offense.

Sec. 34.02. Defenses & Other Provisions

  • Defense (c): It is a defense that the person acted with intent to facilitate lawful seizure, forfeiture, or other legitimate law enforcement purpose.
  • Defense (d) (Right to Counsel): It is a defense that the transaction was necessary to preserve a person's right to representation (6th Am. US / Art. 1, Sec. 10 TX).
  • Defense (d) (Legal Fees): It is a defense that the funds were bona fide legal fees received by a licensed attorney who, at the time of receipt, did not have actual knowledge that the funds were derived from criminal activity.
  • Provision (g): Funds on deposit at one branch of a financial institution are considered property of that branch AND any other branch.
  • Provision (h): If conduct is an offense under this section and another law, the actor may be prosecuted under this section, the other law, or both.

Sec. 34.021. Protection from Civil Liability

  • A financial institution (or its agent) acting for a legitimate law enforcement purpose (under 34.02(c)) is not liable for civil damages to a person who claims an ownership interest in the funds.

Sec. 34.03. Assistance by Attorney General

  • The Attorney General, if requested by a prosecuting attorney, may assist in the prosecution of an offense under this chapter.
