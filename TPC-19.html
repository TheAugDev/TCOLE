Ch. 19: Criminal Homicide

Back to Main Page

Sec. 19.01. Types of Criminal Homicide

  • (a) Definition: A person commits criminal homicide if they intentionally, knowingly, recklessly, or with criminal negligence causes the death of an individual.
  • (b) Types: Criminal homicide is Murder, Capital Murder, Manslaughter, or Criminally Negligent Homicide.

Sec. 19.04. Manslaughter

  • (a) Definition: A person commits an offense if he recklessly causes the death of an individual.
  • (b) Punishment: Felony of the Second Degree.
  • (b) Enhancement: Becomes a Felony of the First Degree if it's shown the actor committed an offense under Sec. 28.09 (Impairment of Public Comm/Transport) and that conduct caused the death.

Sec. 19.05. Criminally Negligent Homicide

  • (a) Definition: A person commits an offense if he causes the death of an individual by criminal negligence.
  • (b) Punishment: State Jail Felony.

Sec. 19.02. Murder

Four Ways to Commit Murder (19.02(b)):

A person commits murder if they:

  1. (b)(1): Intentionally or knowingly cause the death of an individual.
  2. (b)(2): Intend to cause serious bodily injury and commit an act clearly dangerous to human life that causes the death.
  3. (b)(3): Commit or attempt a felony (other than manslaughter), and in furtherance of or in flight from, commit an act clearly dangerous to human life that causes the death. (Felony Murder)
  4. (b)(4): Knowingly manufacture or deliver a controlled substance (PG 1-B)... and an individual dies as a result of using any amount of it.

Punishment (c):

  • An offense under this section is a Felony of the First Degree.

Defense for (b)(4) Offense (e):

  • It is a defense to prosecution under (b)(4) (Fentanyl delivery) that the actor's conduct was authorized by law (e.g., a pharmacist).

Sudden Passion (19.02(d))

  • When: Raised at the punishment stage of a trial.
  • Issue: That the defendant caused the death under the immediate influence of sudden passion arising from an adequate cause.
  • Burden of Proof: The defendant must prove the issue by a preponderance of the evidence.
  • Effect: If proven, the offense is reduced from a Felony of the First Degree to a Felony of the Second Degree.

Sec. 19.03. Capital Murder

Base Requirement (a):

A person must first commit Murder as defined under Sec. 19.02(b)(1) (intentionally or knowingly causes death) AND one of the following 10 factors must be present:

Aggravating Factors (a)(1) - (a)(10):

  1. Murders a peace officer or fireman (who is on duty and actor knows is PO/FF).
  2. Intentionally murders in the course of kidnapping, burglary, robbery, aggravated sexual assault, arson, obstruction or retaliation, or terroristic threat.
  3. Murders for remuneration (murder for hire, either paying or being paid).
  4. Murders while escaping or attempting to escape from a penal institution.
  5. While incarcerated, murders an employee OR murders with intent to participate in a combination.
  6. While incarcerated for Murder (or other specific serious felonies), murders another.
  7. Murders more than one person (either in the same transaction or same scheme).
  8. Murders an individual under 10 years of age.
  9. Murders an individual 10 years or older but younger than 15.
  10. Murders a judge or justice in retaliation for their service.

Punishment & Other Provisions:

  • (b) Punishment: An offense under this section is a Capital Felony.
  • (c) Lesser Included: If a jury does not find guilt for Capital Murder, they may convict of Murder or any other lesser included offense.
  • (d) Presumption for (a)(1): The actor is presumed to have known the victim was a PO/FF if they were wearing a distinctive uniform or badge.

Key Definitions (19.02(a))

Adequate Cause (19.02(a)(1))

Cause that would commonly produce a degree of anger, rage, resentment, or terror in a person of ordinary temper, sufficient to render the mind incapable of cool reflection.

Sudden Passion (19.02(a)(2))

Passion directly caused by and arising out of provocation by the individual killed (or another acting with them), which passion arises at the time of the offense and is not solely the result of former provocation.

Sec. 19.06. Applicability to Certain Conduct

This chapter (Criminal Homicide) does not apply to the death of an unborn child if the conduct charged is:

  • (1) Conduct committed by the mother of the unborn child.
  • (2) A lawful medical procedure (e.g., abortion) where the death was the intended result.
  • (3) A lawful medical procedure as part of an assisted reproduction.
  • (4) The lawful dispensation or administration of a drug.
