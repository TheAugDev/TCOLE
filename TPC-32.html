Ch. 32: Fraud

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 32.01)

Financial institution

Bank, trust, insurance co., credit union, savings/loan, investment trust, etc., held out for public deposit/investment.

Property

(A) Real property; (B) Tangible/intangible personal property; (C) A document (including money) that represents value.

Service

Includes: (A) Labor; (B) Telecommunication, utility, transport; (C) Lodging, restaurant, entertainment; (D) Supply of motor vehicle for use.

Steal

To acquire property or service by theft.

Sec. 32.02. Value

  • Default (a): (1) Fair market value at time/place of offense; OR (2) If no market value, cost of replacing.
  • Documents (b): (1) For debt (check, etc.): amount due/collectible; (2) Other docs: greatest amount of economic loss owner might suffer.
  • Cannot Ascertain (c): If value can't be found by (a) or (b), it is deemed to be $750 or more but less than $2,500.
  • Deduction (d): If actor proves they gave consideration or had legal interest, that amount is deducted from the total value.

Sec. 32.03. Aggregation

  • Amounts obtained via one scheme or continuing course of conduct can be combined (aggregated) into one offense to determine the penalty grade.

Sec. 32.21. Forgery

  • "Forge" (a)(1): (A) Alter, make, complete, execute, or authenticate a writing to purport it's: (i) by another who didn't authorize it; (ii) executed at a different time/place; (iii) a copy of a non-existent original. (B) Issue, transfer, pass, or utter a forged writing. (C) Possess a forged writing with intent to utter.
  • "Writing" (a)(2): Includes printing, money, coins, tokens, stamps, seals, credit cards, badges, trademarks, and symbols of value/ID.
  • Offense (b): Commits offense if he forges a writing with intent to defraud or harm another.
  • Penalties:
    • State Jail Felony: Base offense (c).
    • Felony of the Third Degree (d): If writing is a: will, codicil, deed, deed of trust, mortgage, security instrument, credit card, check, debit authorization, or similar commercial instrument.
    • Felony of the Second Degree (e): If writing is: (1) Money, securities, stamps; (2) Govt record (37.01(2)(C)); (3) Other instrument issued by state/national govt.
  • Value-Based Penalties (e-1): If done to obtain property/service, the offense is punished by the standard theft ladder (Class B up to F1).
    • Class B Misdemeanor: < $100
    • Class A Misdemeanor: $100 - $749.99
    • State Jail Felony: $750 - $2,499.99
    • Felony 3: $2,500 - $29,999.99
    • Felony 2: $30,000 - $149,999.99
    • Felony 1: $150,000 or more
  • Elderly Enhancement (e-2): Increased to next higher category if victim is elderly (does not apply to F1).
  • Presumption (f): Intent to defraud is presumed if actor has 2 or more forged government records (37.01(2)(C)).
  • Presumption (f-1): Possession of forged money is presumed intent to obtain property/service equal to the total purported value.

Sec. 32.22. Criminal Simulation

  • Offense (a): With intent to defraud or harm: (1) Makes/alters an object so it appears to have value due to age, antiquity, rarity, source, or authorship it doesn't have; (2) Possesses such an object with intent to sell/pass; (3) Authenticates such an object as genuine.
  • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 32.23. Trademark Counterfeiting

  • "Counterfeit mark" (a)(1): A mark identical/indistinguishable from a protected mark, used without authorization.
  • Offense (b): Intentionally manufactures, displays, advertises, distributes, offers for sale, sells, or possesses with intent to sell a counterfeit mark or an item/service bearing a counterfeit mark, knowing or should have known it was counterfeit.
  • Aggregation (d): Amounts from one scheme/continuing course of conduct may be aggregated.
  • Penalties (e): Follows the standard theft value ladder (Class C up to F1, but with higher top-end values).
    • Class C: < $100
    • Class B: $100 - $749.99
    • Class A: $750 - $2,499.99
    • SJF: $2,500 - $29,999.99
    • F3: $30,000 - $149,999.99
    • F2: $150,000 - $299,999.99
    • F1: $300,000 or more

Sec. 32.24. Stealing or Receiving Stolen Check...

  • Offense (a): (1) Steals an unsigned check or similar sight order; OR (2) With knowledge it was stolen, receives it with intent to use, sell, or transfer it.
  • Penalty (b): State Jail Felony.

Sec. 32.31. Credit Card or Debit Card Abuse

  • Offense (b): Commits offense if:
    1. (b)(1) With intent to obtain benefit fraudulently, uses a card knowing it's not issued to him, expired, revoked, or cancelled.
    2. (b)(2) Uses a fictitious card or number.
    3. (b)(3) Receives a benefit knowing it was obtained in violation.
    4. (b)(4) Steals a card, or receives a stolen card with intent to use/sell/transfer.
    5. (b)(8) Not the cardholder, possesses a card with intent to use it.
    6. (b)(9) Possesses two or more incomplete cards with intent to complete them.
    7. (b)(10) Authorized merchant, with intent to defraud, furnishes goods on a card obtained in violation (stolen, forged, expired).
    8. (b)(11) Authorized merchant, with intent to defraud, fails to furnish goods he represents in writing he did furnish.
  • Presumption (c): Knowledge of revocation/cancellation is presumed if notice was sent (oral, mail, telegram). Notice presumed received 5 days after sending.
  • Penalty (d): State Jail Felony.
  • Elderly Enhancement (d): Becomes a Felony of the Third Degree if committed against an elderly individual.

Sec. 32.315. Fraudulent Use/Possession of Card Info

  • Offense (b): With intent to harm/defraud, obtains, possesses, transfers, or uses: (1) a counterfeit card; (2) card number + expiration date (w/o consent); (3) data from digital imprint (w/o consent).
  • Presumption (c): If actor possesses 5 or more items (b)(2) or (b)(3), presumed to possess w/o consent.
  • Penalties (e): Based on number of items.
    • State Jail Felony: < 5 items
    • Felony 3: 5 to 9 items
    • Felony 2: 10 to 49 items
    • Felony 1: 50 or more items

Sec. 32.32. False Statement to Obtain Property/Credit

  • Offense (b): Intentionally/knowingly makes a materially false or misleading written statement to obtain property or credit (including a mortgage loan).
  • Offense (b-1): Intentionally/knowingly makes a materially false/misleading written statement in providing an appraisal of real property for compensation.
  • Penalties (c): Follows the standard theft value ladder (Class C up to F1).

Sec. 32.33. Hindering Secured Creditors

  • Offense (b): Person who signed security agreement, with intent to hinder enforcement, destroys, removes, conceals, encumbers, or otherwise harms/reduces value of the property.
  • Presumption (c): Intent to hinder is presumed if debt was due AND person failed to (1) pay and (2) deliver property after demand.
  • Penalties (d): Follows the standard theft value ladder (Class C up to F1).
  • Offense (e): Debtor sells/disposes of property (or fails to account for proceeds) with intent to appropriate the proceeds/value.
  • Penalties (e): Follows the standard theft value ladder (Class C up to F1).

Sec. 32.34. Fraudulent Transfer of a Motor Vehicle

  • Offense (b): Person acquires/accepts a motor vehicle under agreement to transfer to 3rd party AND:
    1. (b)(1) Knowing vehicle is subject to lien/lease, transfers w/o written authorization from lienholder.
    2. (b)(2) Intending to defraud owner, transfers to 3rd party.
    3. (b)(4) Fails to disclose location of vehicle on request of owner/lienholder.
  • Penalties (f): For (b)(1), (b)(2), (b)(3):
    • State Jail Felony: Value < $30,000
    • Felony 3: $30,000 - $149,999.99
    • Felony 2: $150,000 - $299,999.99
    • Felony 1: $300,000 or more
  • Penalty (g): For (b)(4) (failure to disclose location): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 32.35. Credit Card Transaction Record Laundering

  • Offense (b): Authorized vendor, with intent to defraud, presents a record of a sale that was not made by the vendor.
  • Offense (c): Person causes a vendor to present a record of a sale that was not made by the vendor.
  • Penalties (e): Follows the standard theft value ladder (Class C up to F1).

Sec. 32.41. Issuance of Bad Check

  • Offense (a): Issues/passes a check knowing the issuer does not have sufficient funds.
  • Presumption (b): Knowledge presumed if: (1) No account; OR (2) Payment refused for NSF within 30 days, AND issuer failed to pay within 10 days after receiving notice of refusal.
  • Penalty (f): Class C Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (f): Becomes Class B Misdemeanor if the check was for child support.

Sec. 32.42. Deceptive Business Practices

  • Offense (b): In course of business, w/ I, K, R, or CN, commits:
    1. (1) Using false weight/measure.
    2. (2) Selling less than represented quantity.
    3. (4) Selling adulterated/mislabeled commodity.
    4. (6) Representing used commodity as new.
    5. (8) Advertising w/ intent not to sell as advertised.
    6. (11) Conducting a deceptive sales contest.
    7. (12) Making materially false/misleading statement.
  • Penalties (c): For (b)(1-6):
    • Class C Misdemeanor: If done w/ Criminal Negligence (and no prior).
    • Class A Misdemeanor: If done w/ I, K, R (or has prior).
  • Penalties (d): For (b)(7-12): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 32.43. Commercial Bribery

  • Offense (b): Fiduciary (agent, employee, trustee, lawyer, etc.) solicits, accepts, or agrees to accept a benefit without beneficiary's consent, on agreement it will influence his conduct.
  • Offense (c): Person offers, confers, or agrees to confer a benefit, the acceptance of which is an offense.
  • Penalty (d): State Jail Felony.

Sec. 32.44. Rigging Publicly Exhibited Contest

  • Offense (a): With intent to affect outcome: (1) Offers benefit / threatens harm to participant or official; (2) Tampers with person, animal, or thing.
  • Offense (b): Solicits, accepts, or agrees to accept benefit from (a).
  • Penalty (c): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 32.441. Illegal Recruitment of an Athlete

  • Offense (a): W/o consent of university, solicits, accepts, or agrees to accept benefit on agreement it will influence enrollment/participation in intercollegiate athletics.
  • Offense (b): Offers, confers, or agrees to confer a benefit from (a).
  • Penalties (e): Follows the standard theft value ladder (Class C up to F1).

Sec. 32.45. Misapplication of Fiduciary Property...

  • "Misapply" (a)(2): Deal with property contrary to (A) agreement or (B) law.
  • Offense (b): Intentionally, knowingly, or recklessly misapplies property held as fiduciary (or property of financial institution) in a manner involving substantial risk of loss.
  • Penalties (c): Follows the standard theft value ladder (Class C up to F1).
  • Elderly Enhancement (d): Increased to next higher category if victim is elderly (does not apply to F1).

Sec. 32.46. Fraudulent Securing of Document Execution

  • Offense (a)(1): With intent to defraud/harm, causes another, without effective consent, to sign or execute any document affecting property, service, or pecuniary interest.
  • Offense (a)(2): Causes public servant (w/o effective consent) to file/record a purported judgment or document from a purported court (not a real court).
  • Penalties (b): For (a)(1): Follows the standard theft value ladder (Class C up to F1).
  • Penalty (c): For (a)(2): State Jail Felony.
  • Elderly/Medicaid Enhancement (c-1): Increased to next higher category.

Sec. 32.47. Fraudulent Destruction... of Writing

  • Offense (a): With intent to defraud/harm, destroys, removes, conceals, alters, substitutes, or otherwise impairs the verity/legibility/availability of a writing (non-govt record).
  • "Writing" (b): Includes money, coins, credit cards, trademarks, UPCs, labels, price tags.
  • Penalty (c): Base offense is Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Penalty (d): Becomes State Jail Felony if writing is a will, codicil, deed, mortgage, or other publicly recorded document.
  • Price Tag Switch (e): If actor alters a price tag to get a lesser price, penalty follows the standard theft value ladder based on the *difference* in price.

Sec. 32.51. Fraudulent Use/Possession of Identifying Info

  • "Identifying information" (a)(1): Name, DOB, biometric data, unique electronic ID/account number, SSN, etc.
  • Offense (b): With intent to harm/defraud, obtains, possesses, transfers, or uses: (1) ID info of another (w/o consent); (2) ID info of a deceased person (w/o legal auth); (3) ID info of a child < 18.
  • Presumption (b-1): Intent to harm/defraud presumed if actor possesses ID info of 3 or more other persons.
  • Penalties (c): Based on number of items of ID info.
    • State Jail Felony: < 5 items
    • Felony 3: 5 to 9 items
    • Felony 2: 10 to 49 items
    • Felony 1: 50 or more items
  • Enhancement (c-1): Increased to next higher category if victim is elderly or used to facilitate a sex offender reg. offense.

Sec. 32.53. Exploitation of Child, Elderly, or Disabled

  • "Exploitation" (a)(2): Illegal or improper use of the person or their resources for monetary or personal benefit, profit, or gain.
  • Offense (b): Person intentionally, knowingly, or recklessly causes the exploitation of a child, elderly, or disabled individual.
  • Penalty (c): Felony of the Third Degree.

Sec. 32.55. Financial Abuse of Elderly Individual

  • "Financial abuse" (a)(2): Wrongful taking, appropriation, obtaining, retention, or use of money/property, including by undue influence.
  • "Financial exploitation" (a)(3): Wrongful taking by a person in a relationship of confidence or trust (relative, caregiver, fiduciary, etc.).
  • Offense (c): Person knowingly engages in the financial abuse of an elderly individual.
  • Penalties (d): Follows the standard theft value ladder (Class B up to F1).
  • Presumption (f): If actor is a caregiver (b)(5) and victim has dementia/Alzheimer's, transfer is rebuttably presumed wrongful.

Sec. 32.48. Simulating Legal Process

  • Offense (a): Recklessly causes delivery of a document that simulates a summons, complaint, judgment, etc. with intent to (1) induce payment or (2) cause person to submit to its authority.
  • Penalty (e): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (f): Becomes State Jail Felony if actor has prior conviction under this section.

Sec. 32.49. Refusal to Execute Release of Fraudulent Lien

  • Offense (a): With intent to defraud/harm, holds a fraudulent lien AND, refuses to execute a release within 21 days of receiving notice.
  • Presumption (b): Failure to release within 21 days presumes intent to harm/defraud.
  • Penalty (c): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (c): Becomes Felony of the Third Degree if property owner is a public servant.

Sec. 32.50. Deceptive Preparation/Marketing of Academic Product

  • Offense (b): With intent to make a profit, prepares, sells, or offers an academic product (term paper, thesis, etc.) knowing it will be submitted by another person for credit.
  • Offense (c): Advertises they will prepare such a product.
  • Penalty (g): Class C Misdemeanor.

Sec. 32.52. Fraudulent, Substandard, or Fictitious Degree

  • Offense (b): Uses/claims to hold a postsecondary degree knowing it is fraudulent, fictitious, or revoked AND uses it to: (A) promote a business; or (B) get employment, a license, a promotion, admission to education, or a govt position.
  • Penalty (c): Class B Misdemeanor.

Sec. 32.54. Fraudulent or Fictitious Military Record

  • Offense (b): Uses/claims to hold a military record (medal, award, etc.) knowing it is fraudulent, fictitious, or revoked AND uses it to: (A) promote a business; or (B) get priority services, employment preference, a license, promotion, benefit/donation, admission to education, or a govt position.
  • Penalty (c): Class B Misdemeanor.

Multiple "32.56" Sections (New Offenses)

  • Sec. 32.56 (Gift Card Fraud): (b) With intent to harm/defraud: (1) Acquires/possesses gift card/info w/o consent; (2) Alters/tampers with card/packaging; (3) Uses counterfeit/stolen card/info. (c) Presumption if possesses 3 or more. (e) Penalties based on number of cards (SJF: < 5; F3: 5-9; F2: 10-49; F1: 50+).
  • Sec. 32.56 (Mail Receptacle Key): (b) With intent to harm/defraud, obtains, possesses, duplicates, transfers, or uses a postal service key or lock. (c) Penalty: Felony of the Third Degree (F2 if prior conviction).
  • Sec. 32.56 (Fictitious Doc Conveying Real Property): (a) With intent to enter/remain on real property, knowingly presents a false/fraudulent/fictitious lease, deed, or other instrument. (b) Penalty: Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Sec. 32.56 (Financial Abuse via AI/Phishing): (b) Knowingly engages in financial abuse through: (1) Artificially generated media (deepfake); or (2) Phishing (email, etc. to get info). (c) Penalties: Follows standard theft value ladder (Class B to F1).

Sec. 32.57. Fraudulent Sale/Rental of Residential Property

  • Offense (a): Knowingly: (1) Lists or advertises residential property for sale/rent knowing person has no legal title/authority; or (2) Sells, rents, or leases property to another w/o legal title/authority.
  • Penalty (b): Felony of the First Degree.

Sec. 32.60. Real Property Fraud (Effective Dec 2025)

  • Offense (b): (1) I/K makes false written statement to obtain real property; OR (2) With intent to defraud/harm: (A) Causes person w/o effective consent to sign document affecting real property; (B) Causes public servant to file/record false document affecting title/lien.
  • Penalties (c):
    • Felony 2: Value < $300,000
    • Felony 1: Value $300,000 or more
  • Enhancement (d): Increased to next higher category if owner is elderly, disabled, or a non-profit, or if property is a residence homestead.

Sec. 32.61. Malicious Solicitation of Disaster Victim (Effective Dec 2025)

  • Offense (b): With intent to defraud/harm: (1) Solicits donation for disaster relief (but doesn't use it for that); (2) Solicits for donee but keeps donations; (3) Solicits payment from victim after inducing belief actor is a volunteer providing free services; (4) Solicits payment from victim to search for missing relative.
  • Penalties (c): For (b)(1-3): Felony 3 (F2 if prior conviction).
  • Penalty (e): For (b)(4): Felony 1.
