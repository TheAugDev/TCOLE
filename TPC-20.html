Ch. 20: Kidnapping, Restraint, & Smuggling

Back to Main Page

Sec. 20.01. Key Definitions

Restrain (1)

To restrict a person's movements without consent, so as to interfere substantially with the person's liberty, by moving the person or by confining the person.

"Without Consent" (1)

Restraint is "without consent" if it is accomplished by: (A) force, intimidation, or deception; OR (B) any means if the victim is a child < 14 (or incompetent) and parent has not acquiesced; OR (B)(ii) the victim is a child 14-17, is taken out of state AND > 120 miles from home, and parent has not acquiesced.

Abduct (2)

To restrain a person with intent to prevent his liberation by: (A) secreting or holding him in a place where he is not likely to be found; OR (B) using or threatening to use deadly force.

Relative (3)

A parent or stepparent, ancestor, sibling, or uncle or aunt, including adoptive relatives of the same degree.

Individual (5)

Notwithstanding Sec. 1.07, means a human being who has been born and is alive.

Special Investigator (8)

Includes an agent of the United States Department of Homeland Security.

Sec. 20.02. Unlawful Restraint

  • (a) Offense: A person commits an offense if he intentionally or knowingly restrains another person.
  • (d) Exception: It is no offense if it is for the purpose of a lawful arrest.
  • (b) Affirmative Defense (< 14): Actor is a relative, victim is < 14, and sole intent was to assume lawful control.
  • (e) Affirmative Defense (14-17): Actor is not > 3 years older, victim is 14-17, and actor does not use force, intimidation, or deception.

Punishments (c):

  • Base Offense: Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Victim < 17: State Jail Felony.
  • Recklessly exposes to SBI: Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Restrains Public Servant on duty: Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Actor is in custody: Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Restrains Peace Officer or Judge on duty: Felony of the Second Degree.

Sec. 20.03. Kidnapping

  • (a) Offense: A person commits an offense if he intentionally or knowingly abducts another person. (See 20.01 for "Abduct").
  • (c) Punishment: Felony of the Third Degree.
  • (b) Affirmative Defense: (1) actor was a relative, (2) sole intent was to assume lawful control, AND (3) abduction was not coupled with intent to use/threaten deadly force.

Sec. 20.04. Aggravated Kidnapping

A person commits an offense if they intentionally or knowingly abduct another person:

(a) With specific intent to:

  • (1) Hold for ransom or reward.
  • (2) Use as a shield or hostage.
  • (3) Facilitate a felony or flight after a felony.
  • (4) Inflict bodily injury on him or violate/abuse him sexually.
  • (5) Terrorize him or a third person.
  • (6) Interfere with a governmental or political function.

(b) OR:

  • Uses or exhibits a deadly weapon during the commission.

Punishments (c) & (d):

  • Base Punishment: Felony of the First Degree.
  • Mitigation: At punishment, defendant can prove by preponderance of the evidence that they voluntarily released the victim in a safe place. If proven, offense becomes a Felony of the Second Degree.

Sec. 20.05. Smuggling of Persons

(a) Offense:

A person commits an offense if they knowingly:

  • (1) Use a conveyance to transport an individual with intent to (A) conceal from LEO or (B) flee from LEO.
  • (2) Encourage or induce a person to enter/remain in US in violation of federal law by concealing, harboring, or shielding.
  • (3) Assist, guide, or direct 2 or more individuals onto agricultural land without consent.

(b) Punishments:

  • Base: F3 with a 10-year term.
  • F2 (w/ 10-year min) if:
    • Creates substantial likelihood of SBI/death.
    • Smuggled individual is < 18.
    • Done for pecuniary benefit.
    • Actor possessed a firearm.
    • Actor was fleeing from LEO ((a)(1)(B)).
  • F1 (w/ 10-year min) if:
    • Direct result: victim of Sexual Assault / Agg SA.
    • Direct result: victim suffers SBI / death.

Defenses/Mitigation:

  • (b-1) Mitigation: "Significant cooperation" can reduce F3 min term to 5 years.
  • (b-2) Mitigation: Actor related in 3rd degree (consanguinity/affinity) can reduce F3 min term to 5 years.
  • (c) Affirmative Defense: Actor related in 2nd degree (consanguinity/affinity). (Does not apply to all offenses).

Sec. 20.06. Continuous Smuggling of Persons

  • (a) Offense: Engages 2 or more times in conduct under 20.05, during a period that is 10 or more days in duration.
  • (b) Jury: Jury does not need to be unanimous on specific dates/conduct, only that the actor engaged 2+ times in a 10+ day period.
  • (e) Base Punishment: F2 with a 10-year minimum.
  • (f) Enhancement (F1 w/ 10-year min): Likelihood of SBI/death OR victim < 18.
  • (g) Enhancement (F1 w/ 25-year min): Victim suffers SA / Agg SA / SBI / death.

Sec. 20.07. Operation of Stash House

  • (a) Offense: Knowingly (1) uses or permits use of property to commit/facilitate smuggling/trafficking OR (2) rents or leases property *intending* it be used for this.
  • (b) Base Punishment: F3 with a 5-year minimum.
  • (b) Enhancement (F2 w/ 5-year min): Property used for *Continuous* Smuggling (20.06) or other trafficking offenses OR victim suffers SA / Agg SA / SBI / death.
