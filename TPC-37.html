Ch. 37: Perjury & Other Falsification

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 37.01)

Court record

A decree, judgment, order, subpoena, warrant, minutes, or other document issued by a court (of this state, other state, US, recognized foreign country, Indian tribe, etc.).

Governmental record

Includes: (A) anything belonging to/kept by govt; (B) anything required by law to be kept by others for govt; (C) license, permit, title, etc.; (D) proof of motor vehicle insurance; (E) official ballot; (F) mobile food unit docs; (G) temporary tags.

Statement

Any representation of fact.

Sec. 37.02. Perjury

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if, with intent to deceive and with knowledge of the statement's meaning:
    1. He makes a false statement under oath or swears to the truth of a false statement previously made, AND the statement is required or authorized by law to be made under oath; OR
    2. He makes a false unsworn declaration under Chapter 132, Civil Practice and Remedies Code.
  • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 37.03. Aggravated Perjury

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if he commits Perjury (37.02), AND the false statement:
    1. Is made during or in connection with an official proceeding; AND
    2. Is material.
  • Penalty (b): Felony of the Third Degree.

Sec. 37.04. Materiality

  • (a): A statement is material if it could have affected the course or outcome of the official proceeding (regardless of admissibility).
  • (b): It is no defense to Aggravated Perjury that the declarant mistakenly believed the statement to be immaterial.
  • (c): Whether a statement is material is a question of law.

Sec. 37.05. Retraction

  • Defense to 37.03 (Aggravated Perjury) that the actor retracted his false statement:
    1. Before completion of the testimony at the official proceeding; AND
    2. Before it became manifest that the falsity of the statement would be exposed.

Sec. 37.06. Inconsistent Statements

  • An indictment for perjury/aggravated perjury alleging two statements under oath, both of which cannot be true:
    • Need not allege which statement is false.
    • At trial, the prosecution need not prove which statement is false.

Sec. 37.07. Irregularities No Defense

  • (a): It is no defense that the oath was administered/taken in an irregular manner, or by an irregularly qualified person.
  • (b): It is no defense that a document was not sworn to IF it contains:
    1. A recital it was made under oath;
    2. The declarant was aware of the recital when signing; AND
    3. It contains the signed jurat of a public servant.

Sec. 37.08. False Report to Peace Officer...

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if, with intent to deceive, he knowingly makes a false statement that is material to a criminal investigation AND makes it to:
    1. A peace officer or federal special investigator conducting the investigation;
    2. An employee of a law enforcement agency (authorized + actor knows is conducting); OR
    3. A corrections officer or jailer.
  • Penalty (c): Class B Misdemeanor.

Sec. 37.081. False Report Regarding Missing Child or Missing Person

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if, with intent to deceive, the person knowingly:
    1. Files a false report of a missing child/person with a LE officer/agency; OR
    2. Makes a false statement to a LE officer/employee relating to a missing child/person.
  • Penalty (b): Class C Misdemeanor.

Sec. 37.082. Misrepresenting Child as Family Member at Port of Entry

  • Offense (b): A person commits an offense if, with intent to commit an offense under 20A.02 (Trafficking), the person knowingly misrepresents a child as a family member to a peace officer or federal special investigator at a port of entry.
  • Penalty (c): Class B Misdemeanor.

Sec. 37.09. Tampering With or Fabricating Physical Evidence

  • Offense (a): Knowing an investigation/proceeding is pending or in progress, he:
    1. Alters, destroys, or conceals any record/thing with intent to impair its verity/availability; OR
    2. Makes, presents, or uses any record/thing with knowledge of its falsity and intent to affect the course/outcome.
  • Offense (d): A person commits an offense if:
    1. Knowing an offense has been committed, alters, destroys, or conceals any record/thing with intent to impair its availability in a subsequent investigation; OR
    2. Observes a human corpse (believing offense committed), knows LE is unaware, and fails to report it.
  • Defense (b): Does not apply if the item concealed is privileged or work product.
  • Defense (c-1): Defense to (a) or (d)(1) if the record was visual material prohibited under 43.261 (child pornography) and was destroyed as described by that section.
  • Penalties (c):
    • (a) or (d)(1): Felony of the Third Degree.
    • (a) or (d)(1) + Human Corpse: Felony of the Second Degree.
    • (d)(2) (Fail to Report Corpse): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 37.10. Tampering With Governmental Record

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if he: (1) Knowingly makes false entry/alteration; (2) Makes/uses item w/ knowledge of falsity & intent to be taken as genuine; (3) Intentionally destroys/conceals/removes; (4) Possesses/sells w/ intent to use unlawfully; (5) Makes/uses w/ knowledge of falsity; OR (6) Possesses/sells w/ knowledge it was obtained unlawfully.
  • Exception (b): It is an exception to (a)(3) (destruction) if the record is destroyed pursuant to legal authorization (e.g., Subtitle C, Title 6, Local Govt Code).
  • Penalties (c), (d) (Complex):
    • Base Offense: Class A Misdemeanor.
    • Base + Intent to Defraud/Harm: State Jail Felony.
    • Felony 3rd Degree: If record is (A) public school record, license, permit, title; (B) expert lab report for criminal action; (C) instrument cert report; (D) search warrant.
    • Felony 2nd Degree: If (A) above + intent to defraud/harm.
    • Class C Misdemeanor: If record is for school enrollment residency.
    • Class A Misdemeanor: If record is a temporary tag (c)(6).
    • F3/F2 (d): Specific rules for proof of insurance (37.01(2)(D)). F3 for *making*, F2 if intent to defraud/harm.
    • Class B (d)(1): For *presenting/using* false proof of insurance.
  • Affirmative Defense (e): For possession under (a)(6), it is an affirmative defense that possession occurred in the actual discharge of official duties as a public servant.
  • Defense (f): Defense to (a)(1), (a)(2), (a)(5) if the false entry/info could have no effect on the government's purpose.
  • Presumption (g): Intent to defraud/harm is presumed if actor acts w/ 2 or more of the same type of govt doc (license, title, etc.).
  • Prosecution Overlap (h, i): Actor may be prosecuted under 32.48 or 37.13 for same conduct. AG has concurrent jurisdiction for Medicaid fraud (with local DA consent).
  • "NOT A GOVT DOC" (j): This statement is NOT a defense unless it is printed diagonally, clearly, indelibly, on both front/back, in solid red capital letters at least 1/4 inch high.

Sec. 37.101. Fraudulent Filing of Financing Statement

  • Offense (a): Knowingly files (or causes to be filed) a financing statement that the person knows is: (1) Forged; (2) Contains material false statement; OR (3) Is groundless.
  • Penalties (b):
    • (a)(1) Forged: Felony of the Third Degree (F2 if 2+ priors).
    • (a)(2) False / (a)(3) Groundless: Class A Misdemeanor (SJF if intent to defraud/harm).

Sec. 37.11. Impersonating Public Servant

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if:
    1. Impersonates a public servant with intent to induce another to submit to his pretended official authority or rely on his pretended acts; OR
    2. Knowingly purports to exercise (without legal authority) any function of a public servant or public office.
  • Penalty (b): Felony of the Third Degree.

Sec. 37.12. False Identification as Peace Officer; Misrepresentation of Property

  • Offense (a): (1) Makes, provides, or possesses an item (badge, insignia, vehicle) bearing LE insignia identifying person as a peace officer; AND (2) Knows the person identified is not commissioned.
  • Offense (d): Intentionally or knowingly misrepresents an object (e.g., vehicle) as property belonging to a LE agency.
  • Defense (b): (1) Item clearly identifies as "honorary," "junior," or "junior posse"; OR (2) Person *was* commissioned when item was made.
  • Exception (b-1): Item used *exclusively* for decorative, artistic, or dramatic presentation.
  • Definition (c-1): Item bearing insignia includes items with the word "police," "sheriff," "constable," or "trooper."
  • Penalty (e): Class B Misdemeanor.

Sec. 37.13. Record of a Fraudulent Court

  • Offense (a): Makes, presents, or uses any document/record with:
    1. Knowledge that the document is not a record of a real court (under state/US law); AND
    2. Intent that the document be given the same legal effect as a real court record.
  • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor (becomes F3 if 2 or more prior convictions under this section).
  • Prosecution Overlap (c): If conduct also violates 32.48 or 37.10, actor may be prosecuted under any of those sections.

Sec. 37.14. False Statement Regarding Child Custody Determination Made in Foreign Country

  • Offense (b): A person knowingly makes or causes to be made a false statement relating to a child custody determination made in a foreign country during a hearing (under Ch 152 or Subch I, Ch 153, Family Code).
  • Penalty (c): Felony of the Third Degree.
