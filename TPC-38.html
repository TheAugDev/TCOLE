Ch. 38: Obstructing Governmental Operation

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 38.01)

Custody

(A) Under arrest by peace officer or under restraint by public servant via court order; OR (B) Under restraint by agent/employee of US facility confining arrested/charged/convicted persons.

Escape

Unauthorized departure from custody or failure to return after temporary leave/intermittent sentence. *Excludes* violation of CS/parole conditions (unless it imposes confinement).

Economic benefit

Anything reasonably regarded as economic gain/advantage (accepting employment/fees, fee contracts, money, etc.).

Finance

To provide funds or capital or furnish with necessary funds.

Fugitive from justice

A person for whom a valid arrest warrant has been issued.

Governmental function

Any activity a public servant is lawfully authorized to undertake for government.

Invest funds

To commit money to earn a financial return.

Member of the family

Related within the 3rd degree of consanguinity or affinity (Ch. 573, Govt Code).

Qualified nonprofit organization

Nonprofit meeting specific conditions (primary purpose not legal services, no financial benefit from legal services, person is client not org, etc.).

Public media

Telephone/legal directory, newspaper, billboard, radio/TV, recorded message, or written communication not prohibited by 38.12(d).

Solicit employment

Communicate (in person/phone) with prospective client/family about professional employment arising from occurrence/problem for purpose of providing services, *when not requested*. Excludes family-initiated, prior relationship, qualified nonprofit member education, or public media ads.

Professional

Attorney, chiropractor, physician, surgeon, private investigator, or other person licensed/certified/registered by state agency regulating a health care profession.

Sec. 38.02. Failure to Identify

  • Offense (a): Intentionally refuses to give name, residence address, or DOB to peace officer who has lawfully arrested the person and requested info.
  • Offense (b): Intentionally gives false/fictitious name, residence address, or DOB to peace officer who has: (1) lawfully arrested; (2) lawfully detained; OR (3) requested info from person officer believes is a witness.
  • Offense (b-1): Motor vehicle operator lawfully detained: (2) Fails to provide/display DL on request; AND (3) Intentionally refuses to give name, DL#, address, or DOB on request.
  • Note (b-2): Giving actual current address different from DL address is NOT a refusal under (b-1)(3).
  • Penalties (c):
    • (a) Refusal After Arrest OR (b-1) Operator Refusal: Class C Misdemeanor.
    • (b) False Info After Arrest/Detain/Witness: Class B Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancements (d): If actor is Fugitive From Justice:
    • (a) becomes Class B Misdemeanor.
    • (b) becomes Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancements (d-1): Offense under (b-1) Operator Refusal becomes Class B Misdemeanor if actor gave false/fictitious name.
  • Exception (e): If conduct also violates ABC 106.07 (Minor ID), prosecute only under 106.07.

Sec. 38.03. Resisting Arrest, Search, or Transportation

  • Offense (a): Intentionally prevents/obstructs person known to be peace officer (or person acting at direction) from effecting an arrest, search, or transportation of actor/another by using force against the peace officer or another.
  • No Defense (b): It is no defense that the arrest or search was unlawful.
  • Penalty (c): Base offense is Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (d): Becomes Felony of the Third Degree if actor uses a deadly weapon to resist.

Sec. 38.04. Evading Arrest or Detention

  • Offense (a): Intentionally flees from person known to be peace officer or federal special investigator attempting lawfully to arrest or detain.
  • Penalties (b - first version listed):
    • Base Offense: Class A Misdemeanor.
    • State Jail Felony if: (A) Actor has prior conviction under this section; OR (B) Actor uses vehicle or watercraft AND no prior conviction.
    • Felony of the Third Degree if: (A) Actor uses vehicle or watercraft AND has prior conviction; OR (B) Another suffers SBI as direct result of officer's attempt to apprehend during flight.
    • Felony of the Second Degree if: Another suffers death as direct result of officer's attempt to apprehend during flight.
  • Enhancement (b-1): Felony of the Third Degree if committed during Smuggling of Persons (20.05(a)(2)).
  • Prosecution Overlap (d): May be prosecuted under this section and/or other applicable laws.

Note: There are two conflicting versions of subsection (b) in the official statute due to legislative amendments. The penalties listed above reflect the first version (as amended by Acts 2011, Ch. 839 & Ch. 391).

Sec. 38.05. Hindering Apprehension or Prosecution

  • Offense (a): With intent to hinder arrest/prosecution/etc. of another for an offense (or child for delinquent conduct, or person under warrant): (1) harbors or conceals; (2) provides means of avoiding arrest/escape; OR (3) warns of impending discovery/apprehension.
  • Defense (b): Defense to (a)(3) (warning) if warning was given in connection with effort to bring another into compliance with the law.
  • Penalty (c): Base offense is Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (d): Becomes Felony of the Third Degree if person hindered was under arrest for/charged with/convicted of a felony (or juv equivalent), AND actor knew the person's felony status.

Sec. 38.06. Escape

  • Offense (a): Escapes from custody when person is: (1) Under arrest/detained/charged/convicted of offense; (2) In custody via court order; (3) Detained in secure juvenile facility; OR (4) In custody of JPO for violating juvenile court order.
  • Penalty (b): Base offense is Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (c): Becomes Felony of the Third Degree if actor: (1) Is under arrest/charged/convicted of a felony; (2) Is confined/detained in secure correctional or LE facility; OR (3) Is committed/detained in secure TJJD facility (not halfway house).
  • Enhancement (d): Becomes Felony of the Second Degree if actor causes bodily injury to effect escape.
  • Enhancement (e): Becomes Felony of the First Degree if actor causes SBI or uses/threatens deadly weapon to effect escape.

Sec. 38.07. Permitting or Facilitating Escape

  • Offense (a): Official or employee of correctional facility knowingly permits or facilitates escape of person in custody.
  • Offense (b): Person knowingly causes or facilitates escape of one in custody pursuant to: (1) Juvenile delinquency; OR (2) Involuntary mental health/chemical dependency commitment.
  • Penalty (c): Base offense is Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (d): Becomes Felony of the Third Degree if person in custody: (1) Was under arrest/charged/convicted of felony; OR (2) Was confined (non-secure) after felony conviction.
  • Enhancement (e): Becomes Felony of the Second Degree if: (1) Actor or escapee used/threatened deadly weapon; OR (2) Person in custody was confined in secure facility after felony conviction.

Sec. 38.08. Effect of Unlawful Custody

  • It is no defense to prosecution under 38.06 (Escape) or 38.07 (Permitting/Facilitating) that the custody was unlawful.

Sec. 38.09. Implements for Escape

  • Offense (a): With intent to facilitate escape, introduces into correctional facility, OR provides person in custody/inmate with, a deadly weapon or anything useful for escape.
  • Penalty (b): Base offense is Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Enhancement (b): Becomes Felony of the Second Degree if actor introduced/provided a deadly weapon.

Sec. 38.10. Bail Jumping and Failure to Appear

  • Offense (a): Person lawfully released from custody (w/ or w/o bail) on condition to subsequently appear commits offense if intentionally or knowingly fails to appear per terms of release.
  • Defense (b): Defense if appearance was incident to CS, parole, or intermittent sentence.
  • Defense (c): Defense if actor had reasonable excuse for failure to appear.
  • Penalty (e): Class C Misdemeanor if underlying offense punishable by fine only.
  • Penalty (d): Base offense is Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (f): Becomes Felony of the Third Degree if underlying offense was a felony.

Sec. 38.11. Prohibited Substances and Items in Correctional or Civil Commitment Facility

  • Offense (a): Provides (or possesses w/ intent): (1) Alcohol/CS/Drug (except prescription); (2) Deadly weapon; (3) Cell phone/component; (4) Money (correctional only); (5) Cigarette/Tobacco (correctional only, w/ local jail exceptions).
  • Offense (b): Takes Alcohol/CS/Drug *into* facility.
  • Offense (c): Takes CS/Drug *on property* of facility.
  • Offense (d): Possesses: (1) Alcohol/CS/Drug *in* facility or *on property*; OR (2) Deadly weapon *in* facility.
  • Offense (j): Confined person possesses cell phone/component.
  • Offense (j-1): Civil commitment resident possesses unauthorized cell phone/component.
  • Offense (k): With intent to provide cell phone to confined person: (1) Acquires phone; (2) Provides phone to another for delivery; OR (3) Makes payment to carrier/service.
  • Affirmative Defenses (e): (b/c/d1) Possession via prescription or authorized delivery. (d2) Peace officer or authorized employee possessing weapon.
  • Affirmative Defense (i): (b) Authorized clergy taking <= 4oz alcohol for ritual & consuming/removing all.
  • Penalty (g): Base offense is Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Penalty (g-1): If (a)(1), (b), or (c) committed by *employee* w/ respect to correctional facility, it is Felony of the Second Degree.
  • Penalty (g-2): If (a)(1), (b), or (c) committed by *employee*, *causes death* of inmate via CS/drug, it is Felony of the First Degree (15-99/Life, $250k fine).
  • Attempt (h): Criminal attempt of (a), (b), or (c) is Felony of the Third Degree.

Sec. 38.111. Improper Contact With Victim

  • Offense (a): Person confined (for 42.072 or Art 62.001(5) offense) contacts victim/family without prior written consent (obtained via facility director).
  • Consent Rules (a,b): Requires specific consent process; confined person cannot give consent for victim contact.
  • Affirmative Defense (c): Indirect contact via attorney solely for representing person in criminal proceeding.
  • Penalty (d): Base offense is Class A Misdemeanor. Becomes Felony of the Third Degree if actor confined after felony conviction (for the underlying offense).

Sec. 38.112. Tampering With Electronic Monitoring Device

  • Offense (a): Person required to submit to electronic monitoring knowingly removes/disables (or conspires/cooperates to) the tracking device.
  • Penalty (b): Base offense is State Jail Felony. Becomes Felony of the Third Degree if person is in super-intensive supervision program (SISP).
  • Exception (c): Does not apply if removed/disabled by health care provider due to medical necessity.

Sec. 38.113. Unauthorized Absence From Community Corrections Facility...

  • Offense (a): Person required to be at CCF/county center fails to report to or leaves facility/center/assignment site as directed, without approval.
  • Penalty (b): State Jail Felony.

Sec. 38.114. Contraband in Correctional Facility

  • Offense (a): (1) Provides contraband to inmate; (2) Introduces contraband into facility; OR (3) Possesses contraband while confined.
  • "Contraband" (b): Item not provided/authorized OR authorized item altered. *Excludes* items specifically prohibited under 38.11 (drugs, weapons, phones, etc.).
  • Penalty (c): Base offense is Class C Misdemeanor. Becomes Class B Misdemeanor if committed by employee or volunteer.

Sec. 38.115. Operation of Unmanned Aircraft Over Correctional/Detention Facility

  • Offense (b): Intentionally/knowingly: (1) Operates drone over facility (<= 400ft AGL); (2) Allows drone to contact facility; OR (3) Allows drone close enough to interfere/disturb.
  • Exceptions (c): Does not apply to govt, contractors, persons w/ consent, LE agencies.
  • Penalties (d): Base is Class B Misdemeanor. Becomes Class A Misdemeanor if prior conviction. Becomes State Jail Felony if used to provide/introduce contraband.

Sec. 38.12. Barratry and Solicitation of Professional Employment

  • Offense (a): With intent for economic benefit: (1) Institutes unauthorized suit; (2) Solicits employment; (3) Pays client to obtain employment; (4) Pays person to solicit; (5) Pays family member to solicit; (6) Accepts payment to solicit.
  • Offense (b): (1) Finances (a); (2) Invests funds for (a); OR (3) Professional knowingly accepts employment from solicitation violating (a).
  • Offense (d): Licensed professional (atty, Dr, PI, etc.) w/ intent for employment provides communication/solicitation concerning specific prohibited matters (e.g., recent accident, client already represented, involves coercion/fraud, etc.).
  • Exception (c): Conduct authorized by TX Disciplinary Rules or court rule.
  • Penalty (f): Offense under (a) or (b) is Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Penalty (g): Offense under (d) is Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (h): Offense under (d) becomes Felony of the Third Degree if prior conviction under (d).

Sec. 38.122. Falsely Holding Oneself Out as a Lawyer

  • Offense (a): With intent for economic benefit, holds self out as lawyer, unless currently licensed & in good standing.
  • Penalty (b): Felony of the Third Degree.

Sec. 38.123. Unauthorized Practice of Law

  • Offense (a): With intent for economic benefit: (1) Contracts to represent re personal injury/property damage; (2) Advises re rights/claims; (3) Advises re settlement; (4) Enters contingent fee contract w/ assignment; (5) Enters contract granting exclusive right to select counsel.
  • Exception (b): Does not apply to person currently licensed & in good standing.
  • Penalty (c): Base offense is Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (d): Becomes Felony of the Third Degree if prior conviction under (a).

Sec. 38.13. Hindering Proceedings by Disorderly Conduct

  • Offense (a): Intentionally hinders official proceeding by noise/violent/tumultuous behavior.
  • Offense (b): Recklessly hinders official proceeding by noise/violent/tumultuous behavior AND continues after explicit official request to desist.
  • Penalty (c): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 38.14. Taking or Attempting to Take Weapon From Peace Officer...

  • Offense (b): Intentionally/knowingly with force takes/attempts to take from PO, Fed Inv, correctional employee, parole/CSCD officer, commissioned security officer their firearm, nightstick, stun gun, or chemical spray.
  • Presumption (c): Actor presumed to know person's status if in uniform/badge or if identified.
  • Defense (d): Defense if officer was using excessive force.
  • Penalties (e): (1) Taking weapon: Felony of the Third Degree. (2) Attempting to take: State Jail Felony.

Sec. 38.15. Interference With Public Duties

  • Offense (a): With criminal negligence interrupts/disrupts/impedes/interferes with: (1) Peace officer; (2) EMS; (3) Firefighter; (4) LE animal; (5) Emergency CB radio; (6) Animal control officer; (7) Public health/env/safety official; (8) Utility employee/agent.
  • Penalty (b): Class B Misdemeanor.
  • Defense (c): Defense to (a)(1) if conduct was intended to warn driver of PO enforcing traffic law.
  • Defense (d): Defense if interference consisted of speech only.
  • Presumption (d-1): Rebuttable presumption of interference if actor intentionally disseminates officer's home address/phone/SSN etc. (Exceptions d-2 for media).

Sec. 38.151. Interference With Police Service Animals

  • Offense (b): Recklessly: (1) Taunts/torments/strikes; (2) Throws object/substance at; (3) Interferes/obstructs animal or handler; (4) Releases animal; (5) Enters area of control; (6) Injures/kills; (7) Engages in conduct likely to injure/kill.
  • Penalties (c): Ranges from Class C Misd (taunting) up to Felony 2nd Degree (killing/permanently injuring).

Sec. 38.152. Interference With Radio Frequency Licensed to Government Entity

  • Offense (a): Without effective consent, intentionally interrupts/disrupts/impedes/jams/interferes with radio freq licensed to govt & used by LE/Fire/EMS.
  • Penalty (b): Base offense is Class A Misdemeanor. Becomes State Jail Felony if intent to facilitate another offense OR interfere with emergency response.

Sec. 38.16. Preventing Execution of Civil Process

  • Offense (a): Intentionally/knowingly by words or physical action prevents execution of process in a civil cause.
  • Exception (b): Exception if actor evaded service by avoiding detection.
  • Penalty (c): Class C Misdemeanor.

Sec. 38.17. Failure to Stop or Report Sexual or Assaultive Offense Against Child

  • Offense (a): Observes commission/attempt of sex/assault offense against child (reasonable belief), fails to assist child OR immediately report to LE, AND could do so without danger of SBI/death.
  • Penalty (b): Felony of the Third Degree.

Sec. 38.171. Failure to Report Felony

  • Offense (a): Observes felony where reasonable person believes SBI/death may have resulted, AND fails to immediately report to LE when reasonable person believes not reported AND could report without danger of SBI/death.
  • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 38.172. Failure to Report Assault, Neglect, or Omission of Care in Certain Group Homes

  • Offense (b): Has actual knowledge resident of group home suffered BI due to assault/neglect/omission, AND fails to report to LE or DFPS.
  • Exceptions (c): Applies to specific licensed persons/facilities.
  • Penalty (d): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 38.18. Use of Collision Report Information... for Pecuniary Gain

  • Offense (b): Obtains collision/EMS/911 info from govt entity AND subsequently uses it for direct solicitation of business for pecuniary gain.
  • Offense (c): Employs another to obtain info AND subsequently uses it for direct solicitation for pecuniary gain.
  • Penalty (d): Class B Misdemeanor.

Sec. 38.19. Failure to Provide Notice and Report of Death of Resident of Institution

  • Offense (a): Superintendent/manager of institution fails to provide notice, submit report, or include material facts in report regarding death of resident as required by CCP Art. 49.24/49.25 (or future 49A.151/152).
  • Penalty (b): Class B Misdemeanor.
