Sec. 21.01+ Key Definitions

Definitions from 21.01 and other sections within Chapter 21.

Deviate Sexual Intercourse (21.01)

(A) Contact between genitals of one person and the mouth or anus of another; OR (B) Penetration of the genitals or anus of another with an object.

Sexual Contact (21.01)

Any touching of the anus, breast, or any part of the genitals of another person with intent to arouse or gratify the sexual desire of any person. (Note special definitions in 21.11/21.12).

Sexual Intercourse (21.01)

Any penetration of the female sex organ by the male sex organ.

Spouse (21.01)

A person to whom a person is legally married.

Intimate Area (21.15)

The naked or clothed genitals, pubic area, anus, buttocks, or female breast of a person.

Female Breast (21.15)

Any portion of the female breast below the top of the areola.

Changing Room (21.15)

A room or portioned area provided for or primarily used for changing clothes (includes dressing rooms, locker rooms, swimwear changing areas).

Intimate Parts (21.16)

The naked genitals, pubic area, anus, buttocks, or female nipple of a person.

Sexual Conduct (21.16)

Sexual contact, actual or simulated sexual intercourse, deviate sexual intercourse, sexual bestiality, masturbation, or sadomasochistic abuse.

Simulated (21.16)

Explicit depiction creating appearance of actual sexual conduct, during which a person exhibits uncovered breasts, genitals, or buttocks.

Deep Fake Media (21.165)

Visual depiction created/altered by software/AI that looks real but depicts a person doing something that didn't occur in reality.

Visual Depiction (21.165)

Photograph, film, videotape, digital image/video, or other visual recording.

Place w/ Reasonable Expectation of Privacy (21.15)

A place where a reasonable person would believe they could disrobe in privacy without being photographed, recorded, or broadcasted. Includes bathrooms, bedrooms, and changing rooms.

Promote (21.16)

Procure, manufacture, issue, sell, give, provide, lend, mail, deliver, transfer, transmit, publish, distribute, circulate, disseminate, present, exhibit, or advertise, or offer/agree to do any of the above.

Visual Material (21.16)

(A) Film, photograph, videotape, negative, slide, or reproduction thereof; OR (B) Disk, diskette, other physical medium, or digital file allowing image display on a screen, or image transmitted electronically.

Sec. 21.02. Continuous Sexual Abuse of Young Child or Disabled Individual

  • Offense: Actor (17+) commits 2 or more "acts of sexual abuse" during a 30+ day period.
  • Victim: (A) A child younger than 14 (regardless of actor's knowledge of age) OR (B) A disabled individual.
  • "Act of Sexual Abuse" (c): Includes specific violations of: 20.04(a)(4)(Agg Kid w/ sex intent), 21.11(a)(1)(Indecency by contact, excluding breast touch), 22.011 (SA), 22.021 (Agg SA), 30.02 (Burglary w/ sex intent), 43.25 (Sexual Performance), 20A.02(a)(3,4,7,8)(Trafficking), 43.05 (Compelling Prostitution).
  • Jury Rule (d): Jury must be unanimous that 2+ acts occurred in 30+ days, but not on the specific acts or dates.
  • Charging Rule (e): Cannot convict of both 21.02 AND an underlying offense against the same victim in the same action unless charged in alternative, occurred outside the period, or lesser included.
  • Charging Rule (f): Cannot charge more than one count if all acts are against a single victim.
  • Affirmative Defense (g): (Applies to < 14 victims only) Actor was (1) not more than 5 years older, (2) used no duress/force/threat, AND (3) was not a registered sex offender (for life) or had prior conviction under this section or for an underlying "act of sexual abuse".
  • Punishment (h): Felony of the First Degree (25-Life / 25-99).

Sec. 21.03. Continuous Sexual Abuse

  • Offense: Actor (17+) commits 2 or more "acts of sexual abuse" during a 30+ day period against 2 or more victims.
  • "Act of Sexual Abuse" (b): Same "laundry list" as Sec. 21.02.
  • Jury Rule (c): Jury must be unanimous that 2+ acts occurred against 2+ victims in 30+ days, but not on specifics.
  • Charging Rule (d): Cannot convict of both 21.03 AND an underlying offense against the same victim in the same action unless charged in alternative, occurred outside the period, or lesser included.
  • Charging Rule (e): Cannot charge more than one count if all acts are against only two victims.
  • Affirmative Defense (f): (Applies if *all* victims < 17 only) Actor was (1) not more than 5 years older than the youngest victim, (2) used no duress/force/threat, AND (3) was not a registered sex offender (for life) or had prior conviction under this section or for an underlying "act of sexual abuse".
  • Punishment (g): Felony of the First Degree (Ordinary).

Sec. 21.11. Indecency with a Child

Commits offense if, with a child younger than 17 (regardless of age knowledge), the person:

  • (a)(1): Engages in "sexual contact" with the child or causes the child to engage in sexual contact. (F2)
  • (a)(2): With intent to arouse/gratify: (A) exposes their anus/genitals, knowing child is present; OR (B) causes child to expose their anus/genitals. (F3)

Special Definition of "Sexual Contact" (c)

For *this section*, "sexual contact" means (1) touching (incl. through clothing) the child's anus/breast/genitals or (2) touching any part of the child's body (incl. through clothing) with the actor's anus/breast/genitals, IF committed with intent to arouse or gratify.

Affirmative Defenses

  • (b): Actor was (1) not more than 3 years older AND of the opposite sex, (2) used no duress/force/threat, AND (3) was not a registered sex offender (for life) or had prior reportable conviction under Ch 62.
  • (b-1): The actor was the spouse of the child.

Sec. 21.12. Improper Relationship Between Educator and Student

An employee of a public/private primary or secondary school commits an offense if they:

  • (a)(1): Engage in sexual contact/intercourse/DSI with a student enrolled at their school.
  • (a)(2): Hold a specific role (teacher, coach, counselor, etc., regardless of certification) AND engage in sexual contact/intercourse/DSI with a person they know is a student at another school OR a student participant in a sponsored educational activity.
  • (a)(3): Engage in Online Solicitation of a Minor (33.021) conduct with any such student.

Special Definition of "Sexual Contact" (e)

For *this section*, similar to 21.11(c), requires touching specified areas with intent to arouse or gratify.

Punishment, Defenses, & Other Rules

  • Punishment (b): Felony of the Second Degree (F2).
  • Affirmative Defense (b-1): (1) The actor was the spouse of the student; OR (2) Actor was not more than 3 years older AND the relationship began *before* the actor's employment.
  • Prosecution Rule (c): Actor may be prosecuted under this section or other sections (or both).
  • Confidentiality (d): The student's name is not public information.
  • Confidentiality (d-1): The employee's name may not be released externally to the general public until indictment (with exceptions for reporting to agencies/LE or internal investigations).

Sec. 21.07. Public Lewdness

  • Offense: Person knowingly engages in an act of (1) sexual intercourse, (2) deviate sexual intercourse, or (3) sexual contact in a public place.
  • Alternate Offense: If not in a public place, person is reckless about whether another is present who will be offended or alarmed.
  • Punishment: Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement: Felony of the Third Degree if the actor is a civilly committed sexually violent predator (SVP).

Sec. 21.08. Indecent Exposure

  • Offense: Person (1) exposes anus or any part of genitals (2) with intent to arouse or gratify sexual desire, AND (3) is reckless about whether another is present who will be offended or alarmed.
  • Punishment: Class B Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancements:
    • Class A Misdemeanor if 1 prior conviction under this section.
    • State Jail Felony if 2 or more prior convictions under this section.
    • Felony of the Third Degree if the actor is a civilly committed SVP.

Sec. 21.17. Voyeurism

  • Offense: Person (1) with intent to arouse or gratify sexual desire, (2) observes (including remotely through electronic means) (3) another person without consent (4) while the person is in a dwelling or structure where they have a reasonable expectation of privacy.
  • Punishment: Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Enhancement (SJF): Offense is a State Jail Felony if: (1) actor has a prior conviction under this section, OR (2) victim was child < 18, OR (3) offense committed on postsecondary (college) premises.
  • Enhancement (F3): Offense is a Felony of the Third Degree if: (1) victim was child < 18 AND (2) actor has a prior conviction for victim < 18 under (c)(2).
  • Prosecution Rule (e): Actor may be prosecuted under this section or other laws (or both).

Sec. 21.15. Invasive Visual Recording

Person commits offense if, without consent and with intent to invade privacy, the person:

  • (b)(1): Records/broadcasts/transmits a visual image of an intimate area of a person who has a reasonable expectation that the area is not subject to public view.
  • (b)(2): Records/broadcasts/transmits *any* visual image of a person in a place with a reasonable expectation of privacy (e.g., bathroom, changing room).
  • (b)(3): Knowing the character/content, promotes a recording/broadcast/transmission from (b)(1) or (b)(2).

Key Rule (e): Posting a sign saying "you are being recorded" is NOT sufficient to establish consent.

Prosecution Rule (d): Actor may be prosecuted under this section or other laws (or both).

Punishment (c): State Jail Felony.

Sec. 21.16. Unlawful Disclosure... (Revenge Porn)

  • Offense (Disclosure) (b): Person (1) without consent & with intent to harm, (2) discloses visual material of intimate parts/sexual conduct, (3) knowing the material was private, (4) the disclosure causes harm, AND (5) it reveals the victim's identity (incl. via accompanying info or info provided by 3rd party).
  • Offense (Threat) (c): Intentionally threatens to disclose such material to obtain a benefit (in return for not disclosing, or in connection with the threat).
  • Offense (Promotion) (d): Knowing character/content, promotes such material on an Internet website/forum they own/operate.
  • No Defense (e): It is NOT a defense that the victim (1) consented to the CREATION of the material, or (2) voluntarily SENT the material to the actor.
  • Affirmative Defense (f): Disclosure/promotion was for (A) lawful LE/medical purpose, (B) reporting unlawful activity, (C) legal proceeding; OR actor is an ISP; OR it depicts voluntary exposure in a PUBLIC or COMMERCIAL setting.
  • Prosecution Rule (h): Actor may be prosecuted under this section or other laws (or both).
  • Punishment (g): State Jail Felony.

Sec. 21.165. Unlawful Production... (Deep Fake)

  • Offense (Produce/Distribute) (b): Knowingly produces or distributes by electronic means "deep fake media" without effective consent that appears to depict a real person with fake intimate parts or engaging in sexual conduct they did not engage in.
    • Punishment (c): Class A Misdemeanor. (Enhanced to F3 if prior or victim < 18).
  • Offense (Threat) (b-1): Intentionally threatens to produce/distribute deep fake media with intent to coerce/extort/harass/intimidate.
    • Punishment (c-1): Class B Misdemeanor. (Enhanced to Class A if prior or victim < 18).
  • Consent Rule (b-2): Effective consent must be written, signed, plain language, describe media, and describe audiovisual work if applicable.
  • No Defense (c-2): It is NOT a defense that the media contained a disclaimer or label indicating it's fake/unauthorized.
  • Affirmative Defense (c-3): Production/distribution in course of lawful LE, reporting crime, or legal proceeding.
  • Affirmative Defense (c-4): Actor is ISP, cloud provider, etc., acting solely in technical/automatic nature.
  • Affirmative Defense (c-5): Actor is AI developer who prohibited creation in user policies AND took affirmative steps to prevent/filter such content.
  • Prosecution Rule (d): Actor may be prosecuted under this section or other laws.
  • Restitution (e): Court shall order restitution for psychological, financial, or reputational harm.

Sec. 21.18. Sexual Coercion

  • Offense (b): Intentionally threatens (incl. coercion/extortion) to commit a specified sex/disclosure offense (e.g., Ch 43, 20A.02(a)(3+), 21.02, 21.03, 21.08, 21.11, 21.12, 21.15, 21.16, 21.17, 22.011, 22.021) to get a benefit of (1) intimate visual material, (2) sexual conduct, OR (3) monetary benefit/other benefit of value.
  • Offense (c): Intentionally threatens (incl. coercion/extortion) to commit a specified violence/trafficking offense (Ch 19, Ch 20, 20A.02(a)(1,2,5,6)) to get a benefit of (1) intimate visual material OR (2) sexual conduct.
  • Communication (d): Threat applies regardless of how communicated (incl. electronic means).
  • Punishment (e): State Jail Felony. (Enhanced to F3 if prior conviction under this section).

Sec. 21.19. Unlawful Electronic Transmission... (Cyberflashing)

  • Offense (b): Person knowingly transmits by electronic means visual material that:
    • (1) Depicts (A) sexual conduct or intimate parts exposed, OR (B) covered genitals of a male person that are in a discernibly turgid state; AND
    • (2) Is not sent at the request or with the express consent of the recipient.
  • Prosecution Rule (d): Actor may be prosecuted under this section or other laws (or both).
  • Punishment (c): Class C Misdemeanor.

Sec. 21.06. Homosexual Conduct

  • Offense: Engages in deviate sexual intercourse with another individual of the same sex.
  • Punishment: Class C Misdemeanor.
  • Note: This statute was declared unconstitutional by the U.S. Supreme Court in *Lawrence v. Texas* (2003) and is unenforceable.

Sec. 21.09. Bestiality

  • Offense (a): Knowingly engages in, causes, permits, promotes, possesses/transfers/advertises animal for, or observes any of the following:
    • (1) Contact between person's genitals/mouth/anus and animal's genitals/anus/mouth.
    • (2) Fondling animal's genitals/anus (not for husbandry/vet practice).
    • (3) Causing animal to contact seminal fluid of the person.
    • (4) Inserting object/body part into animal's anus/genitals (not for husbandry/vet practice).
    • (8) Permitting conduct on premises under person's control.
    • (9) Committing any of these acts in the presence of a child < 18.
  • Exception (c): Conduct is a generally accepted and otherwise lawful animal husbandry or veterinary practice.
  • Punishment (b): State Jail Felony.
  • Enhancement (b): Felony of the Second Degree if committed under (a)(9) (presence of child) OR results in SBI/death of the animal.
