Ch. 42: Disorderly Conduct & Related Offenses

Back to Main Page

Sec. 42.01. Disorderly Conduct

  • Offense (a): Person I/K:
    1. Uses abusive/indecent/profane/vulgar language in public place & it tends to incite immediate breach of peace ("Fighting Words").
    2. Makes offensive gesture/display in public place & it tends to incite immediate breach of peace.
    3. Creates noxious/unreasonable odor (chemical) in public place.
    4. Abuses/threatens person in public place in obviously offensive manner. (Defense: Significant provocation (b)).
    5. Makes unreasonable noise in public place (not shooting range) or near private residence they can't occupy. (Presumed unreasonable if > 85dB after notice (c)(2)).
    6. Fights with another in public place.
    7. Discharges firearm in public place (not public road or shooting range).
    8. Displays firearm/DW in public place in manner calculated to alarm.
    9. Discharges firearm on or across public road.
    10. Exposes anus/genitals in public place & is reckless if another offended/alarmed.
    11. For lewd/unlawful purpose: (A) Enters property & looks into dwelling; (B) Looks into hotel guest room (not own); (C) Looks into private area (restroom, etc.) in public place.
  • Penalties (d):
    • (a)(7) [Discharge Firearm Public Place] or (a)(8) [Display Firearm Alarmingly]: Class B Misdemeanor.
    • All others: Class C Misdemeanor.
  • Public Place (a-1): Includes public school campus/grounds.
  • Defense (e): For (a)(7) & (a)(9) [Discharge Firearm], reasonable fear of BI from dangerous wild animal.
  • Exception (e-1): (a)(9) [Discharge Across Road] doesn't apply to PWD employee on duty per PWC 12.013(c).
  • Exception (f): Subsections (a)(1, 2, 3, 5, 6) do NOT apply to student < 12 on public school campus during school hours.
  • Exception (g): Lawful space flight activities noise is not "unreasonable noise".

Sec. 42.02. Riot

  • "Riot" Definition (a): Assemblage of 7 or more persons resulting in conduct which: (1) Creates immediate danger of property damage/injury; (2) Substantially obstructs LE/gov functions; OR (3) By force/threat/action deprives person of legal right or disturbs enjoyment of right.
  • Offense (b): Person knowingly participates in a riot.
  • Penalties (e, f):
    • Base Offense: Class B Misdemeanor.
    • Enhanced Offense (f): Same classification as any higher grade offense committed by *anyone* engaged in the riot IF offense was in furtherance of assembly's purpose OR should have been anticipated.
  • Defense (c): Assembly was initially lawful & actor retired when someone manifested intent to engage in riotous conduct.
  • No Defense (d): Not a defense that another party was acquitted, not arrested, immune, etc.

Sec. 42.03. Obstructing Highway or Other Passageway

  • Offense (a): Without legal privilege/authority, person I/K/R: (1) Obstructs public highway, street, sidewalk, railway, waterway, elevator, aisle, hallway, entrance, exit, etc.; OR (2) Disobeys reasonable request/order to move from peace officer/firefighter/person w/ authority to prevent obstruction OR maintain safety near hazard.
  • "Obstruct" Definition (b): Render impassable or unreasonably inconvenient or hazardous.
  • Penalties (c, c-1, d, e):
    • Base Offense: Class B Misdemeanor.
    • Enhancement (c-1): State Jail Felony if actor knowingly prevents passage of authorized emergency vehicle w/ signals OR obstructs access to hospital/emergency facility.
    • Enhancement (d): Class A Misdemeanor if operating motor vehicle during reckless driving exhibition.
    • Enhancement (e): State Jail Felony if operating motor vehicle during reckless driving exhibition AND has prior under (d), OR was intoxicated, OR caused BI.
  • "Reckless Driving Exhibition" (f): Operator intentionally breaks traction, spins tires, rotates vehicle on highway/street in presence of 2+ spectators.
  • Prosecution Note (g): If conduct also violates another law, can be prosecuted under either, but not both.

Sec. 42.04. Defense When Conduct Consists of Speech or Other Expression

  • Applicability (a): Applies if conduct violating 42.01(a)(5) [Noise], 42.03 [Obstruction], or 42.055 [Funeral] consists of speech, communication, gathering to hear/observe, or nonviolent picketing/expression.
  • Requirement (a): Actor must be ordered to move/disperse/remedy *before* arrest if they haven't yet intentionally harmed interests protected by those sections.
  • Who Can Order (b): Peace officer, fireman, person w/ authority over premises, or person directly affected.
  • Defense (c): It is a defense if: (1) Required order not given; (2) Order was manifestly unreasonable; OR (3) Order was promptly obeyed.

Sec. 42.05. Disrupting Meeting or Procession

  • Offense (a): With intent to prevent/disrupt lawful meeting/procession/gathering (in person or virtual), obstructs/interferes by: (1) Physical action; (2) Verbal utterance; OR (3) Electronic disturbance (hacking) of virtual component.
  • Penalty (b): Class B Misdemeanor.

Sec. 42.055. Funeral Service Disruptions

  • "Picketing" (a)(3): Includes displaying signs, loud chanting/yelling, or blocking access.
  • Offense (b): Engages in picketing within 1,000 feet of facility/cemetery used for funeral service during period 3 hours before to 3 hours after service.
  • Penalty (c): Class B Misdemeanor.

Sec. 42.06. False Alarm or Report

  • Offense (a): Knowingly initiates/communicates/circulates report of bombing/fire/offense/emergency that actor knows is false/baseless AND ordinarily would: (1) Cause action by emergency agency; (2) Place person in fear of imminent SBI; OR (3) Prevent/interrupt occupation of building, public place, conveyance.
  • Penalties (b):
    • Base Offense: Class A Misdemeanor.
    • Enhanced Offense: State Jail Felony IF report involves higher ed institution, public school, public comms/transport/utilities.

Sec. 42.0601. False Report to Induce Emergency Response (Swatting)

  • Offense (a): Person makes/causes false report of crime/emergency to authorized entity, knows report is false, report causes emergency response, AND person is reckless whether response may directly result in BI to another.
  • Penalties (b):
    • Base Offense: Class A Misdemeanor.
    • Enhancement (b)(1): State Jail Felony if 2+ prior convictions under this section.
    • Enhancement (b)(2): Felony of the Third Degree if report was of crime, LE/emergency responded, AND person suffered SBI/death as direct result of lawful conduct from that response.

Sec. 42.061. Silent or Abusive Calls to 9-1-1 Service

  • Offense (b): Makes call/request to 9-1-1 when no emergency & K/I: (1) Remains silent; OR (2) Makes abusive/harassing statements to PSAP employee.
  • Offense (c): Knowingly permits device under control to be used by another for (b).
  • Penalty (d): Class B Misdemeanor.

Sec. 42.062. Interference With Emergency Request for Assistance

  • Offense (a): Knowingly prevents/interferes with another's ability to place emergency call/request for assistance.
  • Offense (b): Recklessly renders unusable electronic communication device that would otherwise be used for emergency call/request.
  • Penalties (c):
    • Base Offense: Class A Misdemeanor.
    • Enhanced Offense: State Jail Felony if actor has previous conviction under this section.
  • "Emergency" Definition (d): Condition where individual fears imminent assault OR property in imminent danger.

Sec. 42.07. Harassment

  • Offense (a): With intent to harass, annoy, alarm, abuse, torment, or embarrass another, person:
    1. Initiates communication & makes obscene comment/request/etc.
    2. Threatens BI or felony (manner likely to alarm).
    3. Conveys false report of death/SBI (manner likely to alarm).
    4. Causes phone to ring repeatedly / makes repeated anonymous calls (manner likely to harass, etc.).
    5. Makes call & intentionally fails to hang up.
    6. Knowingly permits phone under control to be used for (1-5).
    7. Sends repeated electronic communications (manner likely to harass, etc.).
    8. Publishes repeated electronic communications online (manner likely to cause emotional distress, etc.; unless public concern).
    9. Tracks/monitors personal property/vehicle without consent (using app/device OR physically following).
    10. Makes obscene/intimidating/threatening calls/comms from temp/disposable number.
  • Penalties (c):
    • Base Offense: Class B Misdemeanor.
    • Enhanced Offense: Class A Misdemeanor IF: (1) Prior conviction under this section; OR (2) Offense under (a)(7)/(8) against child < 18 w/ intent child commit suicide/SBI OR actor previously violated cyberbullying TRO; OR (3) Offense against known utility worker on duty. (Future enhancements for court employees/judges noted but not yet effective).
  • "Obscene" Definition (b)(3): Patently offensive description/solicitation of ultimate sex act or description of excretory function.
  • Tracking Presumption (e): No effective consent presumed if PO filed/issued against actor OR parties are married w/ divorce filed / previously married & divorced.

Sec. 42.072. Stalking

  • Offense (a): On more than one occasion & pursuant to same scheme/course of conduct directed at specific person, knowingly engages in conduct that:
    1. Constitutes offense under 42.07 (Harassment) OR actor knows/should know victim will regard as threatening BI/death or offense against family/household/dating partner/property; AND
    2. Causes victim (or family/household/dating partner) to be placed in fear of BI/death/offense against person/property OR feel harassed/terrified/intimidated/etc.; AND
    3. Would cause reasonable person to fear BI/death/offense against person/property OR feel harassed/terrified/intimidated/etc.
  • Penalties (b):
    • Base Offense: Felony of the Third Degree.
    • Enhanced Offense: Felony of the Second Degree if actor previously convicted of Stalking (this section or substantially similar law from another jurisdiction).
  • Course of Conduct (c): Trier of fact may find different types of conduct under (a) engaged in >1 occasion constitute same scheme/course.
  • Property (d)(2): Includes pet, companion animal, or assistance animal.

Sec. 42.074. Unlawful Disclosure of Residence Address or Telephone Number

  • Offense (a): Person posts on publicly accessible website OR discloses via electronic communication residence address/phone # of individual with intent to cause harm/threat of harm to individual or family/household member.
  • Penalties (b):
    • Base Offense: Class B Misdemeanor.
    • Enhanced Offense: Class A Misdemeanor if offense results in BI to individual or family/household member.
  • Exception (c): Does not apply to public servant posting/disclosing in performance of duties required by law.

Sec. 42.075. Disclosure of Confidential Information Regarding Family Violence or Victims of Trafficking Shelter Center

  • Offense (b): With intent to threaten safety of any inhabitant, discloses or publicizes location or physical layout of family violence or victims of trafficking shelter center.
  • Penalty (c): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 42.09. Cruelty to Livestock Animals

  • "Livestock Animal" Definition (b)(5): Cattle, sheep, swine, goats, ratites, poultry (human consumption); horse, pony, mule, donkey, hinny; native/nonnative hoofstock (ag practices); native/nonnative fowl (ag practices).
  • Offenses (a): Person I/K: (1) Tortures; (2) Unreasonably fails to provide necessary food/water/care; (3) Unreasonably abandons; (4) Transports/confines cruelly; (5) Poisons (not cattle/horse/etc.) belonging to another; (6) Causes fight w/ another livestock/animal; (7) Uses live lure for dogs; (8) Trips horse; (9) Seriously overworks.
  • Penalties (c):
    • (a)(2, 3, 4, 9) - Failure/Abandon/Transport/Overwork: Class A Misdemeanor (SJF if 2+ priors under 42.09/42.092).
    • (a)(1, 5, 6, 7, 8) - Torture/Poison/Fight/Lure/Trip: State Jail Felony (F3 if 2+ priors under 42.09/42.092).
  • Defenses: (d) For tripping horse: ID ownership or vet care. (e) Bona fide scientific research.
  • Exceptions (f): Generally accepted/lawful fishing/hunting/trapping, wildlife management/control, or animal husbandry/ag practices.

Sec. 42.091. Attack on Assistance Animal

  • "Assistance Animal" Definition (e)(1): Per HRC 121.002.
  • Offense (a): Person I/K/R attacks, injures, or kills assistance animal.
  • Offense (b): Person I/K/R incites/permits own animal to attack/injure/kill assistance animal, and it does.
  • Penalties (c):
    • Attack: Class A Misdemeanor.
    • Injury: State Jail Felony.
    • Kill: Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Restitution (d): Court shall order restitution for vet bills, replacement/retraining costs, other reasonable expenses.

Sec. 42.092. Cruelty to Nonlivestock Animals

  • "Animal" Definition (a)(2): Domesticated living creature (incl stray/feral cat/dog) & wild creature previously captured. Excludes uncaptured wild creatures & livestock.
  • Offenses (b): Person I/K/R/CN: (1) Tortures or cruelly kills/causes SBI; (2) Kills/poisons/causes SBI to another's animal w/o consent; (3) Unreasonably fails to provide necessary food/water/care/shelter; (4) Unreasonably abandons; (5) Transports/confines cruelly; (6) Causes BI to another's animal w/o consent; (7) Causes non-dog animal fight; (8) Uses live lure for dogs; (9) Seriously overworks.
  • Penalties (c, c-1, c-2):
    • (b)(3, 4, 5, 6, 9) - Failure/Abandon/Transport/BI/Overwork: Class A Misdemeanor (SJF if 2+ priors under 42.09/42.092).
    • (b)(1, 2) - Torture/Cruel Kill/SBI / Kill/Poison/SBI Another's: Felony of the Third Degree (F2 if prior under b1, b2, b7, b8 or 42.09).
    • (b)(7, 8) - Non-Dog Fight / Live Lure: State Jail Felony (F3 if prior under this section or 42.09).
  • Defenses: (d) Reasonable fear of BI from dangerous wild animal; Bona fide scientific research; (d-1) Vet/assistant acting in scope of duties (defense to CN only); (e) Animal discovered injuring/killing livestock or crops; Actor was public servant/utility worker in scope of employment; (e-1) Releasing stray/feral per Trap-Neuter-Return Program; (e-2) Releasing trapped wild creature per wildlife laws.
  • Exceptions (f): Same as 42.09(f) (fishing/hunting/trapping, wildlife management, animal husbandry).

Sec. 42.10. Dog Fighting

  • Offenses (a): Person I/K: (1) Causes dog to fight another dog; (2) Participates in earnings/operates facility; (3) Uses/permits property use; (4) Possesses dog-fighting equipment w/ intent; (5) Owns/trains dog w/ intent; (6) Attends as spectator.
  • Penalties (e):
    • (a)(4, 5, 6) - Equip/Own/Train/Attend: Class A Misdemeanor.
    • (a)(1, 2, 3) - Cause Fight/Earnings/Facility/Property: State Jail Felony.
  • Defense (d): For (a)(1) Cause Fight: Actor caused fight solely to protect livestock/property/person from other dog.
  • Testimony (c): Conviction under (a)(2) or (3) can be had on uncorroborated party testimony.

Sec. 42.105. Cockfighting

  • Offenses (b): Knowingly: (1) Causes cock fight; (2) Participates in earnings; (3) Uses/permits property; (4) Owns/trains w/ intent; (5) Manufactures/buys/sells/possesses gaff/slasher w/ intent; (6) Attends as spectator.
  • Penalties (g):
    • (b)(1, 2) - Cause Fight / Earnings: State Jail Felony.
    • (b)(3, 4, 5) - Property / Own/Train / Gaffs: Class A Misdemeanor.
    • (b)(6) - Attend: Class C Misdemeanor (MA if prior under (b)(6)).
  • Affirmative Defense (c): Conduct solely for breeding for poultry shows OR incidental to collecting equipment (Not available if evidence shows cockfighting use (d)).
  • Defense (e): Bona fide scientific research OR accepted/lawful animal husbandry.
  • Exception (f): For (b)(6) Attend: Actor is 15 or younger.

Sec. 42.107. Possession of Animal by Person Convicted of Animal Cruelty

  • Offense (a): Possesses/exercises control over animal AND within preceding 5 yrs, convicted of 42.091 (Attack Assist Animal), 42.092 (Cruelty Nonlivestock), 42.10 (Dog Fighting), or similar federal/state law.
  • Penalties (b):
    • Base Offense: Class C Misdemeanor.
    • Enhanced Offense: Class B Misdemeanor if prior conviction under this section.

Sec. 42.08. Abuse of Corpse

  • Offenses (a): Without legal authority, knowingly: (1) Disinters, disturbs, damages, dissects, carries away, treats offensively corpse; (2) Conceals illegally disinterred corpse; (3) Sells/buys/traffics corpse; (4) Transmits corpse out of state; (5) Vandalizes/damages/treats offensively place of interment.
  • "Human Corpse" (c): Includes portion, cremated remains, or portion of cremated remains.
  • Penalties (b):
    • (a)(5) - Vandalize Interment Space: Class A Misdemeanor.
    • All others: State Jail Felony.
  • Defense (e): Actor was member/agent of cemetery org removing/damaging item placed in violation of rules OR item that became wrecked/unsightly/dilapidated.

Sec. 42.11. Destruction of Flag

  • Offense (a): I/K damages, defaces, mutilates, or burns flag of US or Texas.
  • "Flag" Definition (b): Official/recognized emblem/banner capable of being flown. Excludes representations on documents, photos, clothing, jewelry, etc.
  • Exception (c): Act done in conformity w/ statutes regarding proper disposal of damaged flags.
  • Penalty (d): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 42.12. Discharge of Firearm in Certain Municipalities

  • Offense (a): Recklessly discharges firearm inside corporate limits of municipality having population 100,000 or more.
  • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Note (d): Does not affect municipality's authority to enact stricter ordinance.

Sec. 42.13. Use of Laser Pointers

  • Offense (a): Knowingly directs light from laser pointer at uniformed safety officer (peace officer, security guard, firefighter, EMS, etc.).
  • Penalties (c):
    • Base Offense: Class C Misdemeanor.
    • If causes BI: Felony of the Third Degree.
    • If causes SBI: Felony of the First Degree.

Sec. 42.14. Illumination of Aircraft by Intense Light

  • Offense (a): Intentionally directs light from laser pointer/other source at aircraft AND light has intensity sufficient to impair operator's ability to control aircraft.
  • Penalties (c):
    • Base Offense: Class C Misdemeanor.
    • If intensity impairs operator's ability to control: Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Affirmative Defense (b): Actor was using light to send emergency distress signal.

Sec. 42.15. Operation of Unmanned Aircraft Over Airport, Military Installation, or Spaceport

  • Offense (b): I/K: (1) Operates unmanned aircraft over airport/military installation/spaceport; (2) Allows contact w/ facility/person/object; OR (3) Operates interfering w/ or causing disturbance to facility.
  • Penalties (d):
    • Base Offense: Class B Misdemeanor.
    • Enhanced Offense: Class A Misdemeanor if prior conviction under this section.
  • Defense (c): Conduct by gov entity, person under contract w/ gov, LE, person under contract w/ LE, owner/operator, person under contract w/ owner/operator, person w/ written authorization (owner/operator or FAA), or owner/occupant of property w/ consent.
