Ch. 43: Public Indecency

Back to Main Page

Subchapter A: Key Definitions (Sec. 43.01)

Access Software Provider

Provider of software or tools that filter, screen, transmit, receive, search, or otherwise manage content.

Deviate Sexual Intercourse

Any contact between the genitals of one person and the mouth or anus of another person.

Fee

Payment or offer of payment in the form of money, goods, services, or other benefit.

Information Content Provider

Any person or entity wholly or partly responsible for the creation or development of information provided through an interactive computer service.

Interactive Computer Service

Any service, system, or provider that enables computer access by multiple users to a server (e.g., provides Internet access).

Internet

The international computer network of federal and nonfederal interoperable packet switched data networks.

Premises

Has the meaning assigned by Sec. 481.134, Health and Safety Code.

Prostitution

The offense defined in Sec. 43.02 (offering/agreeing to *receive* a fee for sexual conduct).

School

A public or private primary or secondary school.

Sexual Contact

Any touching of the anus, breast, or any part of the genitals of another person with intent to arouse or gratify the sexual desire of any person.

Sexual Conduct

Includes deviate sexual intercourse, sexual contact, and sexual intercourse.

Sexual Intercourse

Any penetration of the female sex organ by the male sex organ.

Solicitation of Prostitution

The offense defined in Sec. 43.021 (offering/agreeing to *pay* a fee for sexual conduct).

Sec. 43.02. Prostitution

  • Offense (a): Person knowingly offers or agrees to receive a fee from another to engage in sexual conduct.
  • Penalties (c):
    • Base Offense: Class B Misdemeanor
    • 1-2 Previous Convictions: Class A Misdemeanor
    • 3+ Previous Convictions: State Jail Felony
  • Defense (d): It is a defense if actor was a victim of Trafficking (20A.02) or Compelling Prostitution (43.05).
  • Enhancement Note (e): Deferred adjudication counts as a previous conviction for enhancement.

Sec. 43.021. Solicitation of Prostitution

  • Offense (a): Person knowingly offers or agrees to pay a fee to another for sexual conduct.
  • Penalties (b):
    • Base Offense: State Jail Felony
    • 1+ Previous Conviction: Felony of the Third Degree
    • Person solicited is < 18 (or represented/believed to be): Felony of the Second Degree
  • Enhancement (b-1): Punishment increased to next highest category if offense occurs on or within 1,000 feet of school premises or school/UIL event.
  • Enhancement Note (c): Deferred adjudication counts as a previous conviction for enhancement.

Sec. 43.03. Promotion of Prostitution

  • Offense (a): Person (acting *other than* as prostitute receiving own fees) knowingly: (1) Receives money/property per agreement to participate in prostitution proceeds; OR (2) Solicits another to engage in sexual conduct with another for compensation.
  • Penalties (b):
    • Base Offense: Felony of the Third Degree
    • 1+ Previous Conviction: Felony of the Second Degree
    • Involves person < 18: Felony of the First Degree (knowledge of age not required)

Sec. 43.031. Online Promotion of Prostitution

  • Offense (a): Person owns, manages, or operates an Interactive Computer Service (ICS) or Information Content Provider (ICP) with intent to promote prostitution.
  • Penalties (b):
    • Base Offense: Felony of the Third Degree
    • 1+ Previous Conviction (this or 43.041) OR Involves person < 18: Felony of the Second Degree

Sec. 43.04. Aggravated Promotion of Prostitution

  • Offense (a): Person knowingly owns, invests in, finances, controls, supervises, or manages a prostitution enterprise that uses two or more prostitutes.
  • Penalty (b): Felony of the First Degree.

Sec. 43.041. Aggravated Online Promotion of Prostitution

  • Offense (a): Person (as ICS/ICP operator) with intent to promote prostitution of five or more persons.
  • Penalties (b):
    • Base Offense: Felony of the Second Degree
    • 1+ Previous Conviction OR Involves two or more persons < 18: Felony of the First Degree

Sec. 43.05. Compelling Prostitution

  • Offense (a): Person knowingly: (1) Causes another by force, threat, coercion, or fraud to commit prostitution; (2) Causes by any means a child < 18 to commit prostitution; OR (3) Causes by any means a disabled individual to commit prostitution.
  • Penalty (b): Felony of the First Degree.
  • "Coercion" Definition (d): Includes destroying/withholding government records/ID, causing intoxication, or withholding alcohol/drugs from a chemically dependent person.
  • No Defense (e): Not a defense that the child or disabled individual lacked culpable mental state or did not complete the act.

Sec. 43.06. Accomplice Witness; Testimony and Immunity

  • (a) A party to an offense under this subchapter may be required to testify.
  • (b) A party required to testify may not be prosecuted for that offense (gains immunity).
  • (d) A conviction can be had on the uncorroborated testimony of a party to the offense.

Sec. 43.032. Continuous Promotion of Prostitution

  • Offense (a): Commits offense under 43.03 (Promotion) two or more times during a period of 30 or more days.
  • Jury Note (b): Jury does not need to be unanimous on specific dates/acts, only that 2+ acts occurred in a 30+ day period.
  • Penalty (e): Felony of the First Degree.

Subchapter B: Key Definitions (Sec. 43.21)

Obscene

The three-prong test: (A) Average person, applying contemporary community standards, finds it appeals to prurient interest; (B) Depicts/describes patently offensive sexual acts (ultimate acts, masturbation, lewd exhibition, etc.); AND (C) Lacks serious literary, artistic, political, and scientific value.

Material

Anything tangible capable of arousing interest (reading, observation, sound). *Does not* include an actual 3D obscene device.

Performance

A play, motion picture, dance, or other exhibition performed before an audience.

Patently Offensive

So offensive on its face as to affront current community standards of decency.

Promote

To manufacture, issue, sell, give, provide, lend, mail, deliver, transfer, transmit, publish, distribute, circulate, disseminate, present, exhibit, or advertise, or offer/agree to do same.

Wholesale Promote

To promote for the purpose of *resale*.

Obscene Device

A device (dildo, artificial vagina) designed or marketed as useful *primarily* for stimulation of human genital organs.

Sec. 43.22. Obscene Display or Distribution

  • Offense (a): Person intentionally or knowingly displays/distributes obscene material AND is reckless about whether a person is present who will be offended or alarmed.
  • Penalty (b): Class C Misdemeanor.

Sec. 43.23. Obscenity

  • Offense (a): Knowing its content/character, wholesale promotes or possesses w/ intent to wholesale promote obscene material/device.
    • Penalty (b): State Jail Felony.
  • Offense (c): Knowing its content/character: (1) Promotes or possesses w/ intent to promote obscene material/device; OR (2) Produces, presents, or directs an obscene performance.
    • Penalty (d): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Presumption (e): Person promoting in course of business is presumed to know content/character.
  • Presumption (f): Person possessing six or more obscene devices is presumed to possess with intent to promote.
  • Affirmative Defense (g): For bona fide medical, psychiatric, judicial, legislative, or law enforcement purpose.
  • Enhancement (h): Offense under (a) or (c) becomes Felony of the Second Degree if material visually depicts a child < 18 (or image indistinguishable from child).

Sec. 43.28. Certain Sexually Oriented Performances Prohibited

  • "Sexually Oriented Performance" (a)(2): Visual performance featuring a nude performer OR performer engaging in sexual conduct, that appeals to prurient interest in sex.
  • "Sexual Conduct" (a)(1): Includes simulated sex acts, lewd exhibition of genitals, contact, or sexual gesticulations w/ prosthetics.
  • Offense (b): Person engages in sexually oriented performance: (1) On public property where it could be viewed by a child; OR (2) In the presence of an individual < 18.
  • Penalty (c): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 43.231. Promotion or Possession of Child-like Sex Doll

  • "Child-like sex doll": An obscene, anatomically correct doll, mannequin, or robot with features of a child, intended for sexual stimulation.
  • Offenses (b): Person knowingly: (1) Promotes a doll; (2) Possesses w/ intent to promote a doll; OR (3) Possesses a doll.
  • Penalties (c, d, e):
    • (1) Promote: Felony of the Second Degree
    • (2) Possess w/ Intent: Felony of the Third Degree
    • (3) Possess: State Jail Felony
  • Presumption (f): Possessing two or more dolls is presumed intent to promote.
  • Affirmative Defense (g): Bona fide law enforcement purpose.

Sec. 43.235. Possession, Promotion, or Production of Certain Visual Material Appearing to Depict Child

  • Offense (b)(1): Knowingly possesses, accesses, or promotes obscene visual material depicting what *appears* to be a child < 18 (e.g., cartoon, AI).
  • Offense (b)(2): Uses image of actual child < 18 to *train* an AI model to produce child pornography.
  • Penalties (c):
    • Base Offense: State Jail Felony
    • 1 Prior Conviction (this, 43.23, 43.26, etc.): Felony of the Third Degree
    • 2+ Prior Convictions: Felony of the Second Degree

Sec. 43.24. Sale, Distribution, or Display of Harmful Material to Minor

  • "Minor": Individual < 18.
  • "Harmful Material": Material (A) appeals to prurient interest of *minor*; (B) is patently offensive to adult standards re what's suitable for *minors*; AND (C) is utterly without redeeming social value for *minors*.
  • Offense (b): Knowing material is harmful: (1) Knowing person is minor, sells/distributes/exhibits to minor; (2) Displays harmful material & is reckless if minor present; OR (3) Hires/employs/uses minor to assist in acts.
  • Defense (c-1): Actor was spouse of minor.
  • Affirmative Defense (c-2): Actor was judicial or LE officer on duty.
  • Penalties (d):
    • (b)(1) or (b)(2): Class A Misdemeanor
    • (b)(3) (Hiring Minor): Felony of the Third Degree

Sec. 43.25. Sexual Performance by a Child

  • "Sexual Performance": Performance including sexual conduct by child < 18.
  • "Sexual Conduct": Includes sexual contact, actual/simulated intercourse, lewd exhibition of genitals/anus/female breast below areola, etc.
  • Offense (b): Knowing character/content, employs, authorizes, or induces child < 18 to engage in sexual conduct/performance. Also offense for parent/guardian to consent.
    • Penalty (c): Felony of the Second Degree (F1 if victim < 14).
  • Offense (d): Knowing character/content, produces, directs, or promotes performance including sexual conduct by child < 18.
    • Penalty (e): Felony of the Third Degree (F2 if victim < 14).
  • Affirmative Defense (f): Actor was spouse, LEO on duty, or not more than 2 years older than child.

Sec. 43.251. Employment Harmful to Children

  • "Child": Person < 21.
  • Offense (b): Person employs, authorizes, or induces a child (< 21) to work: (1) In a sexually oriented commercial activity; OR (2) In any place of business requiring child to work nude or topless.
  • Penalties (c): Felony of the Second Degree (F1 if child < 14).

Sec. 43.26. Possession or Promotion of Child Pornography

  • "Depiction of a child": Image of actual child < 18 OR recognizable image of actual child < 18 used to create material (e.g., AI).
  • "Depiction of a computer-generated child": Image appearing to be < 18, created by AI/software, and virtually indistinguishable from actual child.
  • Offense (a-1): Knowingly possess/access w/ intent visual material of actual child (< 18) engaging in sexual conduct.
    • Base Penalty (c-1): Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Offense (a-2): Knowingly possess/access w/ intent visual material of computer-generated child engaging in sexual conduct.
    • Base Penalty (c-3): State Jail Felony.
  • Offense (e): Knowingly promote (or possess w/ intent) material of actual child.
    • Base Penalty (g, S.B. 1621): Felony of the Second Degree.
  • Offense (e-1): Knowingly promote (or possess w/ intent) material of computer-generated child.
    • Base Penalty (g-1): Felony of the Third Degree.
  • Enhancements (c-1/c-3): Penalties increase for prior convictions or quantity (10-49 images, 50+ images).
  • Presumption (f): Rebuttable presumption that depiction is of an actual child, not computer-generated.
  • Affirmative Defense (h-2): For computer-generated (a-2, e-1) offenses, actor not more than 2 years older than depicted child.

Sec. 43.261. Electronic Transmission of Certain Visual Material Depicting Minor

  • Offense (b): A minor (< 18) intentionally or knowingly: (1) By electronic means promotes to another minor visual material depicting minor in sexual conduct (if actor or another minor produced it); OR (2) Possesses in electronic format material depicting another minor in sexual conduct (if actor or another minor produced it).
  • Penalties (c, d):
    • Base Offense: Class C Misdemeanor.
    • Enhancement (c)(1)(A): (Promotion) Class B Misdemeanor if done w/ intent to harass, annoy, alarm, etc.
    • Enhancement (c/d): Class B for 1 prior, Class A for 2+ priors.
  • Affirmative Defense (e): Depicted only actor/other minor in dating relationship (max 2 yr age diff) OR spouse, AND material only promoted between them.
  • Defense (f): (Possession) Actor didn't produce/solicit, received from minor, AND destroyed w/in reasonable time.

Sec. 43.262. Possession or Promotion of Lewd Visual Material Depicting Child

  • Offense (b): Knowingly possesses, accesses, or promotes visual material depicting lewd exhibition of genitals/pubic area of child < 18, that appeals to prurient interest, AND has no serious value.
  • Penalties (c):
    • Base Offense: State Jail Felony
    • 1 Previous Conviction: Felony of the Third Degree
    • 2+ Previous Convictions: Felony of the Second Degree
  • No Defense (d): Not a defense that the depicted child consented.

Sec. 43.27. Duty to Report

  • (b) A business that develops or processes visual material and determines it may be evidence of a crime under this subchapter shall report its existence to local law enforcement.
