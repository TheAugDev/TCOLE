Ch. 47: Gambling

Back to Main Page

Key Definitions (Sec. 47.01)

Bet

Agreement to win/lose something of value solely/partially by chance. Excludes insurance, prizes in bona fide skill contests, certain small carnival merchandise prizes ($25 max).

Bookmaking

(A) Receive/record/forward > 5 bets in 24 hrs; (B) Receive/record/forward bets > $1,000 in 24 hrs; OR (C) Scheme by 3+ persons to receive/record/forward bets.

Gambling Place

Any property (real estate, room, vehicle, boat, etc.) one use of which is making/settling bets, bookmaking, conducting lottery, or playing gambling devices.

Gambling Device

Electronic/electromechanical/mechanical contrivance that for consideration affords chance to obtain anything of value, determined solely/partially by chance (even if skill involved). Includes versions of bingo, keno, blackjack, etc. Excludes bona fide amusement devices rewarding only noncash prizes (toys/novelties) with wholesale value <= 10x play cost or $5 (whichever less).

Altered Gambling Equipment

Contrivance altered to enhance winning chances (e.g., shaved/loaded dice, marked cards, electronic sensors).

Gambling Paraphernalia

Book, instrument, apparatus for recording/registering bets; record, ticket, token, scratch sheet, etc., for carrying on bookmaking, lotteries, numbers games, etc.

Lottery

Scheme where prize(s) distributed by chance among persons who paid/promised consideration for chance to win anything of value (regardless of name: pool, raffle, gift enterprise, etc.).

Private Place

Place public does not have access. Excludes streets, highways, restaurants, taverns, schools, hospitals, common areas of apartments/hotels/offices, transport facilities, shops.

Thing of Value

Any benefit. Excludes unrecorded/immediate right of replay not exchangeable for value.

Sec. 47.02. Gambling

  • Offense (a): Person commits offense if they:
    1. Make bet on partial/final result of game/contest or participant performance.
    2. Make bet on result of political nomination/appointment/election or candidate success.
    3. Play and bet money/thing of value at game played with cards, dice, balls, or other gambling device.
  • Penalty (d): Class C Misdemeanor.
  • Defense (b) - Private Place Defense: Actor engaged in gambling in private place; AND no person received economic benefit other than personal winnings; AND risks/chances were same for all (except skill/luck).
  • Defense (c) - Statutory Exceptions: Actor reasonably believed conduct permitted under laws governing: Bingo (Occ Code 2001), Charitable Raffles (Occ Code 2002), Promotional Activities (Occ Code 2004), State Lottery (Gov Code 466), Horse/Greyhound Racing (Occ Code Title 13, Subtitle A-1), or PWD recreational event drawings.
  • Defense (e) - Amusement Machine Defense: Person played for something of value (other than money) using device excluded under 47.01(4)(B) (bona fide amusement device awarding limited noncash prizes).

Sec. 47.03. Gambling Promotion

  • Offense (a): Person I/K does any of the following:
    1. Operates or participates in earnings of a gambling place.
    2. Engages in bookmaking.
    3. For gain, becomes custodian of anything of value bet or offered to be bet.
    4. Sells chances on result/margin of game/contest/performance or political event.
    5. For gain, sets up/promotes lottery or sells/offers/possesses for transfer/transfers lottery evidence (ticket, etc.).
  • Penalty (b): Class A Misdemeanor.

Sec. 47.04. Keeping a Gambling Place

  • Offense (a): Person knowingly uses or permits another to use property owned/controlled by them as gambling place; OR rents/lets property expecting it to be so used.
  • Penalty (c): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Affirmative Defense (b): Gambling occurred in private place; AND no person received economic benefit other than personal winnings; AND risks/chances same for all (except skill/luck). (Same elements as 47.02(b) defense).

Sec. 47.05. Communicating Gambling Information

  • Offense (a): With intent to further gambling, knowingly communicates info on bets/odds/changes OR knowingly provides/installs/maintains equipment for transmission/receipt of such info.
  • Penalty (c): Class A Misdemeanor.
  • Exception (b): Info intended for lawful wager under Occ Code Ch 2027 (Fantasy Sports?) and not communicated violating Occ Code 2033.013.

Sec. 47.06. Possession of Gambling Device, Equipment, or Paraphernalia

  • Offense (a): W/ intent to further gambling, knowingly owns, manufactures, transfers, or possesses gambling device known designed for gambling OR equipment known as subassembly/essential part.
  • Offense (b): W/ intent to further gambling, knowingly owns, manufactures, transfers commercially, or possesses altered gambling equipment known designed for gambling OR equipment known as subassembly/essential part.
  • Offense (c): W/ intent to further gambling, knowingly owns, manufactures, transfers commercially, or possesses gambling paraphernalia.
  • Penalty (e): Class A Misdemeanor (for all subsections).
  • Defense (d) - Private Place: For (a) [Device/Equip] & (c) [Paraphernalia]: Used/intended for gambling entirely in private place; AND no economic benefit other than winnings; AND risks/chances same for all (except skill/luck).
  • Defense (f) - Shipping: For (a) & (c): Owned/manufactured/transferred/possessed *solely* for shipping to jurisdiction where legal.
  • Inspection (g): DA/CA not required to have warrant/subpoena to inspect gambling items on ocean-going vessel calling at TX port.

Sec. 47.09. Other Defenses

  • Defense (a) - Authorized Conduct: It is a defense that conduct was authorized under laws governing:
    • Bingo (Occ Code 2001)
    • Charitable Raffles (Occ Code 2002)
    • Promotional Activities (Occ Code 2004)
    • Horse/Greyhound Racing (Occ Code Title 13, Subtitle A-1)
    • Savings Promotion Raffles (Finance Code 280)
    • Or consisted entirely of participation in State Lottery (Gov Code 466)
    • Or was necessary incident to State Lottery operation authorized by law/TDLR/Exec Director.
  • Affirmative Defense (b) - Ocean Vessel: For 47.04 (Keeping Place), 47.06(a) (Poss Device), 47.06(c) (Poss Paraph.), device/equip/paraph is aboard ocean vessel calling at TX port IF:
    1. DA/CA receives prior notice of items and dates in territorial waters.
    2. Items are disabled remotely/securely while in TX waters.
    3. Disabling device only removed for inspection/repair.
    4. Items not used for gambling while in TX waters.

Sec. 47.10. American Documentation of Vessel Required

If federal law 18 USC 1082 is repealed, the ocean vessel affirmative defense (47.09b) applies only if the vessel is documented under US law.

Sec. 47.11. Deposits in Certain Accounts Not Consideration

Opening/depositing in savings account/program subject to Savings Promotion Raffle (Finance Code 280) does NOT constitute "consideration" for gambling purposes.

Sec. 47.07. Evidence

Published report of sporting event in daily newspaper/magazine/periodical is admissible & prima facie evidence the event occurred.

Sec. 47.08. Testimonial Immunity

  • (a) Party to Ch 47 offense may be required to furnish evidence/testify.
  • (b) If required, party may not be prosecuted for offense testified about (except Aggravated Perjury), and testimony can't be used against them.
  • (d) Conviction under Ch 47 may be had on uncorroborated testimony of party to offense.
