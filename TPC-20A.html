Ch. 20A: Trafficking of Persons

Key Definitions (Ch. 20A)

Child (20A.01(1))

A person younger than 18 years of age.

Coercion (20A.01(1-a))

Includes (A) destroying/withholding documents, (B) causing intoxication without consent, or (C) withholding alcohol/drugs from a dependent person to impair their ability to resist.

Disabled individual (20A.01(1-b))

Has the meaning assigned by Section 22.021(b) (Aggravated Sexual Assault).

Forced labor or services (20A.01(2))

Labor or services (other than sexual conduct) performed by another and obtained through the actor's use of force, fraud, or coercion.

Premises (20A.01(2-a))

Has the meaning assigned by Section 481.134, Health and Safety Code (related to drug-free zones).

School (20A.01(2-b))

A public or private primary or secondary school.

Sexual conduct (20A.01(3))

Has the meaning assigned by Section 43.25 (Sexual Performance by a Child).

Traffic (20A.01(4))

To transport, entice, recruit, harbor, provide, or otherwise obtain another person by any means.

Sec. 20A.02. Trafficking of Persons

Note: Penalties reflect amendments effective Sept 1, 2025, as provided in the source text.

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if they knowingly:
    1. Traffics another with intent they engage in forced labor/services.
    2. Receives benefit from a venture involving (1).
    3. Traffics another and uses force, fraud, or coercion to cause them to engage in Prostitution (43.02), Promotion (43.03/43.031), Agg. Promotion (43.04/43.041), or Compelling Prostitution (43.05).
    4. Receives benefit from a venture involving (3) or engages in sexual conduct with a person trafficked under (3).
    5. Traffics a child or disabled individual with intent they engage in forced labor/services (culpability regarding age/disability not required).
    6. Receives benefit from a venture involving (5) (culpability regarding age/disability not required).
    7. Traffics a child or disabled individual and causes them to engage in or become victim of listed sexual offenses (e.g., Continuous Sexual Abuse, Indecency, Sexual Assault, Agg. SA, Prostitution, Promotion, Compelling Prostitution, Sexual Performance, Employ Harmful, Child Porn) (culpability regarding age/disability not required).
    8. Receives benefit from a venture involving (7) or engages in sexual conduct with a child/disabled individual trafficked under (7) (culpability regarding age/disability not required).
  • Base Penalty (b - per SB 955, SB 1212, HB 1778 eff 9/1/25): Felony of the first degree. (Note: SB 955 also lists F2 base with F1 enhancements, presented below for context).
  • Enhancements to F1 (per SB 955 eff 9/1/25): Becomes F1 if:
    • Offense involves a child/disabled individual (a)(5)-(8).
    • Results in SBI or Death (of victim or unborn child).
    • Actor used/exhibited deadly weapon, impeded breathing/circulation, or recruited from specific shelters/facilities/correctional facilities.
  • Enhanced Penalty Range (b-1 - per SB 955, SB 1212, HB 1778 eff 9/1/25): Becomes an F1 punishable by life or 25-99 years if committed on or within 1000 ft of:
    • School, Higher Ed Institution, Juvenile Facility, Secure Correctional Facility, Shelter (runaway, foster, homeless, trafficking, DV, SA), Youth Community Center, Child-Care Facility, Correctional Facility (SB 955 only adds correctional facility).
    • Official school function or UIL event.
    • (SB 1212 / HB 1778 add: School bus stop, pick-up/drop-off zone, or school bus/transportation vehicle).
  • Overlap (c): Can be prosecuted under this section and another section if conduct fits both.
  • Limitation (d): Cannot convict under (a)(7)(A) (causing victim Continuous Sexual Abuse) and Sec. 21.02 (Continuous Sexual Abuse) in the same action unless charged in alternative, occurred outside the period, or lesser included.
  • No Defense (e): For prosecution under (a)(7) or (8) involving prostitution, it is NOT a defense that the child/disabled individual lacked culpable mental state or did not complete the act.

Sec. 20A.03. Continuous Trafficking of Persons

  • Offense (a): A person commits an offense if, during a period of 30 or more days, the person engages two or more times in conduct constituting an offense under Sec. 20A.02 against one or more victims.
  • Jury Unanimity (b): Jury does *not* need to agree on specific conduct or exact dates, only that the defendant engaged twice or more during the 30+ day period.
  • Conviction Limitation (c): Similar to 20A.02(d), cannot convict under this section AND 20A.02 in the same action unless charged in alternative, occurred outside the period, or lesser included.
  • Charging Limitation (d): Cannot charge more than *one count* under (a) if all conduct is against the same victim.
  • Penalty (e): Felony of the first degree, punishable by life or 25-99 years.
  • No Defense (f): Similar to 20A.02(e), for prosecution based on causing prostitution, it's NOT a defense that the child/disabled victim lacked mental state or didn't complete the act.

Sec. 20A.04. Accomplice Witness; Testimony and Immunity

  • Compelled Testimony (a): A party to a Ch. 20A offense may be required to testify.
  • Immunity (b): A party required to testify *may not* be prosecuted for any offense they testify about (except Aggravated Perjury), and the testimony cannot be used against them.
  • Uncorroborated Testimony (c): A conviction under Ch. 20A *may* be had on the uncorroborated testimony of a party to the offense.

Sec. 20A.05. Prosecution by Attorney General

  • Jurisdiction: The Attorney General has jurisdiction to prosecute offenses under this chapter.
